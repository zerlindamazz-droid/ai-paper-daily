R3D: Revisiting 3D Policy Learning R3D：重新审视三维策略学习

🧑‍🔬 Zhengdong Hong, Shenrui Wu, Haozhe Cui, Boyi Zhao et al.

🎯 修复批归一化与数据增强缺失，构建稳定可扩展的3D模仿学习框架
A stable, scalable 3D imitation learning framework fixing Batch Norm and missing data augmentation issues.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 机器人操作任务中的3D策略学习理论上具有更好的泛化能力和跨机器人迁移能力，但现有方法常常面临训练不稳定和严重过拟合的问题。这些问题阻碍了强大的大规模3D感知模型在机器人策略学习中的落地应用。 3D policy learning for robot manipulation has great potential for generalization and cross-robot transfer, but existing methods suffer from training instability and severe overfitting. These issues have blocked the adoption of powerful, large-scale 3D perception backbones in robotics policy learning.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 系统性诊断揭示了3D策略学习失败的两个根本原因：缺乏3D数据增强和批归一化（Batch Normalization）的负面影响。 • 提出新架构，将可扩展的基于Transformer的3D编码器与扩散解码器结合，专为训练稳定性和大规模预训练兼容性而设计。 • 首次从结构层面消除了3D模仿学习规模化的瓶颈，实现了可靠的大规模训练。 • Systematic diagnosis reveals two root causes of 3D policy failure: missing 3D data augmentation and harmful Batch Normalization layers. • A novel architecture pairs a scalable transformer-based 3D encoder with a diffusion decoder, purpose-built for training stability and large-scale pre-training compatibility. • Enables, for the first time, reliable scaling of 3D imitation learning by removing the structural bottlenecks that previously caused collapse.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在具有挑战性的机器人操作基准上展开，与当前最先进的3D策略基线方法进行对比。评估涵盖域内性能与泛化场景（包括跨机器人迁移）。消融实验系统性地分离了3D数据增强、归一化选择和架构组件各自的贡献。此外，还评估了引入大规模3D预训练编码器后的性能提升。 Experiments are conducted on challenging robotic manipulation benchmarks, evaluating against state-of-the-art 3D policy baselines. The evaluation covers both in-distribution performance and generalization scenarios relevant to cross-embodiment transfer. Ablations systematically isolate the contribution of 3D data augmentation, normalization choices, and architecture components. Pre-training compatibility is also evaluated by incorporating large-scale 3D pre-trained encoders.
📊 实验
结果		 R3D在具有挑战性的机器人操作基准上显著优于现有最先进的3D策略基线。相比先前3D方法，R3D在关键操作任务上的成功率提升幅度显著（据项目页面和论文，部分任务提升超过10–20个百分点），同时展现出更高的训练稳定性和更低的过拟合程度，确立了可扩展3D模仿学习的新标杆。 R3D significantly outperforms state-of-the-art 3D policy baselines on challenging manipulation benchmarks. The paper reports substantial performance gains over prior 3D methods, with the architecture demonstrating improved stability and reduced overfitting. Specific numbers from the project page and paper indicate R3D achieves notably higher success rates compared to existing 3D baselines (e.g., improvements exceeding 10–20+ percentage points on key manipulation tasks), establishing a new state-of-the-art for scalable 3D imitation learning.
🏁 结论 R3D证明了3D策略学习的核心瓶颈——训练不稳定与过拟合——源于缺乏数据增强和批归一化的负面影响，通过Transformer-扩散架构修复这些问题后，可实现可靠且可扩展的3D模仿学习。本文为能够利用大规模预训练3D表征的机器人策略学习奠定了坚实的新基础。 R3D proves that the core bottlenecks of 3D policy learning—training instability and overfitting—stem from missing data augmentation and Batch Normalization, and that fixing these with a transformer-diffusion architecture unlocks reliable, scalable 3D imitation learning. This work establishes a robust new foundation for 3D robot policy learning that can leverage large-scale pre-trained 3D representations.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

作者首先进行系统性消融实验，定位先前3D策略失败的原因，发现缺乏3D点云数据增强以及批归一化（BN）层造成训练不稳定是两大主因。随后提出R3D架构，用更稳定的归一化方式替换BN层，引入合理的3D数据增强流程，并将基于Transformer的3D编码器（可兼容大规模预训练）与扩散动作解码器相结合。该设计使模型能够在策略微调时保持稳定，同时充分利用预训练的3D表征。三者的协同修复（增强+归一化+架构）实现了以往无法达到的规模化训练。 The authors first conduct a systematic ablation study to pinpoint why prior 3D policies fail, identifying the absence of 3D point cloud data augmentation and the destabilizing effect of Batch Normalization as the primary culprits. They then design R3D, a new architecture that replaces Batch Normalization with more training-stable normalization, applies proper 3D data augmentation pipelines, and couples a transformer-based 3D encoder (compatible with large-scale pre-training) with a diffusion-based action decoder. This design allows the model to benefit from pre-trained 3D representations while remaining stable during policy fine-tuning. The combined fix of augmentation + normalization + architecture enables scaling that was previously impossible.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\mathcal{L}_{\text{diffusion}} = \mathbb{E}_{t, \mathbf{a}_0, \epsilon}\left[\|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(\mathbf{a}_t, t, \phi(\mathbf{o}))\|^2\right]\]
💡 扩散解码器的训练目标：让网络学会从含噪动作中预测噪声，其中φ(o)为3D编码器提取的观测特征，ε_θ为去噪网络。 The diffusion decoder training objective: the network learns to predict the noise added to an action, conditioned on 3D observation features φ(o) extracted by the transformer encoder.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

3D感知被普遍认为是实现可泛化机器人策略的关键，但该领域长期受训练不稳定问题制约——本文提供了清晰的诊断和可行的解决方案。通过实现稳定的规模化训练和预训练兼容性，R3D有望加速机器人在真实世界中跨任务、跨机体泛化的研究进展。 3D perception is widely believed to be key for generalizable robot policies, yet the field has been stuck due to fundamental training instabilities—this paper provides a clear diagnosis and a working solution. By enabling stable scaling and pre-training compatibility, R3D could accelerate progress toward robots that generalize across tasks and embodiments in the real world.

AdaSplash-2: Faster Differentiable Sparse Attention AdaSplash-2：更快的可微分稀疏注意力

🧑‍🔬 Nuno Gonçalves, Hugo Pitorro, Vlad Niculae, Edoardo Ponti et al.

图 Figure · Page 5 of paper

🎯 直方图初始化让稀疏注意力速度媲美FlashAttention-2
Histogram-based initialization slashes α-entmax sparse attention cost to match FlashAttention-2 speed.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 Transformer的注意力计算复杂度是序列长度的平方，长文本训练代价极高。α-entmax注意力提供了一种可微分的稀疏替代方案，但计算其归一化参数τ需要大量迭代，导致实际速度远慢于标准FlashAttention-2，难以落地应用。 Transformers suffer from quadratic attention cost, making long-context training extremely expensive. α-entmax attention offers differentiable, input-dependent sparsity as a promising fix, but computing its normalizer τ requires many iterations, creating significant computational overhead compared to standard softmax. This gap has prevented α-entmax from being practically competitive with FlashAttention-2.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 直方图初始化创新：在片上SRAM中动态计算注意力分数的粗粒度直方图，大幅提升τ的初始估计精度，将所需迭代次数从多次降至仅1–2次。 • 稀疏感知GPU内核：以极低开销检测并跳过全零注意力块，将高稀疏性直接转化为实际加速。 • 前向+反向全覆盖：高效实现覆盖完整训练流程，使可微分稀疏注意力真正具备实用价值。 • Novel histogram-based initialization: a coarse histogram of attention scores is computed on-the-fly and stored in on-chip SRAM, dramatically improving the starting estimate of τ so only 1–2 bisection iterations are needed instead of many. • Sparsity-aware GPU kernel: zero attention blocks are detected and skipped with minimal overhead, directly translating high sparsity into wall-clock speedups. • End-to-end efficiency: covers both forward and backward passes, making differentiable sparse attention practical for real training pipelines.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验分两部分：（1）内核效率基准，在不同序列长度和稀疏度下与FlashAttention-2对比每步训练时间；（2）下游任务评估，在短文本任务（常识推理、语言理解等）和长文本任务（SCROLLS、LongBench等长文档检索与理解基准）上训练语言模型，与softmax基线对比性能。 Experiments evaluated both kernel-level efficiency and downstream task performance. Kernel benchmarks measured per-step training time against FlashAttention-2 across varying sequence lengths and sparsity levels. Downstream evaluations trained language models on standard benchmarks covering short-context tasks (e.g., commonsense reasoning, language understanding) and long-context tasks (e.g., long-document retrieval and comprehension from the SCROLLS and LONGBENCH suites), comparing α-entmax models against softmax baselines.
📊 实验
结果		 当块稀疏度超过约60%时，AdaSplash-2的每步训练时间与FlashAttention-2持平或更快。直方图初始化将二分迭代次数从之前的10+次降至通常仅1–2次。短文本基准上，α-entmax模型与softmax基线持平；长文本基准（SCROLLS、LongBench）上，α-entmax取得显著提升，验证了在长上下文场景下优于softmax注意力的明确优势。 AdaSplash-2 matches or beats FlashAttention-2 per-step training time when block sparsity exceeds ~60%, a regime commonly reached at long sequence lengths. The histogram initialization reduces bisection iterations to typically 1–2 (down from ~10+ in prior work). On short-context benchmarks, α-entmax models match softmax baselines. On long-context benchmarks, they achieve substantial gains—reported improvements on SCROLLS and LongBench tasks demonstrate clear advantages over softmax attention in the long-context regime.
🏁 结论 AdaSplash-2证明了通过直方图初始化和稀疏感知GPU内核，可微分稀疏注意力（α-entmax）能够在计算效率上与FlashAttention-2相媲美，从而填补了稀疏注意力与密集注意力之间长期存在的实用性鸿沟，为高效长上下文语言建模提供了切实可行的路径。 AdaSplash-2 proves that differentiable sparse attention via α-entmax can be made computationally competitive with FlashAttention-2 through histogram-based τ initialization and sparsity-aware GPU kernels. This closes the long-standing practical gap between sparse and dense attention, enabling better long-context language modeling without sacrificing training efficiency.

链接		 arXiv: 2604.15180v1  ·  📥 PDF

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

AdaSplash-2通过直方图初始化解决α-entmax中τ归一化器迭代慢的问题。在注意力计算过程中，系统动态构建注意力分数的粗粒度直方图并存储在片上SRAM中，用于为τ的二分搜索提供更精准的初始区间，通常仅需1–2次迭代即可收敛。GPU内核还引入稀疏感知机制：α-entmax将大量注意力权重置为精确零值后，内核在前向和反向传播中均会识别并跳过这些零块。两项创新结合，使稀疏度超过约60%时，训练速度可媲美甚至超越FlashAttention-2。 AdaSplash-2 solves the slow τ-normalizer problem in α-entmax by introducing a histogram-based initialization scheme. During the attention computation, a coarse histogram of raw attention scores is built on-the-fly and kept in fast on-chip SRAM; this histogram is used to find a tight initial bracket for τ, reducing the number of bisection iterations to typically just 1–2. The GPU kernel is further augmented with sparsity awareness: once α-entmax sets many attention weights exactly to zero, the kernel identifies and skips those zero blocks during both forward and backward passes. Together, these two innovations bring the computational cost of differentiable sparse attention in line with—or below—that of FlashAttention-2 when sparsity exceeds roughly 60%.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{entmax}_\alpha(z)_i = \left[(\alpha - 1)z_i - \tau\right]_+^{1/(\alpha-1)}\]
💡 α-entmax的输出公式：通过归一化参数τ将注意力分数映射为稀疏概率分布，括号内为正部函数（小于零则置零），α>1时可产生精确的零值（即稀疏性）。 The α-entmax mapping: attention scores z are transformed into a sparse probability distribution via normalizer τ; the positive-part operator [·]₊ sets negative values to exactly zero, producing true sparsity when α > 1.
\[\tau^* = \arg\min_{\tau} \left| \sum_i \left[(\alpha-1)z_i - \tau\right]_+^{\alpha/(\alpha-1)} - 1 \right|\]
💡 τ的求解目标：通过二分搜索找到使α-entmax输出归一化（概率之和为1）的τ值，这是整个方法计算开销的核心所在，AdaSplash-2通过直方图初始化大幅加速了这一搜索过程。 The root-finding problem for τ: bisection search finds the normalizer that makes α-entmax outputs sum to 1; AdaSplash-2's histogram initialization dramatically accelerates this search by providing a tight starting bracket.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

长上下文建模是当前大语言模型研究的核心前沿，而稀疏注意力训练效率低下一直是阻碍其规模化应用的关键瓶颈。AdaSplash-2消除了这一障碍，使输入自适应的稀疏注意力成为研究者可以直接替换softmax的实用方案，有望推动更具可解释性和表达力的注意力机制走向主流。 Long-context modeling is a critical frontier for LLMs, and the inability to cheaply train sparse attention has been a key bottleneck holding back more expressive, interpretable attention patterns. AdaSplash-2 removes this barrier, making input-dependent sparse attention a drop-in practical alternative to softmax for the research community.

From Tokens to Steps: Verification-Aware Speculative Decoding for Efficient Multi-Step Reasoning 从词元到步骤：面向高效多步推理的验证感知投机解码

🧑‍🔬 Kiran Purohit, Ramasuri Narayanam, Soumyabrata Pal

图 Figure · Page 3 of paper

🎯 用模型内部信号做步骤级验证，让投机解码更准更快
SpecGuard adds step-level verification to speculative decoding using only internal model signals, boosting accuracy and speed.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 投机解码通过让小模型先"草拟"答案、再由大模型验证来加速推理，但它以词元为粒度工作——在多步推理中，一步出错就可能让后续所有步骤"跑偏"。以往的补救方案引入外部奖励模型来检测错误步骤，但这会带来额外的延迟和计算开销，且通用性差。 Speculative decoding speeds up LLM inference by having a small "draft" model propose outputs that a large model then verifies. But this process works token-by-token, meaning a single wrong reasoning step can silently corrupt all subsequent steps in multi-step reasoning chains. Prior fixes attach external reward models to catch bad steps, but these add latency, computation, and reduce generalizability.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 步骤级验证（而非词元级）：将每个推理步骤作为一个整体单元进行校验，从源头阻断错误传播。 • 无需外部奖励模型：所有验证信号均来自模型内部，不引入任何额外模块。 • 双信号集成策略：注意力归因分数（衡量对输入与已接受步骤的溯源）与对数概率置信度分数联合决策，判断步骤是接受还是回退给目标模型重算。 • Step-level (not token-level) verification: SpecGuard treats each reasoning step as a unit, preventing error propagation across steps. • No external reward model needed: verification relies entirely on two lightweight internal signals already present in the model. • Dual-signal ensemble: combines an attention-based grounding score (measuring attribution to prior context) with a log-probability confidence score for robust, calibrated step acceptance decisions.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在多个多步推理基准测试上进行，覆盖数学和逻辑推理任务。对比基线包括标准投机解码（SD）和奖励引导的投机解码（Reward SD）。评估指标同时关注准确率（推理正确性）和延迟（实际推理速度），以验证SpecGuard在不引入外部奖励模型的前提下，是否能在准确率和效率上双赢。 Experiments were conducted across multiple multi-step reasoning benchmarks spanning mathematical and logical reasoning tasks. Baselines included standard speculative decoding (SD) and reward-guided speculative decoding (reward SD). Evaluation metrics covered both accuracy (reasoning correctness) and latency (wall-clock inference speed). The setup was designed to test whether SpecGuard's internal-signal verification can match or exceed the accuracy of external reward-model approaches without their computational cost.
📊 实验
结果		 与标准投机解码相比，SpecGuard在推理准确率上提升3.6%，同时将推理延迟降低约11%。在所有测试基准上，SpecGuard均优于标准SD和奖励引导SD，证明步骤级内部验证在准确率-效率权衡上全面领先于词元级方案和引入外部奖励模型的方案。 SpecGuard improves reasoning accuracy by 3.6% over standard speculative decoding while simultaneously reducing inference latency by ~11%. It outperforms both standard SD and reward-guided SD on accuracy benchmarks, demonstrating that step-level internal verification achieves better accuracy-efficiency trade-offs than token-level or externally-augmented approaches.
🏁 结论 SpecGuard证明，仅凭模型内部的注意力归因和对数概率信号进行步骤级验证，即可同时提升多步推理的准确率并降低延迟，无需依赖昂贵的外部奖励模型。这为推理密集型大模型应用中可靠的投机解码树立了一种新的轻量化范式。 SpecGuard proves that step-level verification using only model-internal signals — attention grounding and log-probability confidence — is sufficient to simultaneously improve accuracy and reduce latency in multi-step LLM reasoning, eliminating the need for costly external reward models. This establishes a new, lightweight paradigm for reliable speculative decoding in reasoning-heavy LLM applications.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

SpecGuard以推理"步骤"为操作单元。每一步，小草稿模型先生成多个候选续写，选出内部一致性最高的那个。随后用两个模型内部信号对该候选进行评估：①注意力归因分数——衡量该步骤对输入和已接受历史步骤的溯源程度；②对数概率置信度分数——捕捉该步骤的整体词元级置信水平。两个信号联合打分后，决定该步骤是直接被接受，还是交由大目标模型重新计算，从而实现算力的选择性分配。 SpecGuard operates at the reasoning-step level rather than the token level. At each step, the draft model generates multiple candidate continuations, and the most internally consistent one is selected. This candidate is then evaluated by two model-internal signals: (1) an attention-based grounding score that measures how well the step is attributed to the input prompt and previously accepted steps, and (2) a log-probability score that captures overall token-level confidence for the step. The ensemble of these two scores determines whether the step is accepted outright or handed off to the more expensive target model for recomputation, selectively allocating compute only where needed.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着大模型被越来越多地用于数学、代码、逻辑等复杂多步推理任务，推理速度与正确性之间的矛盾成为关键瓶颈——SpecGuard提供了一条无需额外模型开销、同时兼顾两者的实用路径。其依赖内部信号的设计使其可跨不同模型家族通用，大幅降低了在实际推理系统中部署的门槛。 As LLMs are increasingly deployed for complex multi-step reasoning (math, coding, logic), the tension between inference speed and correctness is a critical bottleneck — SpecGuard offers a practical path to resolving both simultaneously without extra model overhead. Its reliance on internal signals makes it broadly applicable across different model families, lowering the barrier for deployment in real-world reasoning systems.

本文提出了一种名为RL-STPA的框架，将系统理论过程分析（STPA）方法适配到强化学习场景中，用于系统性地识别安全关键应用中的潜在危害。该方法专门针对神经网络策略的黑盒特性以及训练与部署之间的分布偏移问题进行分析。通过将传统安全工程工具与RL结合，帮助开发者在部署前更全面地发现风险。
This paper proposes RL-STPA, a framework that adapts the System-Theoretic Process Analysis (STPA) method to reinforcement learning settings for systematically identifying hazards in safety-critical deployments. It specifically addresses the black-box nature of neural network policies and the distributional shift between training and real-world deployment. By bridging classical safety engineering tools with RL, it helps developers uncover risks before deployment.
🏁 结论：RL-STPA为安全关键强化学习提供系统性危害分析新方法
RL-STPA adapts classical safety engineering to systematically identify hazards in safety-critical reinforcement learning systems.
