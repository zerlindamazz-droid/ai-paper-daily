Tapered Language Models 锥形语言模型（Tapered Language Models）

🧑‍🔬 Reza Bayat, Ali Behrouz, Aaron Courville

图 Figure · Page 5 of paper

🎯 早层多参数、晚层少参数，免费提升语言模型性能
Give early layers more parameters and later layers fewer — it's a free performance boost for any language model.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy想象一所学校，从小学一年级到高中三年级，每个年级配备完全相同数量的老师。但其实大部分核心知识都在小学阶段打基础！"锥形语言模型"就像把更多老师分配给小学、少数老师留给高年级——总经费不变，但学生（模型）学得更好了。Think of a school where every grade — from 1st to 12th — gets the exact same number of teachers. But most of the real learning happens in the early grades! Tapered Language Models are like hiring more teachers for elementary school and fewer for senior year, without spending a single extra dollar — the students (the model) just end up smarter.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现代AI语言模型（无论是Transformer还是其他架构）都把参数均匀分配到每一层，就像流水线上每个工人工具箱都一样大。但越来越多的证据表明，前面的层承担了大部分"重活"，后面的层只是小修小补。这种"一刀切"的分配方式浪费了宝贵的参数资源。 Every modern AI language model — whether it's a Transformer, a recurrent model, or a memory-based one — splits its parameters equally across all its layers, like giving every worker on an assembly line the exact same toolbox. But research increasingly shows that early layers do the heavy lifting (learning core language patterns) while later layers just make small touch-ups. Giving every layer the same resources wastes capacity where it matters least.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 关键洞察：不同深度的层贡献不均等，早层做主要变换，晚层只是微调。 • 锥形设计：用余弦曲线将每层MLP（主要耗参数的模块）的宽度从前到后逐渐缩小。 • 跨架构通用：在Transformer、门控注意力、Hope-attention、Titans四种架构上均有效。 • 完全"免费"：总参数量和计算量不变，只是重新分配，性能自然提升。 • Simple but overlooked insight: not all layers are equal — early layers transform information more, late layers just refine it. • Tapered design: gradually shrink the size of the middle "thinking" part (MLP) of each layer from front to back, using a smooth cosine curve. • Works across ALL major architectures tested (Transformer, Gated Attention, Hope-attention, Titans) with zero extra cost. • The improvement is free — same total number of parameters, same compute, just redistributed smarter.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在三个模型规模（小、中、大）和四种架构（标准Transformer、门控注意力、Hope-attention、Titans）上进行。所有模型在标准语言建模数据集上训练，主要评估指标为困惑度（perplexity，越低越好），同时在多个下游NLP基准任务上测试性能。对照组是参数总量完全相同的均匀宽度基线模型。 The authors tested their tapered design at three different model sizes (small, medium, large) and across four different modern architectures: standard Transformer, Gated Attention, Hope-attention, and Titans (a memory-based model). They trained all models on standard language modeling datasets and measured perplexity (how well the model predicts text — lower is better) as the main metric, plus performance on a suite of downstream NLP benchmark tasks. They compared tapered models against uniform-width baselines with identical total parameter counts.
📊 实验
结果		 锥形语言模型在所有四种架构和三种规模上均一致性地降低了困惑度（perplexity），超过均匀宽度基线。反向锥形（前窄后宽）则一致性地损害性能，验证了方向的重要性。下游基准任务性能同样全面提升。所有提升在零额外参数和计算开销下实现，纯靠更聪明的容量分配。（具体数值在论文各架构/规模实验中均有报告，各设置下困惑度均有实质性下降。） Tapered Language Models consistently improved perplexity over uniform-width baselines across all four architectures and all three model scales. The reverse taper (wide at the end, narrow at the start) consistently hurt performance, confirming that the direction of tapering matters. Downstream benchmark performance also improved across the board. The gains were achieved with strictly zero additional parameters or compute — purely through smarter capacity redistribution. (Specific numerical perplexity deltas are reported per architecture/scale in the paper, with consistent improvements in the range of meaningful perplexity reductions across settings.)
🏁 结论 本文证明，只需将参数向早层倾斜、晚层收窄，在不增加任何预算的情况下，即可稳定提升各主流架构语言模型的性能。"深度感知容量分配"是一个简单、通用、完全免费的设计原则，长期被忽视，现在终于被挖掘出来。 This paper proves that simply giving earlier layers more parameters and later layers fewer — without changing the total budget — reliably makes language models better across all major architectures. Depth-aware capacity allocation is a free, universally applicable design principle that has been hiding in plain sight since the original Transformer.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

核心操作是调整每层中的MLP模块（负责大部分参数的"思考单元"）的宽度。不再让每层MLP等宽，而是按余弦曲线从第一层到最后一层平滑地逐渐缩小——前宽后窄。这样，早层有更多容量学习丰富的语言表示，晚层只需少量参数做微调。总参数预算不变，只是换了个更聪明的分配方式。 The key component being resized is called the MLP (a feedforward "thinking" block inside each layer), which holds most of the model's parameters. Instead of making every MLP the same width, the authors shrink it gradually from the first layer to the last, following a smooth cosine-shaped curve — wide at the start, narrow at the end. This means early layers get more capacity to learn rich representations, while later layers, which only need to do light refinement, use fewer parameters. The total parameter budget stays the same; resources are just redistributed depth-aware.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[w_l = w_{\text{base}} \cdot \left(1 + \alpha \cos\left(\frac{\pi l}{L}\right)\right)\]
💡 第 l 层 MLP 的宽度由余弦函数决定：l=0（第一层）时宽度最大，l=L（最后一层）时宽度最小，α 控制锥形的幅度，总参数量保持不变。 The width of the MLP at layer l follows a cosine curve: widest at the first layer (l=0) and narrowest at the last (l=L), with α controlling how steep the taper is, all while keeping the total parameter count fixed.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

这一发现可以立即落地：任何AI团队今天就能把锥形设计套用到现有架构上，零成本换来更好的模型。在AI训练成本高昂的今天，更聪明的参数分配哪怕带来小幅提升，也意味着真实的成本节省和产品竞争力提升。 This finding is immediately actionable: any AI lab can apply tapering to their existing model architecture today, for free, and get a better model. As AI models are trained at enormous cost, even small efficiency gains from smarter parameter distribution translate into real savings and better products.

🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像给一个厨师学徒一本精心编排的菜谱大全——从简单到复杂，还针对不同类型的厨师写了不同版本。以前只有大饭店（大公司）才有这种秘密配方，现在任何人都能用这本开放菜谱，培养出一个厨艺出众的厨师，哪怕厨房不大。Think of it like teaching someone to cook by giving them a massive, cleverly organized cookbook — with recipes ranging from easy to hard, written for different types of chefs. 维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 能够使用电脑终端（命令行）来完成任务的AI智能体越来越流行，但训练这类模型非常困难。公开的训练数据极少，也没有清晰的训练方法，测试用的评测基准也很难。这导致大多数优秀的终端AI智能体只掌握在大公司手里，学术界很难跟上。 AI models that can use a computer terminal (like a command line) to do tasks are increasingly popular, but training them well is hard. There's very little public data, no clear training recipe, and the benchmarks used to test them are very tough. This means most good terminal-using AI agents are only developed by big companies behind closed doors.
💡 解决
的亮点		 - 创新的数据生成方法：通过"分类法"组合任务难度、用户角色和多种验证器，低成本生成大量多样化的终端训练环境。 - 极简的强化学习训练方式：只看最终结果（成功/失败），不需要复杂的奖励设计。 - 开源数据集规模是此前同类数据集的2.5倍以上，训练出的模型超越更大参数量的竞争模型。 - A novel data generation strategy using a "taxonomy" that mixes task difficulty, different user personas, and multiple verifiers to cheaply create diverse terminal training environments. - A clean, outcome-only RL training recipe — no complex reward shaping, just: did the agent succeed or not? - The resulting open dataset is 2.5x larger than any previously released terminal-agent dataset, and the trained model beats much bigger closed models.
🧪 实验
内容		 主要评测基准是Terminal-Bench 2.0，这是一个测试真实终端任务的高难度基准。他们将自己的90亿参数模型与此前更大的闭源和开源模型进行对比，以任务成功率为核心指标。他们还通过消融实验评估数据配方中每个组成部分（难度控制、用户角色、验证器多样化）的贡献，并分别测试了强化学习训练版本和监督微调（SFT）版本的模型。 The main benchmark used is Terminal-Bench 2.0, a challenging test of real terminal tasks. They compared their 9B model against larger closed-source and open-source models from prior work, using task success rate as the key metric. They also evaluated the impact of each component of their data recipe (difficulty, personas, verifiers) through ablation studies, and tested both RL-trained and supervised fine-tuning (SFT) versions of their model.
📊 实验
结果		 Tmax在Terminal-Bench 2.0上取得27%的成功率，仅使用90亿参数的模型，超越了此前参数量大得多的开源和闭源模型。发布的数据集规模超过此前所有公开终端智能体数据集的2.5倍。消融实验表明，强化学习训练与新型数据分类法的组合是性能提升的关键驱动因素。 Tmax achieves 27% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 with only a 9B parameter model, outperforming prior open and closed models that are significantly larger. The released dataset is over 2.5x larger than any previously public terminal-agent dataset. The combination of RL training with their novel data taxonomy is shown through ablations to be the key driver of performance gains.
🏁 结论 Tmax证明了一个参数量小但数据和训练方法精心设计的开源模型，可以在终端智能体任务上媲美甚至超越更大的商业模型。它为学术界提供了一个强大、可复现的基线，供未来研究使用。 Tmax proves that a small, carefully trained open model with a well-designed data recipe can rival or beat much larger commercial models on terminal agent tasks. It gives the research community a strong, reproducible baseline to build on.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队设计了一套自动生成大量真实终端任务的方法。他们创建了一个"分类法"——类似一本菜谱——混合了三种"食材"：任务难度（简单到复杂）、用户角色（比如开发者、系统管理员）、以及多种验证答案正确性的方式。这样可以低成本生成海量多样化的训练场景。之后，他们用强化学习（RL）训练一个90亿参数的开源语言模型，训练信号极其简单：任务完成就是成功，否则就是失败，不需要复杂的打分机制。模型通过在数千个模拟终端环境中反复尝试来学习。 The team built a smart way to automatically generate lots of realistic terminal tasks. They created a "taxonomy" — think of it as a recipe book — that mixes three ingredients: how hard the task is, what kind of user is asking (e.g., a developer vs. a sysadmin), and different ways to check if the answer is correct. This produces a huge variety of training scenarios cheaply. They then trained an open 9-billion-parameter language model using reinforcement learning (RL), where the only signal is whether the agent completed the task or not — dead simple, no elaborate scoring tricks. The model learns by trying, failing, and succeeding across thousands of simulated terminal environments.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

能操作终端的AI智能体正在成为开发者和系统管理员的实用工具，能够公开训练出强大的此类模型（而非只有大公司才能做到）对整个AI社区意义重大。通过开源数据、模型和代码，这项工作让任何人都能构建更好的编程和系统管理AI助手。 Terminal-using AI agents are becoming real-world tools that developers and system administrators rely on — being able to train powerful ones openly (not just in secret at big companies) is a big deal for the broader AI community. By open-sourcing the data, model, and code, this work lets anyone build better AI assistants for coding and system management tasks.

维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练一个能根据文字描述生成精美图片的AI模型，需要消耗天文数字的计算资源——就像为了画一张草图要用掉整个印刷厂的墨水。现有方法虽然尝试用"语义引导"来加速训练，但都只在简单的小数据集和低分辨率图片上验证过，无法直接用于真实世界的大规模应用。这篇论文想解决的核心问题是：能不能在保持顶级图片质量的前提下，大幅降低训练所需的计算量？ Training a powerful AI that turns text descriptions into beautiful images costs an enormous amount of computing power — think of it like needing a whole power plant just to run a single art studio. Existing approaches have tried using "semantic guidance" to speed things up, but only tested it on small, simple datasets, so it wasn't clear if the trick would work at real-world scale. This paper asks: can we get top-quality image generation while spending only a fraction of what competitors spend on training?
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 提出"语义优先扩散"新范式：让模型先理解图片的整体语义意图，再逐步生成细节，而不是从纯粹的随机噪声开始盲猜。 • 首次将语义引导扩散训练成功扩展到十亿参数级别（1B、2B、5B），并系统研究了模型规模的影响。 • 仅用约12.5万A800 GPU小时（相当于竞争对手10%–20%的算力）就训练出可媲美甚至超越Qwen-Image和Z-Image的模型。 • 提供了每种规模的"快速版"蒸馏模型（DMD2 Turbo），方便在不同硬件和延迟要求下灵活部署。 • Introduces "semantic-first diffusion": the model grasps the overall meaning of what to draw before filling in fine details, rather than starting from pure random noise with no direction. • First to successfully scale semantic-guided diffusion training to billion-parameter models (1B, 2B, 5B), with a systematic study of how size affects quality. • Achieves results on par with or better than leading rivals using only ~10–20% of their training compute budget. • Releases distilled "turbo" variants for each model size, making fast inference possible on a wide range of hardware.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究团队在多个业界标准评测基准上测试了SeFi-Image，包括GenEval（综合生成能力）、DPG（文本-图像对齐）、LongTextBench（长文本理解）、OneIG和CVTG-2K。对比的基线模型包括Qwen-Image和Z-Image等同量级的顶级文图生成模型。实验覆盖了1B、2B、5B三种参数规模，并对快速蒸馏版本也进行了评估，以验证在不同算力条件下的实用性。 The team evaluated SeFi-Image across five major industry benchmarks: GenEval (overall generation quality), DPG (how well images match text descriptions), LongTextBench (handling long and complex prompts), OneIG, and CVTG-2K. They compared against top-tier rivals like Qwen-Image and Z-Image. All three model sizes (1B, 2B, 5B) were benchmarked, and the distilled turbo variants were also tested to confirm practical usability under tighter compute budgets.
📊 实验
结果		 SeFi-Image 5B模型仅用约12.5万A800 GPU小时训练完成（约为Z-Image训练算力的10%–20%），却在GenEval、DPG、LongTextBench、OneIG和CVTG-2K等基准上达到与Qwen-Image和Z-Image相当甚至更优的成绩。较小的1B和2B规模模型也表现出良好的性能扩展规律，验证了"语义优先扩散"在不同规模下的一致有效性。蒸馏加速版（DMD2 Turbo）在大幅减少推理步骤的同时保持了接近原模型的图像质量。 The 5B model was trained with just ~125K A800 GPU hours — roughly 10–20% of the compute used by Z-Image — yet matches or surpasses both Qwen-Image and Z-Image on benchmarks including GenEval, DPG, LongTextBench, OneIG, and CVTG-2K. The 1B and 2B models demonstrate consistent scaling behavior, confirming the approach works across sizes. The DMD2-distilled turbo variants maintain near-original image quality while significantly reducing the number of inference steps required.
🏁 结论 SeFi-Image证明了"语义优先扩散"能够以极低的训练成本（仅需竞争对手约10%–20%的算力）训练出顶级质量的文图生成模型，并成功将这一范式扩展至50亿参数规模。这项工作不仅推动了高效图像生成技术的发展，也通过全面开源为社区提供了实用、可直接部署的工具。 SeFi-Image proves that semantic-first diffusion can train a world-class text-to-image model at just 10–20% of competitors' compute costs, successfully scaling the paradigm to 5 billion parameters for the first time. By open-sourcing all code and weights, the work gives the broader community practical, ready-to-use tools and valuable insights into efficient image generation.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

普通的图像扩散模型从一张完全随机的噪声图开始，一步步去噪，就像在完全黑暗中拼拼图。SeFi-Image的关键创新是"语义优先"：在扩散过程的早期阶段，模型先利用文本的语义理解来锚定图片的整体结构和内容（比如先搞清楚"这是一只猫坐在椅子上"），然后再在这个语义框架下逐步填充纹理和细节。这样做相当于给模型一张"蓝图"，避免了大量无效的早期探索，从而大幅减少了训练所需的步骤和算力。模型提供三种参数规模（1B/2B/5B），并额外提供蒸馏版加速推理，代码和权重全部公开。 Typical image diffusion models start from pure random noise and slowly "de-noise" their way to a picture — like solving a jigsaw puzzle in pitch darkness. SeFi-Image's key trick is "semantic first": in the early stages of generation, the model uses its understanding of the text prompt to anchor the big-picture structure (e.g., "a cat sitting on a chair") before filling in textures and fine details. Think of it as handing the artist a rough blueprint before letting them paint freely — it avoids a lot of wasted early guesswork. This makes training far more efficient. The team also distilled each model into a faster "turbo" version and released all code and weights publicly.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

训练顶级AI绘图模型的高昂成本一直是只有少数科技巨头才能负担的门槛，SeFi-Image大幅降低了这一门槛，让更多研究者和小团队也能构建和定制强大的图像生成工具。对于普通用户而言，这意味着更快、更便宜、更广泛可用的AI绘图服务将成为现实。 The sky-high cost of training top-tier image AI has kept this capability locked inside a handful of tech giants — SeFi-Image dramatically lowers that barrier, letting smaller teams and researchers build and customize powerful image generators. For everyday users, this paves the way for faster, cheaper, and more widely available AI art and design tools.

🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像给一支足球队每个球员写战术手册——这篇论文研究的是：自动帮每个队员优化战术说明，能不能让整支球队赢得更多？结果发现：有时候很管用，但效果完全取决于队伍规模和球员之间怎么传球配合。Think of it like coaching a sports team: each player gets a game plan (the prompt). This paper tests whether automatically rewriting those game plans makes the whole team win more — and discovers it works great sometimes, but totally depends on the team size and how players pass the ball to each other.

❓ 待解决
的问题		 当你搭建一个由多个AI协同工作的系统时（就像一支AI团队），每个成员都需要一份"工作说明书"——即提示词。手写好的提示词很难，而自动优化这些提示词究竟有没有用、什么时候有用，目前完全没人搞清楚。之前的研究只针对单个AI模型，多智能体团队场景几乎是一片空白。 When you build an AI system made of multiple AI agents working together (like a team of robots), you need to give each agent written instructions called "prompts." Writing good prompts is hard, and we don't know if automatically optimizing these prompts actually makes the whole team work better. Existing research only studied single AI models, leaving a big gap for multi-agent teamwork scenarios.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首个专门针对多智能体系统提示词优化的系统性基准框架MAS-PromptBench，填补了领域空白。 • 全面测试了任务类型、团队规模、工作流结构、通信协议等多种变量，揭示了哪些因素决定优化是否奏效。 • 揭示了核心挑战：随着智能体团队规模增大，寻找最优提示词的搜索空间呈指数级爆炸，使优化难度大幅上升。 • First systematic benchmark (MAS-PromptBench) designed specifically to study prompt optimization in multi-agent LLM systems across diverse settings. • Tests a wide range of variables: task type, team size, workflow structure, and how agents communicate — revealing which factors most influence whether optimization helps. • Exposes a key challenge: as teams grow larger, the search space for finding good prompts explodes exponentially, making optimization much harder.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者将两种单智能体提示词优化方法扩展后，在多种多智能体系统配置下进行测试。配置变量涵盖四个维度：任务类型（如推理、问答、编程）、工作流结构（如并行、串行）、智能体通信协议以及团队规模。所有设置均使用标准任务完成质量指标进行评估。 The authors tested two prompt optimizers extended from state-of-the-art single-agent methods across a broad set of multi-agent system configurations. These configurations varied along four key dimensions: task type (e.g., reasoning, QA, coding), workflow structure (e.g., parallel, sequential), communication protocol (how agents share information), and team size. Standard performance metrics were used to evaluate task completion quality across all settings.
📊 实验
结果		 研究表明，在合适条件下，提示词优化可以为多智能体系统带来显著性能提升，部分配置下相比未优化基线有明显改善。但提升效果对系统配置非常敏感：某些团队规模、工作流类型和通信协议下几乎没有收益。随团队规模增大导致的搜索空间指数级爆炸被确认为制约稳定提升的核心瓶颈。具体数值（如各基准准确率、提升幅度等）详见论文实验章节。 The paper reports that prompt optimization can unlock significant performance gains in multi-agent systems under the right conditions — in some configurations delivering meaningful improvements over non-optimized baselines. However, gains are highly sensitive to system configuration: certain team sizes, workflow types, and communication protocols see little to no benefit. The exponentially growing search space as team size increases is identified as a concrete bottleneck limiting consistent gains across all settings. Specific numerical results (e.g., exact accuracy percentages or improvement margins per benchmark) are detailed within the paper's experimental sections.
🏁 结论 本文证明了提示词优化在特定条件下确实能显著提升多智能体AI系统的表现，并首次系统性地绘制出"什么时候有效、为什么有效"的规律图谱。MAS-PromptBench为未来多智能体AI提示词驱动优化研究奠定了基础工具。 This paper proves that prompt optimization can meaningfully boost multi-agent AI systems, but only under specific conditions — and it provides the first systematic map of when and why it works. It establishes MAS-PromptBench as a foundational tool for future research on scalable, prompt-driven improvements in multi-agent AI.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队构建了一个基准框架，在多种多智能体系统配置下测试提示词优化效果。他们将两种专为单个AI模型设计的顶尖提示词优化器扩展到多智能体团队场景，然后在不同任务、不同团队规模和不同协作方式的组合下运行这些优化器，仔细测量什么时候性能会提升、提升多少，以及什么情况下完全没有效果。 The researchers built a benchmark framework that tests prompt optimization across many different multi-agent system configurations. They took two state-of-the-art prompt optimizers originally designed for single AI models and extended them to work on multi-agent teams. Then they ran these optimizers across many combinations of tasks, team sizes, and coordination styles, carefully measuring when performance improved, by how much, and when it failed to help at all.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

当今越来越多的AI产品依赖多个AI智能体协同工作（如客服机器人、编程助手、研究工具），如何在不重新训练模型的情况下高效调整它们的"工作说明书"具有巨大的实际价值。这个基准为开发者提供了清晰的指南，帮助判断何时值得投入提示词优化，从而节省时间、金钱和算力。 As AI products increasingly rely on teams of AI agents working together (like customer service bots, coding assistants, or research tools), knowing how to efficiently tune their instructions without retraining is hugely valuable. This benchmark gives developers a clear guide for when prompt optimization is worth the effort — saving time, money, and compute.
