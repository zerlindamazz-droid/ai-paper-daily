Do as I Do: Dexterous Manipulation Data from Everyday Human Videos 照着我做：从日常人类视频中获取灵巧操作数据

🧑‍🔬 Bhawna Paliwal, Haritheja Etukuru, William Liang, Pieter Abbeel et al.

图 Figure · Page 5 of paper

🎯 把普通人类手部视频变成机器人灵巧手的训练数据
An algorithm that turns everyday human hand videos into training data for dexterous robot hands.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个学徒通过看YouTube学厨师切菜——但这里的"学徒"是机器人：它看着视频里人手的每个动作，在脑海中建出3D立体模型，再把那些动作"翻译"成自己那双结构不同的机械手能做的动作，然后真的去厨房里操作。Think of it like learning to cook by watching YouTube videos — except instead of a human student watching and copying, it's a robot that watches your hands, figures out the exact finger movements in 3D, then adapts those moves to its own different-shaped hands and goes off to cook in the real kitchen.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 要教会机器人用多指机械手灵巧地操作物体，最大的瓶颈是训练数据太少、太贵。互联网上明明有海量人类手部操作视频，但人手和机器手的结构不同，直接"照搬"动作根本行不通。现有方法在估计手和物体的交互细节时误差大，导致转化出来的机器人动作不可用。 Teaching robots to use their "hands" (multi-fingered grippers) to manipulate objects is incredibly hard because we don't have enough training data. Capturing robot data requires expensive setups, but the internet is full of videos of humans using their hands. The challenge is: how do you translate what a human hand does in a regular video into instructions a robot hand can actually follow?
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 仅用普通单目RGB摄像头视频，就能重建精细的3D手物交互，无需深度传感器。• 创新性地解决了"具身迁移"难题——将人手动作映射到结构不同的机器手上，同时保留操作意图。• 同时支持第一人称（自拍视角）和第三人称（旁观视角）的互联网视频，极大提升数据来源的可扩展性。• 为实践者提供了一套收集人类操作视频的最优"操作手册"。 • Reconstructs detailed 3D hand-object interactions from plain, single-camera (monocular RGB) videos — no depth sensors needed. • Bridges the "embodiment gap" by retargeting human hand motions to physically different robot hands while preserving the intent of the manipulation. • Works with both first-person (egocentric) and third-person (exocentric) internet videos, making it highly scalable. • Provides a practical "playbook" of best practices for researchers collecting human video data for robot training.
🧪 实验
内容		 团队在多个基准上测试了DO AS I DO：（1）带有3D真实标注的标准手物交互数据集，用于测量重建精度；（2）一个新收集的"野外"在线视频数据集，包含多种日常操作任务。他们与多个最先进的手部姿态估计和动作重定向基线模型进行对比，评估指标包括3D关节误差、接触准确率，以及生成的机器人轨迹在仿真环境和真实硬件上的成功率。 The team tested DO AS I DO on multiple benchmarks: (1) standard hand-object interaction datasets that come with 3D ground-truth annotations to measure reconstruction accuracy; (2) a newly collected dataset of in-the-wild online video clips showing diverse everyday manipulation tasks. They compared against several state-of-the-art baselines for hand pose estimation and retargeting, evaluating metrics like 3D joint error, contact accuracy, and the success rate of generated robot trajectories in simulation and on real hardware.
📊 实验
结果		 DO AS I DO在所有测试基准上均超越现有最先进方法。在带有真实标注的数据集上，3D关节重建误差显著低于基线模型。重定向后的机器人轨迹在真实灵巧手硬件上的物理可行性和执行成功率更高。该方法在手部姿态估计领域标准基准上的多项评估指标中均排名第一，对第一人称和第三人称视频均表现一致。 DO AS I DO outperforms prior state-of-the-art methods on hand-object interaction estimation across all tested benchmarks. On ground-truth datasets, it achieves measurably lower 3D joint reconstruction error compared to existing baselines. The retargeted trajectories show higher physical plausibility and executability rates on real dexterous robot hardware. Specific quantitative gains are demonstrated on standard benchmarks used in the hand-pose estimation community, with the method consistently ranking first across evaluation metrics for both egocentric and exocentric video sources.
🏁 结论 本文证明了普通的人类手部互联网视频可以成为机器人灵巧操作的高质量、可扩展训练数据来源。通过攻克单目RGB视频下的手物重建与跨具身动作迁移难题，本文为低成本训练高能力机器手铺平了道路。 DO AS I DO proves that ordinary internet videos of human hands are a viable, scalable source of high-quality training data for dexterous robot manipulation. By cracking the problem of hand-object reconstruction and cross-embodiment retargeting from monocular RGB video, it opens a path to training capable robot hands without expensive data collection infrastructure.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

第一步，算法"观看"一段普通人类手部视频，用计算机视觉技术逐帧重建手和物体的3D形状与位置，就像一位雕塑家边看你操作边捏出每一个动作的立体模型。第二步，进行"动作重定向"——算法计算出结构不同的机器手（手指长度、关节数量都不同）应该如何运动，才能实现与人手相同的抓握和物体接触效果。第三步，将这些姿态串联成完整的动作序列，并通过碰撞检测和物理合理性过滤，确保生成的动作在真实机器人上安全可执行。 First, DO AS I DO watches a regular video of a human hand picking up or manipulating an object and uses computer vision to reconstruct the 3D shapes and positions of both the hand and the object frame-by-frame. Think of it like a 3D sculptor who watches you work and builds a clay model of every move you make. Next, it "retargets" those 3D motions — it figures out how a robot hand with different finger lengths and joints would need to move to achieve the same grip and object contact. Finally, it chains these poses into a full action sequence the robot can execute in the real world, complete with collision checks and physical plausibility filters to make sure the robot won't break itself.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

目前训练机器人手需要昂贵的专用设备和大量人工示范，成本极高、速度极慢。这项研究有望让研究人员直接利用YouTube上数十亿条烹饪、手工、维修视频来训练机器人，大幅加速家庭、医院、工厂用机器人的研发进程。 Right now, training robot hands to do useful tasks requires custom lab setups and thousands of hours of painstaking robot demonstrations — it's slow and expensive. This work could let researchers use the billions of cooking, crafting, and repair videos already on YouTube to train robots instead, dramatically speeding up progress toward robots that can help in homes, hospitals, and factories.

You Don't Need Strong Assumptions: Visual Representation Learning via Temporal Differences 你不需要强假设：基于时间差异的视觉表示学习

🧑‍🔬 Ninad Daithankar, Alexi Gladstone, Yann LeCun, Heng Ji

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 无需标签或数据增强，仅靠"过去决定未来"学习视觉表示
A new self-supervised video learning method that needs no labels, augmentations, or tricks — just the idea that the past shapes the future.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像学会预测一个滚动的球会落在哪里——你不需要有人告诉你物理规则，也不需要用特殊方式抛球。你只需一遍遍看它滚动，大脑自然会找到规律。TDV就是用同样的方式教AI：只要看视频，然后学习"接下来发生的事是由刚才发生的事导致的"。Think of it like learning to predict where a rolling ball will land — you don't need anyone to tell you the rules of physics or to spin the ball in a special way first. You just watch it roll, over and over, and your brain figures out the pattern. TDV teaches AI the same way: just watch videos and learn that what happens next is caused by what happened before.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 让AI理解图像和视频，通常需要人工标注数据（成本极高），或者依赖一些"小技巧"，比如随机裁剪、模糊或遮挡图像的一部分。这些技巧就像拐杖，帮AI找到学习重点。但问题是，随着数据规模增大，这些拐杖反而会成为瓶颈。我们需要一种更少依赖预设假设的学习方式。 Teaching AI to understand images and videos normally requires either human-labeled data (expensive!) or clever tricks like randomly cropping, blurring, or masking parts of images. These tricks act as crutches — they tell the AI what to pay attention to. The problem is that as we scale up data, these crutches become limiting bottlenecks. We need a way for AI to learn visual understanding with as few built-in assumptions as possible.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 提出TDV（视觉时间差异）：一种仅利用视频中时间流动的自监督学习方法，无需数据增强、遮挡或裁剪等技巧。• 用实验证明了一个趋势：数据量越大，预设"小技巧"反而越有害，验证了减少假设的必要性。• 在密集空间任务上达到与现有最先进方法相当的性能，证明这种极简方法真正具有竞争力。 • Introduces TDV (Temporal Difference in Vision): a self-supervised learning method that uses only the natural flow of time in video — no augmentations, no masking, no cropping needed. • Empirically confirms the trend: stronger inductive biases (tricks/assumptions) hurt more as data grows, validating the need for assumption-lean methods. • Matches state-of-the-art methods on dense spatial tasks despite using far fewer built-in assumptions, showing this minimalist approach is genuinely competitive.
🧪 实验
内容		 作者在标准视觉表示学习基准上测试了TDV，重点关注密集空间任务（要求模型理解整张图像细粒度细节的任务，而不仅仅是一个类别标签）。他们与使用数据增强或遮挡策略的主流自监督学习基线进行了比较。同时进行了规模化实验，测量随着训练数据增加，最优归纳偏置强度如何变化，直接验证了其核心假设。 The authors tested TDV on standard visual representation learning benchmarks, focusing on dense spatial tasks (tasks requiring the model to understand fine-grained details across the whole image, not just a single label). They compared against leading self-supervised learning baselines that use augmentations or masking strategies. They also conducted scaling experiments to measure how the optimal strength of inductive biases changes as more training data is added, directly testing their core hypothesis.
📊 实验
结果		 TDV在密集空间基准测试上与最先进的自监督学习方法持平，同时所依赖的归纳偏置远少于这些方法。规模化实验明确证实：随着训练数据增加，最优归纳偏置强度可测量地下降，验证了论文的核心论断。该方法在不使用任何数据增强、遮挡或裁剪的情况下达到了有竞争力的性能，证明时间因果性单独作为学习信号是充分且可扩展的。 TDV matches state-of-the-art self-supervised learning recipes on dense spatial benchmarks despite relying on far fewer inductive biases. Scaling experiments confirm that as training data grows, the optimal strength of inductive biases measurably decreases — validating the paper's central claim. The method achieves competitive performance without any augmentation, masking, or cropping, demonstrating that temporal causality alone is a sufficient and scalable learning signal.
🏁 结论 TDV证明了仅靠观察视频中时间的自然因果流动，就能教会AI理解图像和视频，无需标签、无需技巧、无需强假设。这项工作为视觉表示学习奠定了一个可扩展的基础，随着数据和算力的增加，性能只会越来越好。 TDV proves that you can teach AI to understand images and video just by watching the natural cause-and-effect flow of time — no labels, no tricks, no strong assumptions needed. This work lays a scalable foundation for visual representation learning that should only get better as more data and compute become available.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

TDV同时训练两个神经网络：一个负责"看"视频帧的内容（图像编码器），另一个负责"感知"帧与帧之间的运动（运动编码器）。核心规则非常简单：当前帧的表示 + 编码的运动 = 下一帧的表示。就像说"我现在的位置 + 我走的一步 = 我到达的位置"。通过让这个等式成立，模型仅从视频天然的因果结构中学习丰富的视觉特征，不需要人工标注，也不需要任何数据处理技巧，唯一的假设是一个常识：过去会导致未来。 TDV trains two neural networks simultaneously: one that encodes what a video frame looks like (image encoder), and one that encodes how things are moving between frames (motion encoder). The key rule is simple: the current frame's representation + the encoded motion should equal the next frame's representation. Think of it like saying "where I am now + the step I took = where I end up." By forcing this equation to hold, the model learns rich visual features purely from the natural cause-and-effect structure of video — no human labels, no data manipulation tricks required. The only assumption baked in is a commonsense one: the past causes the future.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\mathbf{r}_{t} + f_{\text{motion}}(\mathbf{m}_{t}) = \mathbf{r}_{t+1}\]
💡 当前帧的表示向量 加上 运动编码器对帧间运动的编码 等于 下一帧的表示向量。这是TDV的核心等式，强迫模型同时学会理解静态内容和动态运动。 The current frame's representation plus the encoded motion between frames must equal the next frame's representation. This single equation is the heart of TDV — it forces the model to jointly learn both what things look like and how they move.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

当今大多数AI视觉系统都依赖工程师凭直觉设计的"小技巧"，这些技巧未必能随数据规模增长而持续有效。TDV指向了一个未来：AI只需像婴儿一样通过看视频来学习理解世界，无需人工精心设计，有望以更少的人工干预解锁更强大、更通用的视觉AI。 Most AI vision systems today secretly rely on hand-crafted tricks that engineers designed based on intuition — tricks that may not scale. TDV points toward a future where AI can learn to see the world simply by watching videos, the same way babies learn, potentially unlocking much more powerful and general visual AI with less human engineering effort.

Latent Thought Flow: Efficient Latent Reasoning in Large Language Models 潜在思维流：大语言模型中的高效潜在推理

🧑‍🔬 Xiandong Zou, Jing Huang, Jianshu Li, Pan Zhou

图 Figure · Page 3 of paper

🎯 让大模型"在脑中默想"而非逐字输出，推理更快更准
LTF teaches LLMs to reason silently in continuous space — faster, shorter, and more accurate than thinking out loud.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个经验丰富的棋手，不需要把每一步棋都大声说出来——他们在脑海里快速扫描可能的走法，遇到难局多想一会儿，遇到简单局面直接跳过。LTF就是教AI学会这种"心算"能力：默默思考，遇到难题多花几步内心推理，遇到简单问题直接给出答案，既聪明又高效。Think of it like a seasoned chess player who doesn't mutter every move aloud — instead, they rapidly scan possibilities in their head, spend more mental energy on tricky positions, and quickly skip obvious ones. LTF teaches AI to do exactly that: think quietly and efficiently, allocating more "inner thought steps" only when the question is genuinely hard.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现在的AI大模型（比如ChatGPT）在回答问题前，常常需要把每一步推理过程都用文字写出来（即"链式思维"），这就像学生做题时必须把所有草稿写在答题纸上一样，非常慢也很耗资源。虽然有研究尝试让模型"在内心默想"（潜在推理），但这些方法不够聪明——不知道什么时候该多想一会儿，什么时候可以快速作答，容易在简单题上浪费时间，或在难题上草草了事。 Modern AI models like ChatGPT often "think out loud" by writing out every reasoning step before giving an answer — a technique called Chain-of-Thought. But this is slow and expensive: the model has to generate hundreds of extra words just to reach a conclusion. Existing attempts to make this thinking invisible (latent reasoning) still don't have a smart way to decide how much thinking is actually needed for a given question, often wasting effort on easy problems or cutting short on hard ones.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 提出"潜在思维流"框架：将推理建模为连续空间中长度可变的轨迹，像水流一样灵活，而非固定的文字序列。• 引入基于GFlowNet的采样器：能够在多条推理路径之间智能分配概率，同时权衡答案质量与计算开销。• 熵加权子轨迹平衡目标：即使只有最终答案有监督信号，也能对中间推理步骤给予奖励，解决"稀疏奖励"难题。• 参考先验正则化器：防止模型在探索过程中"跑偏"，确保推理过程合理可控。 • Novel "Latent Thought Flow" framework: reasoning is treated as a flowing, variable-length journey through continuous (invisible) thought space — not a fixed sequence of words. • Smart probability allocation: uses a GFlowNet-inspired sampler that learns to spread probability across many possible reasoning paths, balancing answer quality against computation cost. • Entropy-Weighted Subtrajectory Balance: a clever training trick that gives credit to partial reasoning paths even when only the final answer is checked, solving the "sparse reward" problem. • A reference-prior regularizer keeps the model from going off the rails during exploration, anchoring it to sensible behavior.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究团队在多个数学和逻辑推理基准数据集上评估了LTF，涵盖了常见的逐步推理测试任务。对比基线包括显式链式思维模型（CoT）和现有的潜在推理方法。实验分为微调设置（在同领域训练测试）和迁移学习设置（在未见过的题型上测试）两种场景，同时衡量最终答案准确率和推理长度（计算开销）两个指标。 The authors evaluated LTF on multiple mathematical and logical reasoning benchmarks, including datasets commonly used to test step-by-step reasoning ability. They compared against strong baselines including explicit Chain-of-Thought models and existing latent reasoning methods. Experiments covered both fine-tuning (training on the same domain) and transfer learning (testing on unseen problem types) settings. The evaluation measured both final answer accuracy and the length (cost) of the reasoning process.
📊 实验
结果		 与强潜在推理基线相比，LTF平均准确率提升9.5%，同时推理长度缩短27.2%——也就是说模型既更准确，又更快更省资源，实现了"又好又快"。LTF同样超越了显式链式思维方法，证明经过良好设计的"默想式"潜在推理可以超越冗长的逐步文字推理。 Compared to strong latent reasoning baselines, LTF improves accuracy by 9.5% on average while simultaneously reducing reasoning length by 27.2%. This means the model is both more accurate AND faster/cheaper at the same time. LTF also outperforms explicit Chain-of-Thought methods, demonstrating that silent latent reasoning can beat verbose step-by-step reasoning when done right.
🏁 结论 本文证明，通过在连续内部空间中"默想"，并借助能权衡准确性与计算成本的概率采样器引导，大语言模型可以同时实现更高准确率和更低推理开销。LTF为潜在推理树立了新标杆，说明更聪明的路径分配——而非堆砌更多计算量——才是提升AI思维质量的关键。 This paper proves that LLMs can reason more accurately and efficiently by thinking silently in a continuous internal space, guided by a probability-aware sampler that balances correctness and cost. LTF sets a new standard for latent reasoning, showing that smarter path allocation — not just more computation — is the key to better AI thinking.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

LTF把模型的推理过程想象成一段看不见的"内心旅程"：模型不是逐字写出思考，而是在一个连续的"思维空间"里一步步向前探索，就像在脑海中走迷宫。每一步都带有一点随机性，由一个专门训练的"采样器"来引导方向。这个采样器用GFlowNet的思路训练，学会把更多注意力分配给那些又准确又省力的推理路径。由于训练时只有最终答案有标注（中间步骤没有），论文设计了"熵加权子轨迹平衡"损失函数，把功劳从终点反向传递给每一段中间路径。此外，参考先验正则化器就像一根安全绳，防止模型在探索时"乱跑"，保持推理的合理性。 LTF treats reasoning as an invisible journey through a continuous "thought space" — imagine the model's inner deliberation as a flowing river rather than a printed list of steps. At each moment, the model takes a stochastic (slightly random) step forward in this thought space, guided by a learned sampler. The sampler is trained using a GFlowNet approach, meaning it learns to assign more probability to reasoning paths that are both correct AND efficient — not just the ones that look the most rewarded. To handle the fact that only final answers are labeled (not intermediate thoughts), the paper introduces an Entropy-Weighted Subtrajectory Balance loss that distributes credit backwards through the reasoning flow. A reference-prior regularizer acts like a safety rope, preventing the model from exploring too wildly and losing touch with sensible language representations.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\mathcal{L}_{\text{EWSB}} = \sum_{t} H(q_t) \cdot \left[ \log \frac{F(s_t)}{F(s_{t+1}) \cdot P_F(s_{t+1}|s_t)} \right]^2\]
💡 熵加权子轨迹平衡损失：用每一步的不确定性（熵）加权，让模型对中间推理步骤也能获得合理的训练信号，而不只依赖最终答案。 The Entropy-Weighted Subtrajectory Balance loss: weights each reasoning step by its uncertainty (entropy), so the model gets useful training signals from intermediate steps — not just the final answer.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

的问题		 教机器人做各种任务非常困难。不像文字数据可以从互联网上大量获取，机器人训练数据既昂贵又稀少，而且来自不同机械臂和环境的数据格式差异巨大，混在一起训练反而会让机器人越学越差。现有的机器人AI无法像ChatGPT那样靠"堆数据"来提升能力，因此一直局限于特定任务，稍有变化就"翻车"。 Teaching robots to handle many different tasks is really hard. ⚙️ Method · 方法详解

核心思路是：不能直接把不同机器人的数据混在一起训练，必须先让它们"说同一种语言"。Qwen-RobotManip从三个层面做统一对齐：统一机器人"看世界的方式"（表示对齐）、把各种动作格式转换为统一的动作空间（运动对齐）、把不同任务目标映射到同一个行为框架下（行为对齐）。在此基础上，一条人手视频转机器人轨迹的流水线，自动把普通人操作的视频转换成适配15种机器人平台的训练数据。再经过严格的数据筛选和整合，最终构建出约3.81万小时的预训练数据集。整个系统以Qwen-VL视觉语言模型为基础，天然具备强大的语言理解能力。 The key insight is that you can't just dump different robot datasets together — you first need to "align" them so they speak the same language. Qwen-RobotManip does this in three layers: it standardizes how robots perceive scenes (representation alignment), converts diverse motion formats into a unified action space (motion alignment), and maps different task goals into a common behavioral framework (behavioral alignment). On top of this, a human-to-robot synthesis pipeline watches regular videos of people using their hands and automatically generates equivalent robot arm trajectories for 15 different robot types. A rigorous data curation pipeline then filters and harmonizes everything into a massive ~38,100-hour pretraining corpus. The whole system is built on top of Qwen-VL, a powerful vision-language model, which gives the robot strong language understanding out of the box.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

这项工作让我们离"通用机器人"更近了一步——未来的机器人或许只需观看人类操作视频就能学会新技能，而不必每次都重新采集昂贵的机器人数据。由于全程使用开源数据，这也大幅降低了整个机器人研究社区的入门门槛，有望加速家庭、仓库和医疗机器人的落地。 This work brings us meaningfully closer to general-purpose robots that can learn from watching humans and handle new tasks without retraining from scratch — a key step toward robots that work in homes, warehouses, and hospitals. By using only open-source data and releasing the framework, it also lowers the barrier for the entire robotics research community to build on these advances.
