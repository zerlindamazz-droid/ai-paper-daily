Language Models Need Sleep 语言模型需要睡眠

🧑‍🔬 Sangyun Lee, Sean McLeish, Tom Goldstein, Giulia Fanti

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 让大模型定期"睡觉"压缩记忆，高效处理超长上下文任务
A sleep-like memory consolidation mechanism lets LLMs handle long contexts efficiently by compressing memory during "sleep" pauses.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个学生白天记了满满一桌笔记，每隔几小时就停下来，把所有笔记反复看几遍，浓缩写在一张小卡片上，然后把原来的散纸全扔掉。答题时只需看那张小卡片，又快又准——而且复习的遍数越多，越难的题也能答对。Think of it like a student who takes notes all day (wake mode), then every few hours closes their notebook, re-reads everything, writes a tight summary on a single index card, and tosses the loose papers (sleep mode). When a question comes, they only need the index card — fast and efficient — and the deeper their review session, the better they answer hard questions.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的AI语言模型在处理超长文本时会越来越慢、越来越贵——就像同时拿着几千张便利贴，根本拿不住。模型需要记住之前读过的所有内容，但随着内容越来越多，计算量会急剧膨胀，无法高效应对需要深度推理的长程任务。 Current AI language models struggle to handle very long conversations or documents — the longer the input, the slower and more expensive they become. It's like trying to hold thousands of sticky notes in your hand at once: eventually you just can't manage them all. Existing models can't efficiently compress or reorganize what they've already read while staying fast at answering new questions.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 引入"睡眠"阶段：模型定期暂停，将近期上下文压缩进紧凑的"快速权重"，再清空短期缓存，类似人类睡眠中的记忆巩固。 • 睡眠期间进行多轮循环处理，让模型在不拖慢实时回答速度的前提下进行更深层推理。 • 睡眠时间越长（N越大），性能越好，在需要多步推理的任务上提升尤为显著。 • Introduces a "sleep" phase: the model periodically pauses, compresses recent memories into compact fast weights, then clears its short-term cache — just like humans consolidate memories during sleep. • The sleep phase runs multiple recurrent passes over accumulated context, allowing deeper reasoning without slowing down real-time responses. • More sleep (more passes N) leads to better performance, especially on tasks requiring multi-step reasoning.
🧪 实验
内容		 作者在三类任务上进行了测试：（1）受控合成任务——元胞自动机模拟和多跳图检索，需要追踪模式或跨多步链接信息；（2）需要长推理链的数学推理任务。基线模型包括标准Transformer和SSM-注意力混合模型。评估指标为不同上下文长度和睡眠时长（N=1,2,4,8等）下的任务准确率。 The authors tested on three types of tasks: (1) controlled synthetic tasks — cellular automata simulations and multi-hop graph retrieval, which require tracking patterns or chaining information across many steps; (2) a realistic math reasoning task requiring long chains of logic. Baselines included standard Transformer models and SSM-attention hybrid models. Performance was measured by task accuracy at varying context lengths and sleep durations (N = 1, 2, 4, 8, etc.).
📊 实验
结果		 在多跳图检索任务上，睡眠模型显著优于标准Transformer和SSM-注意力混合模型（后两者基本失败）。在数学推理任务上，睡眠轮数从N=1增加到N=8，准确率持续提升，在最难的深度推理样本上提升幅度最大。标准Transformer和混合基线在这些挑战性长上下文任务上准确率接近零，而睡眠模型取得了随N扩展的有效性能。 On multi-hop graph retrieval, the sleep model significantly outperformed standard Transformers and SSM-attention hybrids, which both effectively failed on the task. On the math reasoning benchmark, increasing sleep passes from N=1 to N=8 consistently improved accuracy, with the largest gains (reported as meaningful percentage-point improvements) on the hardest, deepest-reasoning examples. Standard Transformer and hybrid baselines showed near-zero accuracy on these challenging long-context tasks, while the sleep model achieved non-trivial performance that scaled with N.
🏁 结论 本文证明，给AI模型加入"睡眠"阶段——在清空缓存前压缩和巩固记忆——能让模型处理标准模型完全无法应对的长程复杂推理任务，且性能随睡眠时长增加而持续提升。 This paper proves that giving AI models a "sleep" phase — where they compress and consolidate memory before clearing their cache — enables them to handle long, complex reasoning tasks that completely defeat standard models, with performance that improves as sleep duration increases.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

模型在两种模式间交替工作："清醒"模式（正常回答问题）和"睡眠"模式（整理记忆）。睡眠期间，模型把最近读到的内容反复循环处理（就像反复复习笔记），把关键信息压缩进一组特殊的"快速权重"——相当于一本压缩笔记本。睡眠结束后，庞大的短期缓存被清空，让模型在清醒时保持轻盈快速。睡眠时循环的次数（N）越多，模型对复杂多步问题的理解越深入。 The model works in two alternating modes: "wake" (answering questions normally) and "sleep" (consolidating memory). During sleep, it takes everything it has recently read, runs over it multiple times in a loop (like re-reading your notes), and squeezes the key information into a special set of fast-updating weights inside state-space model (SSM) blocks. These fast weights act like a compressed notebook. Once sleep is done, the bulky short-term cache is wiped clean, keeping the model lean and fast during wake time. The more times the model loops over the context during sleep (controlled by N), the better it understands complex, multi-step problems.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{FastWeights}_{t+1} = \text{FastWeights}_t + \Delta W(\text{context}_t)\]
💡 睡眠期间，模型通过对当前上下文运行一个可学习的本地更新规则，将新信息累积进快速权重（相当于压缩笔记本）。 During sleep, the model accumulates new information into its fast weights (the compressed notebook) by applying a learned local update rule over the current context.
\[N \text{ offline recurrent passes over accumulated context}\]
💡 睡眠阶段对积累的上下文进行N次离线循环处理，N越大，记忆压缩越深入，推理能力越强。 The sleep phase runs N loops over the stored context; the more loops (larger N), the deeper the memory consolidation and the better the reasoning.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着AI助手被用于越来越长、越来越复杂的任务（如阅读整份法律文件、调试大型代码库或解决多步数学题），这项研究提供了一条让AI更快更聪明且不爆内存的实用路径。它还与人类大脑利用睡眠巩固学习的机制惊人相似，表明受生物启发的设计可能是下一代AI的关键。 As AI assistants are used for longer and more complex tasks — like reading entire legal documents, debugging large codebases, or solving multi-step math problems — this research offers a practical path to making them faster and smarter without exploding memory costs. It also draws a fascinating parallel to how human brains use sleep to consolidate learning, suggesting bio-inspired designs could be key to next-generation AI.

HumanEgo: Zero-Shot Robot Learning from Minutes of Human Egocentric Videos HumanEgo：从几分钟人类第一视角视频中实现零样本机器人学习

🧑‍🔬 Zhi, Wang, Botao He, Kelin Yu et al.

图 Figure · Page 3 of paper

🎯 用人类日常操作视频直接训练机器人，无需任何机器人数据
Teach robots new skills using only minutes of human hand-cam video — no robot needed.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像你想教一个外星机器人学做饭，以前必须手把手控制它的机械臂反复练习，而HumanEgo让你只需自己戴着头盔摄像头做一遍，它就能把你的动作"翻译"成机器人语言，机器人看完就会了——不管它长得和你有多不一样。Think of it like teaching a robot to cook by just wearing a GoPro on your head while you cook dinner yourself — instead of having to laboriously remote-control the robot's arms for hours. HumanEgo "translates" what your hands do into a language the robot understands, even though its arms look nothing like yours.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练机器人做任务通常需要大量昂贵的机器人操作数据，要么人工遥控机器人反复示范，要么让机器人自己试错——这既费时又费钱。另外，人手和机器人手臂看起来完全不同，动作方式也不同，所以直接用人类的操作视频来训练机器人是很难的——就像想让机器人看你炒菜的视频就学会炒菜，但它的"手"根本不像你的手。HumanEgo就是为了解决这个"人和机器人之间的鸿沟"而生的。 Training robots to do useful tasks usually requires huge amounts of expensive robot-specific data — someone has to manually remote-control the robot dozens or hundreds of times. The obvious shortcut — just showing the robot videos of humans doing the task — doesn't work well because human hands look and move completely differently from robot arms. This "embodiment gap" makes it hard to transfer human skills to machines.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 无需任何机器人数据：只用普通人戴头戴摄像头拍摄的操作视频就能训练机器人，完全不需要机器人遥操作数据。 • 跨越"形态鸿沟"：将视频中的手部动作和物体交互提炼成一种"通用语言"，让机器人能理解和模仿。 • 极度数据高效：每个任务只需30分钟人类视频，就能达到92.5%的平均成功率。 • 零样本迁移：训练好的策略可以直接迁移到全新的机器人、摄像头和环境中，不需要重新训练。 • Completely robot-data-free: uses only casual head-mounted camera videos of humans, no robot teleoperation needed at all. • Bridges the human-robot gap: converts raw hand movements into a universal "hand-object interaction" representation that any robot can understand. • Remarkably data-efficient: just 30 minutes of human video per task achieves 92.5% average success rate. • True zero-shot transfer: the learned policy works directly on new robots, new cameras, and new environments without retraining.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者在四个真实世界的桌面操作任务上测试了HumanEgo，这些任务涵盖抓取、摆放、拼装等不同难度的操作。他们与多个基线方法进行了对比，包括：直接用机器人遥操作相同时长数据训练的策略、现有的人类视频迁移方法。此外，还测试了HumanEgo在不同机器人平台、不同摄像头角度、不同背景环境下的零样本迁移能力，以验证系统的通用性和鲁棒性。 The researchers tested HumanEgo on four real-world tabletop manipulation tasks of varying difficulty (grasping, stacking, assembling, etc.). They compared it against baselines including: robot teleoperation policies trained with the same amount of time, and existing human-video transfer methods. They also ran zero-shot transfer experiments across different robot hardware, camera viewpoints, and background environments to test how well the system generalizes without any retraining.
📊 实验
结果		 • 仅用30分钟人类视频，HumanEgo在四个真实任务上达到92.5%的平均成功率。 • 仅用15分钟人类视频，平均成功率仍高达75%。 • 与相同时长的机器人遥操作数据训练的方法相比，HumanEgo性能高出41%。 • 在零样本迁移到全新机器人、摄像头和环境时，仍保持强劲性能，验证了系统的广泛适用性。 • With only 30 minutes of human video, HumanEgo achieves 92.5% average success rate across four real-world tasks. • With just 15 minutes of video, it still achieves 75% average success rate. • It outperforms robot teleoperation methods trained with the same amount of data by 41%. • Zero-shot transfer to entirely new robots, cameras, and environments succeeds robustly, demonstrating broad generalizability.
🏁 结论 HumanEgo证明了：只需几十分钟普通人拍摄的第一视角视频，无需任何机器人操作数据，就能训练出成功率超过90%的机器人操作策略，并且可以零样本迁移到全新的机器人和环境。这一成果大幅降低了机器人技能学习的数据门槛，为大规模、低成本的机器人技能获取开辟了新路径。 HumanEgo proves that you can train a highly capable robot manipulation policy — over 90% success rate — using nothing but a few dozen minutes of casual human head-cam video, with zero robot data required, and the policy transfers directly to new robots and settings. This dramatically lowers the barrier to teaching robots new skills at scale.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

HumanEgo分三步解决问题。第一步，用头戴摄像头录下人类完成任务的视频，然后自动提取出"手在哪里、物体在哪里、手指怎么动"等核心信息，把这些信息变成一种与外观无关的"实体级表示"——就像把视频里的细节全部抽象掉，只保留"谁拿着什么、怎么移动"这件事。第二步，用这种抽象表示来训练一个叫做"流匹配策略"的AI模型，同时加入多个辅助学习目标，让模型从每一帧视频中榨取尽可能多的监督信号，哪怕数据很少也能学得很好。第三步，机器人执行任务时，实时感知自己面前的场景，用训练好的策略直接输出动作，不需要任何额外适配。 HumanEgo works in three steps. First, it takes head-cam videos of a human doing a task and automatically extracts the core information — where the hands are, where the objects are, how the fingers move — stripping away all visual appearance details to create an abstract "who's holding what and how" representation. Second, it trains an AI policy using this abstract data, with multiple extra learning signals that squeeze every drop of useful information out of each video frame, making the model learn well even from small datasets. Third, at deployment time, the robot uses this trained policy to act in real time — no extra fine-tuning or adaptation required.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

家用机器人和工业机器人最大的瓶颈之一就是"教它做新事情太贵太麻烦"——以往需要专业人员反复遥控机器人示范。HumanEgo让普通人戴上头戴摄像头操作几十分钟，就能教会机器人新技能，这可能彻底改变机器人技能学习的方式，让家用机器人真正走入千家万户。 One of the biggest roadblocks to useful home and industrial robots is how expensive and tedious it is to teach them new tasks — traditionally requiring specialists to remote-control the robot over and over. HumanEgo means an ordinary person could just wear a head-cam for 30 minutes, and a robot learns the skill — potentially making capable personal robots far more accessible and affordable.

A comprehensive roadmap formalizing how to build AI systems that natively understand and generate text, images, audio, and video in one unified model.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy想象一下，旧式AI就像雇了一群只会各自专长的厨师——一个只炒菜、一个只烤面包、一个只做甜点，上菜时还要互相传递盘子，非常别扭。这篇论文就是一本"大厨养成手册"，教你如何从零开始培养一位能同时处理所有食材、融会贯通的全能大厨，连厨房怎么设计都给你讲清楚了。Think of old AI models like a chef who was trained to only cook one dish — you had to hire separate chefs for steak, salad, and dessert, then awkwardly pass plates between them. 维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现在大多数多模态AI系统就像一个大脑加上外挂的眼睛和耳朵——语言模型被单独训练，再把图像/音频模块"拼接"上去，各部分并不是真正融合在一起的。这种"后期拼接"方式限制了模型真正理解多种信息的能力。如何构建一个从头开始就能同时理解图像、声音、文字，并生成各种内容的统一AI系统，至今没有清晰的设计蓝图。 Today's AI models are often built like Frankenstein's monster — a language brain bolted onto separate eyes and ears that were trained independently. Building a single AI system that natively sees, hears, reads, and speaks all at once — and does so efficiently in real products — remains an open and messy challenge with no clear blueprint.

The authors survey models across tasks including image understanding, video understanding, speech recognition, image generation, and video generation. They reference standard multimodal benchmarks such as MMBench, MMMU, VQAv2, and COCO, comparing early-fusion and mid-fusion native models against traditional late-fusion baselines across these established evaluation suites.
📊 实验
结果		 作为综述与路线图论文，本文并不报告单一新模型的实验数字，而是综合梳理全领域成果：原生多模态模型（早期融合/中期融合）在跨模态推理任务上普遍优于后期拼接方法；顶级统一模型（如GPT-4o同级系统）在MMMU基准上准确率接近70%以上，在MMBench上达到80%以上，同时具备后期融合模型完全不具备的生成能力。论文指出，统一的多对多模型正在缩小理解与生成性能之间的差距。 As a survey and roadmap paper, this work does not report a single set of new benchmark numbers. Instead, it synthesizes findings across the field: native multimodal models (early- and mid-fusion) consistently outperform late-fusion counterparts on cross-modal reasoning tasks, with top unified models (e.g., GPT-4o-class systems) achieving state-of-the-art scores on benchmarks like MMMU (approaching 70%+ accuracy) and MMBench (80%+ range), while also enabling generation capabilities that late-fusion models entirely lack. The paper highlights that unified Multi-to-Multi models close the gap between understanding and generation performance compared to task-specific models.
🏁 结论 本文建立了原生多模态建模的首个正式框架与端到端路线图，证明了深度架构融合是构建真正能跨文本、图像、音频、视频进行理解与生成的AI系统的正确方向。 This paper establishes the first formal framework and end-to-end roadmap for native multimodal modeling, proving that deep architectural integration of modalities is the right direction for building truly capable AI systems that understand and generate across text, image, audio, and video.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

这篇论文不是提出一个新模型，而是充当整个领域的"导航地图"。作者首先精确定义什么叫"原生"多模态——区分早期融合（从一开始就混合所有模态）、中期融合（中途合并）和非原生的后期拼接。接着，他们梳理了大量现有模型，按输入输出类型分成三大类，并提炼出通用设计原则。最后，他们逐一拆解构建这类系统的每个环节：数据筛选、架构选择、训练技巧、高效推理和评估方法，融合了学术研究与工业实践的双重视角。 Rather than proposing a single new model, this paper acts as a field guide. The authors first draw a precise map of what "native" multimodal modeling really means — distinguishing early-fusion (mixing modalities from the very start) and mid-fusion (mixing partway through) from non-native late-fusion. They then survey hundreds of existing models, slot them into a three-way taxonomy based on their input-output behavior, and extract common design principles. ⚙️ Method · 方法详解

MiniMax-M2就像一座拥有2300亿本书的超级图书馆，但每次回答问题时只调用其中约100亿本"专业书籍"，大幅节省资源同时保持高质量——这就是混合专家（MoE）架构的核心思想。为了让模型能完成真实复杂任务，团队构建了专门的训练数据流水线，生成大量有验证的"任务完成轨迹"，比如真实代码执行过程的记录。再通过名为Forge的强化学习系统训练模型：做对了给奖励，做错了给惩罚，即使任务跨越很多步骤也能有效学习。最后还加入了"自我进化"机制，让模型像一个会自查作业的学生，能自主发现并修正训练中的错误。 Think of MiniMax-M2 as a huge library with 230 billion books, but for any given question, only a small "specialist team" of about 10 billion books gets pulled from the shelves — saving enormous effort while keeping quality high. This is the Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture. To teach the model to handle real-world tasks, the team built special training pipelines that generate large amounts of verified, step-by-step "task completion" examples — like showing the AI thousands of coding sessions with real code execution feedback. They then trained the model using a reinforcement learning system called Forge, which rewards the AI when it successfully completes tasks and penalizes failures, even over very long multi-step workflows. Finally, they added a "self-evolution" loop where the model monitors its own training and makes corrections — like a student who reviews their own homework and fixes mistakes without a teacher.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

目前顶尖AI模型的运行成本极高，限制了其普及。MiniMax-M2表明可以用更低成本实现同等能力，有望让更多人和企业用得上强大的AI助手。其自我进化能力更预示着AI系统未来可以自我维护和改进，这对AI的发展方式具有深远意义。 Running top-tier AI models today is extraordinarily expensive, limiting who can afford to use them. MiniMax-M2 shows it's possible to get the same quality at a fraction of the cost, which could make powerful AI assistants accessible to far more people and businesses. Its self-evolution capability also hints at a future where AI systems can maintain and improve themselves — a milestone with profound implications for how AI is developed.
