OPRD: On-Policy Representation Distillation OPRD：在线策略表示蒸馏

🧑‍🔬 Shenzhi Yang, Guangcheng Zhu, Bowen Song, Haobo Wang et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 让小模型从大模型的"内心"而非"嘴巴"学习，更快更省内存
Distill smarter AI students by copying teacher's hidden thinking layers, not just its word outputs.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像学厨艺，传统方法只让徒弟尝老师做的菜（只看最终成品），而OPRD让徒弟站在老师旁边，看他每一步怎么切菜、炒锅、调味——直接学习每道工序的手法，而不只是猜最后端上桌的味道。Imagine learning to cook by only tasting the final dish versus watching the chef's every move — how they chop, season, and stir at each step. OPRD is like giving the student chef a live peek at the master's hands at every stage, instead of just handing them the finished plate to reverse-engineer.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练一个"学生"AI模型来模仿大型"教师"AI模型，传统方法只让学生模仿老师最终输出的词语概率——这就像只看答案抄，却不理解解题过程。当词汇表非常大（比如15万个词）时，这种方式存在大量随机噪声，而且完全浪费了老师内部的丰富推理信息，导致学生再怎么学也追不上老师。 When training a smaller "student" AI to mimic a larger "teacher" AI, existing methods only compare the final word probabilities the teacher produces — like copying just the answer without understanding the working. With huge vocabularies (up to 150,000 words), this introduces a lot of random noise during training. Even worse, it completely throws away all the rich internal reasoning the teacher built up layer by layer, so the student hits a ceiling and can never quite catch up to the teacher.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 创新地将蒸馏从"输出层"提升到"隐藏层"，让学生直接对齐老师内部每一层的表示向量，而不仅仅是最终词概率。 • 从理论上证明这种方法彻底消除了大词汇表带来的采样方差，信号更稳定、更丰富。 • 实际效果：比现有最强的输出空间蒸馏方法快1.44倍，内存消耗减少54%，而且学生模型最终能真正追上老师的成绩。 • Novel idea: instead of matching only the teacher's final word probabilities, align the student's internal hidden states layer-by-layer with the teacher's — learning the thinking process, not just the answers. • Theoretically eliminates the random noise (sampling variance) caused by estimating probabilities over massive vocabularies. • Practical bonus: 1.44× faster training and 54% less memory usage than the best competing method, while actually closing the gap to the teacher's performance.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在数学推理基准上进行评测，主要使用AIME 2024、AIME 2025和AIMO这三个竞赛数学题数据集。基线对比方法包括多种在线策略输出蒸馏（OPD）变体，如标准KL散度蒸馏、top-k OPD等主流方法，以及教师模型本身作为性能上限。评估指标为数学题的答题准确率（pass@1）。 The paper evaluated on challenging math competition benchmarks: AIME 2024, AIME 2025, and AIMO. Competing baselines included standard on-policy KL distillation and top-k on-policy distillation (top-k OPD), which are the current leading output-space distillation methods. The teacher model's performance served as the performance ceiling. The main metric was math problem-solving accuracy (pass@1 — how often the model gets the right answer on the first try).
📊 实验
结果		 在AIME 2024和AIME 2025上，OPRD是唯一能真正缩小与教师模型差距的方法，而所有输出空间OPD基线均无法追上教师，出现"性能天花板"。OPRD比top-k OPD训练速度快1.44倍，GPU内存占用减少54%。在AIMO基准上同样实现了最优学生表现，全面超越输出空间蒸馏方法。 On AIME 2024 and AIME 2025, OPRD was the only method that actually closed the gap between student and teacher model performance, while all output-space OPD baselines plateaued below the teacher. OPRD trained 1.44× faster and used 54% less GPU memory than the top-k OPD baseline. On the AIMO benchmark, OPRD similarly achieved the best student performance, consistently outperforming all output-space distillation competitors.
🏁 结论 本文证明了：将知识蒸馏从输出层提升到隐藏层表示空间，不仅在理论上消除了大词汇表带来的采样噪声，而且在实践中让学生模型首次真正追上教师模型，同时显著提升了训练效率和内存效率。 This paper proves that teaching a student AI model by matching the teacher's internal hidden representations — rather than just its final word outputs — eliminates theoretical noise, enables the student to truly match the teacher's performance on hard math benchmarks, and does so faster and with less memory than previous best methods.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

OPRD让学生和老师在同样的"做题过程"（rollout，即生成的文本序列）上，逐层比较彼此的内部隐藏状态向量，而不是比较最终输出的词概率。这样做完全绕过了词汇表这个"大漏斗"，避免了因词汇量巨大带来的随机估算误差。具体来说，从老师和学生各自选几层对应的隐藏层，用投影矩阵将维度对齐后，最小化两者之间的差距。由于不需要在15万个词上做蒙特卡洛采样估计，训练信号更稳定，计算量也大幅减少。 OPRD takes the same text sequences that the student generates (called "rollouts") and, for each sequence, compares the student's and teacher's internal hidden states at selected matching layers — completely skipping the final vocabulary output step. A small projection layer bridges any size difference between the student and teacher's hidden states. Because you're no longer estimating probabilities across 150,000 words using random sampling (Monte Carlo), the training signal is much cleaner and the math is cheaper to compute. The student essentially learns to "think like the teacher" at each layer, not just produce similar-looking final answers.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\mathcal{L}_{\text{OPRD}} = \sum_{l \in \mathcal{S}} \left\| h_l^{\text{student}} - P_l \cdot h_l^{\text{teacher}} \right\|_2^2\]
💡 OPRD的损失函数：对选定的若干层，计算学生隐藏状态与（经过投影矩阵对齐后的）教师隐藏状态之间的均方误差，驱动学生的内部表示向教师靠拢。 The OPRD loss: for selected layers, measure the squared distance between the student's hidden states and the teacher's hidden states (after a projection to align dimensions), pushing the student to think internally more like the teacher.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

更小、更便宜的AI模型如果能真正追上大模型的水平，将大幅降低AI应用的成本和门槛，让更多人和企业用得起高质量AI。OPRD同时降低了训练成本（更快、更省显存），意味着未来训练高质量小模型的资源门槛会进一步降低。 Making smaller, cheaper AI models that actually match the performance of giant expensive ones is a huge deal — it means powerful AI can run on less hardware and reach more people. OPRD also slashes the cost of the training process itself, which could speed up research and make high-quality AI more accessible globally.

Trajectory-Refined Distillation 轨迹精炼蒸馏

🧑‍🔬 Li Jiang, Haoran Xu, Yichuan Ding, Amy Zhang

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 从轨迹层面纠正学生模型错误，突破逐词蒸馏的瓶颈
TRD fixes a root flaw in AI student-teacher training by correcting entire reasoning paths, not just individual word mistakes.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像学生跟着老师学解题，如果第一步就抄错了，后面逐字纠错根本没用。TRD就像老师说："等等，我们从这个正确的第一步重新来过"——把整条解题路径重新走一遍，而不是在错误的路上修修补补。Think of it like a student copying a math solution from a tutor — if the student misreads the first line and copies the rest wrong, correcting only individual arithmetic steps won't help. TRD is like the tutor saying "Wait, let's restart from this better first step" — fixing the whole path, not just individual words.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 在用大模型（老师）来训练小模型（学生）时，如果学生一开始就走错了方向，后续每一步都会跟着错。现有的方法只是逐字逐词地纠错，就像火车已经出轨了，却还在逐个调整车轮——根本没用。这种"前缀失败"问题导致训练信号混乱，学生模型很难真正学到老师的推理方式。 When training a smaller "student" AI model by having it learn from a smarter "teacher" model, a sneaky problem arises: if the student makes a wrong turn early in its reasoning chain, every single step after that is also wrong — but the training method still tries to fix each word one by one, which is like trying to fix a derailed train by adjusting individual wheels. Existing on-policy distillation methods give feedback at each token (word piece), but this token-level fix can't recover from a fundamentally broken starting point — called "prefix failure" — where the student and teacher are essentially talking past each other from the very beginning.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次揭示"前缀失败"是现有逐词蒸馏崩溃的根本结构性原因，提供统一的理论解释。 • 提出TRD方法，在轨迹层面（整条推理路径）进行纠正，从根源解决问题而非逐词打补丁。 • 即使学生原本答对了，TRD也能通过展示老师的其他正确路径来拓宽学生的探索空间。 • 自然扩展到自蒸馏场景，学生模型可利用特权信息充当自己的老师。 • Identifies "prefix failure" as the root structural cause of why token-level on-policy distillation breaks down — a novel and unifying diagnosis. • Proposes TRD, which operates at the trajectory (full reasoning path) level, correcting wrong prefixes before applying teacher supervision rather than patching them word-by-word. • Also improves cases where the student was already correct, by showing it alternative valid reasoning paths from the teacher. • Extends naturally to self-distillation settings where the student uses privileged information to act as its own teacher.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者在多个数学和推理基准上测试了TRD，包括MATH500、AMC、AIME等。对比基线包括标准在策略蒸馏方法和逐词重加权方法。实验使用了不同规模的基础模型（如Qwen系列），评估指标包括单次尝试准确率（pass@1）和更广泛的推理覆盖率（pass@k），以全面衡量模型的精准性和解题多样性。 The researchers tested TRD across a wide range of math and reasoning benchmarks, including MATH500, AMC, AIME, and others. They compared TRD against multiple strong baselines in on-policy distillation, including standard OPD methods and token-level reweighting approaches. Experiments were run using several base models at different scales (e.g., Qwen and other LLM families), and both single-attempt accuracy (pass@1) and broader reasoning coverage (pass@k) were measured to capture both precision and diversity of correct solutions.
📊 实验
结果		 TRD在所有测试基准和模型规模上均优于现有在策略蒸馏基线。在MATH500上，pass@1准确率相比标准OPD有显著提升。在更难的竞赛级基准（如AIME）上，TRD在单次准确率和pass@k覆盖率上均有明显增益。该方法在多个模型系列和规模上表现一致，具有良好的泛化性。与最强基线相比，TRD在大多数基准上领先数个百分点，在多步推理难题上优势尤为突出。 TRD consistently outperforms prior on-policy distillation baselines across all tested benchmarks and model scales. On MATH500, TRD achieves notable improvements in pass@1 accuracy over standard OPD. On harder competition-level benchmarks like AIME, TRD shows meaningful gains in both single-attempt accuracy and pass@k coverage. The method works across multiple model families and sizes, demonstrating broad generalizability. Specific reported gains place TRD ahead of the next-best baseline by several percentage points on most benchmarks, with the advantage being especially pronounced on harder, multi-step reasoning tasks.
🏁 结论 TRD证明了在整条推理路径层面纠正错误，比逐词打补丁的方式从根本上更有效，为利用大模型指导小模型推理能力训练树立了新的最佳实践。 TRD proves that fixing reasoning errors at the trajectory (whole-path) level is fundamentally more effective than patching them word-by-word, establishing a new best practice for training smaller AI models to reason better using guidance from larger ones.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

TRD的做法是：让学生模型先生成一条推理路径，如果发现开头就走偏了，就引入老师模型对整条路径进行修正，生成一个"修正版"的完整推理链。这个修正后的路径依然贴近学生自己的风格（在策略支撑范围内），所以学到的东西既准确又实用。不是逐字打补丁，而是直接把有问题的开头换掉，用老师引导的替代路径重新训练学生。即使学生原本就答对了，TRD也会让它看到老师的其他正确做法，拓宽思维。 TRD works by letting the student model start a reasoning chain, and then — when something goes wrong early on — it brings in the teacher model to steer the path back on track at the trajectory level, producing a revised, corrected version of the full reasoning chain. This corrected trajectory stays within the student's own "comfort zone" (on-policy support), so the learning signal is both accurate and relevant. Instead of hoping token-level fixes can repair a broken chain, TRD replaces bad prefixes wholesale with teacher-guided alternatives, then trains the student on these cleaner trajectories. Even when the student's original chain was correct, TRD exposes it to the teacher's alternative valid derivations, broadening what the student has seen and can do.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

让小模型尽量接近大模型的推理能力，是让AI真正落地应用的关键——TRD让这一目标更近了一步。更好的知识蒸馏意味着更聪明的AI助手、编程助手和学习工具，而且不需要昂贵的算力就能运行。 This matters because making smaller, cheaper AI models reason as well as giant expensive ones is a key goal for deploying AI in the real world — TRD gets us meaningfully closer to that goal. Better distillation means smarter AI assistants, coding helpers, and tutoring tools that run on everyday hardware without needing massive compute.

FlashMemory-DeepSeek-V4: Lightning Index Ultra-Long Context via Lookahead Sparse Attention FlashMemory-DeepSeek-V4：通过前瞻稀疏注意力实现超长上下文的闪电级索引

🧑‍🔬 Yan Wang, Qifan Zhang, Jiachen Yu, Tian Liang et al.

图 Figure · Page 1 of paper

🎯 预测式稀疏注意力将超长上下文KV缓存压缩至13.5%，精度不降反升
A lookahead sparse attention system slashes LLM memory usage to 13.5% for ultra-long contexts without losing accuracy.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个超级聪明的图书管理员，在你开口提问之前就已经猜到你需要哪几本书，提前把它们摆到你桌上——其他几千本书仍然安静地待在书架上，既不占你的桌面，也不干扰你的思路。Imagine a librarian who can read your mind — before you even finish asking a question, she's already pulled the three most relevant books from a warehouse of thousands and placed them on your desk. You get exactly what you need, without drowning in a pile of every book ever written.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 大型AI语言模型（比如ChatGPT这类）在处理超长文章或超长对话时，需要把所有历史内容都存在GPU显存里，就像你读一本厚书时必须把每一页都摊开放在桌上才能回答问题——显存很快就被撑爆了。当文本长度达到50万个字符时，显存占用极其夸张，导致服务器成本飙升、响应速度变慢，严重限制了AI在长文档分析、超长对话等实际场景中的应用。 Modern AI language models need to keep a record of everything they've read — called a "KV cache" — in expensive GPU memory while answering questions. For very long documents (think 500,000 words), this record gets so huge it barely fits, making the AI extremely slow and costly to run. The problem gets worse as documents get longer, acting like a hard wall that prevents AI from handling book-length or archive-length inputs efficiently.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首创"前瞻"机制：不是被动存储所有历史信息，而是主动预测接下来生成时真正需要哪些内容，提前只保留"有用的"记忆块 • 无需加载巨型主模型即可训练索引器：把记忆索引器设计成独立的双编码器，训练时完全不需要调用数百亿参数的大模型，大幅降低训练成本 • "少即是多"哲学：只保留13.5%的关键信息反而能让模型在长文本任务上更准确，去掉噪声干扰反而提升了推理质量 • Proactive prediction: Instead of blindly keeping everything, the system forecasts which past information will actually be needed next — storing only the essential "memory chunks" • Training without the giant model: The memory indexer is trained as a standalone lightweight system, never needing to load the massive backbone AI, saving enormous compute costs • "Less is more" effect: Keeping only 13.5% of the memory actually makes answers slightly more accurate by eliminating noisy, irrelevant context
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在三个主流长文本评测套件上进行：LongBench-v2（综合长文本理解）、LongMemEval（长期记忆对话）和RULER（超长上下文检索）。基线对比对象是使用完整KV缓存的全量注意力机制，以及其他稀疏注意力方法。测试场景包括从普通长度一直扩展到50万token的极端超长文本，重点评估显存占用量和下游任务准确率两个维度。 The team tested on three major long-context benchmarks: LongBench-v2 (general long-document comprehension), LongMemEval (multi-turn conversations requiring long-term memory), and RULER (ultra-long context retrieval tasks). They compared against the standard full-KV-cache baseline and other sparse attention methods, testing across context lengths ranging from typical to extreme 500K-token inputs, measuring both GPU memory usage and task accuracy.
📊 实验
结果		 • KV缓存平均压缩至完整基线的仅13.5%（节省86.5%显存） • 下游任务准确率平均反而提升+0.6%（绝对值），在多个基准上持平或超越全量注意力 • 在极端50万token场景下，物理KV缓存开销压缩超过90%，主模型核心推理能力保持稳定 • 在LongBench-v2、LongMemEval、RULER三大评测中均达到上述效果 • KV cache compressed to just 13.5% of the full-context baseline on average — an 86.5% memory reduction • Downstream task accuracy improved by +0.6% absolute on average, matching or beating the full-attention baseline • At extreme 500K-token scale, physical KV cache overhead is cut by over 90% with no collapse in reasoning ability • These results hold consistently across LongBench-v2, LongMemEval, and RULER benchmarks
🏁 结论 本文证明了通过主动预测未来所需的记忆内容，AI模型可以用不到14%的显存开销处理超长文本，同时精度不降反升。这为超长上下文大模型推理提供了一条高效、实用、低成本的新路径。 This paper proves that an AI can handle extremely long texts using less than 14% of the usual memory — and actually become slightly more accurate — by intelligently predicting which parts of history it needs ahead of time. It offers a practical, cost-effective blueprint for deploying long-context AI at scale.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

核心思路是在AI生成每个词之前，先用一个轻量级的"记忆索引器"预测：接下来最可能用到的历史信息是哪些？然后只把这些关键内容放入GPU显存参与计算，其余的全部暂时搁置。这个索引器被设计成标准的"双编码器"结构——就像搜索引擎一样，把"问题"和"历史片段"分别编码，用相似度匹配找出最相关的记忆块。最妙的是，这个索引器可以完全独立于主模型进行训练，用标准的检索训练框架即可，完全不需要把几百亿参数的大模型加载进显存，极大降低了开发门槛。 Before generating each new word, a small, fast "memory indexer" model acts like a smart librarian — it looks ahead and predicts which past chunks of text will actually be relevant, then only loads those into the GPU's working memory. The indexer is built like a search engine: it encodes the current question and all past text chunks separately, then scores them for relevance to find the best matches. Because the indexer is completely separate from the giant main AI model, it can be trained cheaply and independently using standard search/retrieval training techniques — no need to ever fire up the enormous backbone model during training.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{KV Cache Ratio} = \frac{|\mathcal{C}_{\text{selected}}|}{|\mathcal{C}_{\text{full}}|} \approx 13.5\%\]
💡 所选关键KV块的数量仅占全量KV缓存的13.5%，即节省了约86.5%的显存 The number of selected KV chunks is only 13.5% of the full cache — meaning 86.5% of memory is freed up without losing accuracy

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

超长上下文处理是AI落地法律文书分析、医疗档案理解、超长代码库问答等高价值场景的核心瓶颈，本工作将显存需求压缩90%意味着同样的硬件可以服务更多用户、处理更长文档，直接降低AI服务成本。更重要的是，"训练不需要加载大模型"这一设计让中小团队也能在顶级大模型之上构建高效推理系统，降低了行业门槛。 Handling very long texts is a key bottleneck for real-world AI applications like legal document review, medical records analysis, and large codebase assistance — cutting memory by 90% means the same hardware can serve far more users at much lower cost. The training approach, which never requires loading the full giant model, also democratizes long-context AI development for smaller teams and companies.

🎯 让机器人"慢想快动"，分离世界预测与动作执行实现高速精准操控
A faster, smarter robot brain that separates "thinking ahead" from "acting now" for real-time manipulation.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像足球队里的教练和球员：教练（世界规划器）慢慢观察全场、制定战术并记录在战术板上，而球员（动作执行器）在场上快速移动，随时查阅教练的战术板做决策——而不是每次传球前都等教练重新分析一遍全场录像。Think of it like a sports team with a coach and players: the coach (world planner) watches the whole game slowly and builds a strategy, while the players (action executor) move fast on the field using the coach's playbook — without waiting for the coach to re-analyze the entire game before every single pass.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的机器人控制AI模型把"预测未来画面"和"执行动作"绑定在同一个速度节奏上——就像要求一个棋手在思考每一步棋的同时，以完全相同的速度移动棋子。这样做导致视觉预测模块把大量算力浪费在每帧微小的变化上，而且整个系统运行太慢，无法满足实时机器人控制的需求。此外，现有方法只能预测短期未来，难以进行长远规划。 Current AI models for robot control think and act at the same speed — like forcing a chess player to move pieces at the exact same moment they're planning their strategy. This means the "thinking" part wastes time on tiny, unimportant scene changes every single step, and the whole system runs too slowly for real-time robot use. Existing world-action models are also stuck predicting only what happens in the near future, missing the bigger picture of long-horizon planning.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 打破"同步节奏"束缚：世界规划模块低频慢想（宏观规划），动作执行模块高频快动（实时控制），这一非对称设计是核心创新。 • 引入滚动键值记忆机制，让慢速规划器的"智慧"可以被存储和复用，无需每步重新计算。 • 设计了"观测引导视频上下文路由"（OVCR）机制，让快速动作模块根据机器人当前实际观测，智能选取最相关的世界上下文，确保决策贴近现实。 • Breaks the "same speed" constraint: the world-planning brain runs slowly (big-picture thinking) while the action brain runs fast (real-time execution) — a key insight no prior model exploited. • Introduces a rolling memory system so the slow planner's insights are stored and reused without recomputing from scratch every step. • A clever "routing" mechanism (OVCR) lets the fast action brain pick the most relevant world-context based on what the robot actually sees right now, keeping decisions grounded in reality.
🧪 实验
内容		 作者在RoboTwin（标准机器人操控仿真基准，包含多种任务）和4个真实世界机器人操控任务上测试了AHA-WAM。对比基线包括Fast-WAM、RDT、π0等多个最先进模型。核心评估指标为任务成功率（机器人是否完成任务）和控制频率（系统每秒运行多少次，单位Hz）。值得注意的是，整个训练过程没有使用任何机器人数据预训练。 The authors tested AHA-WAM on RoboTwin, a standard robot manipulation simulation benchmark with diverse tasks, and on 4 real-world robot manipulation tasks (physical robot arm experiments). They compared against multiple state-of-the-art baselines including Fast-WAM, RDT, π0, and other world-action model variants. The key metrics were task success rate (did the robot complete the task?) and control frequency (how fast does the system run in Hz). Importantly, no robot-data pretraining was used — the model learned from scratch on task data only.
📊 实验
结果		 AHA-WAM在RoboTwin基准上取得了92.80%的平均任务成功率，无需任何机器人数据预训练即达到最先进水平。在4个真实世界操控任务上，平均成功率达78.3%。速度方面，系统以24.17 Hz的频率运行闭环控制，比之前的Fast-WAM基线快4.59倍——这意味着机器人每秒可做出近25次决策，同时保持高精准度。 AHA-WAM achieved 92.80% average task success rate on the RoboTwin benchmark — a state-of-the-art result without any robot-data pretraining. On 4 real-world manipulation tasks, it reached 78.3% average success rate. In terms of speed, it runs at 24.17 Hz closed-loop control, which is 4.59× faster than the previous Fast-WAM baseline. This means the robot can make nearly 25 decisions per second while maintaining high accuracy.
🏁 结论 本文证明了将世界预测和动作执行解耦到不同时间尺度——让机器人"慢想快动"——能够同时大幅提升性能和速度。AHA-WAM在无需大规模机器人预训练数据的情况下创造了新的最优水平，表明更聪明的架构设计可以弥补数据上的不足。 This paper proves that decoupling world prediction and action execution into different time scales — letting the robot "think slow, act fast" — dramatically improves both performance and speed. AHA-WAM sets a new state of the art in robot manipulation without needing large-scale robot pretraining data, showing that smarter architecture design can compensate for data limitations.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

AHA-WAM使用两个独立的AI"大脑"，均基于扩散Transformer（DiT）架构。第一个是低频"世界规划器"，负责观察场景、积累长期记忆，就像一位时刻关注全局的教练。第二个是高频"动作执行器"，负责处理机器人每时每刻的具体动作，并在需要时向教练的记忆库查询指导。为了让两个大脑异步工作，系统引入了视界自适应偏移训练（让动作大脑适应不同时间偏移的上下文）和OVCR路由机制（让动作大脑根据当前观测，智能提取最相关的已存储记忆，无需每步都唤醒慢速规划器）。 AHA-WAM uses two separate AI "brains" built on Diffusion Transformer (DiT) architecture. The first is a slow, low-frequency "world planner" that watches the scene and builds a long-term memory of how things are evolving — like a coach watching the whole game. The second is a fast, high-frequency "action executor" that handles moment-to-moment robot movements by querying the coach's memory whenever it needs guidance. To make these two brains work asynchronously, the system uses horizon-adaptive offset training (teaching the action brain to work with context from different time offsets) and OVCR routing (letting the action brain pick the most relevant stored memories based on what the robot currently observes, without needing to wake up the slow planner every single step).

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\hat{a}_{t} = \text{ActionDiT}\left(a_t^{\text{noisy}},\, C_{\text{world}}^{\text{routed}},\, o_t\right)\]

\[C_{\text{world}} = \text{RollingKV}\left(\{o_{t-k}, \ldots, o_{t}\}\right)\]
