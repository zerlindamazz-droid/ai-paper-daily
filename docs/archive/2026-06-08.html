OPRD: On-Policy Representation Distillation OPRD：在线策略表示蒸馏

🧑‍🔬 Shenzhi Yang, Guangcheng Zhu, Bowen Song, Haobo Wang et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 让小模型直接学习大模型的"内部思维"，而非模仿其输出
Distill smarter by teaching small models to mimic a big model's hidden thoughts, not just its word choices.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像学厨艺，普通徒弟只能尝到师傅做好的菜（模仿输出）；而OPRD让徒弟站在师傅旁边，一步步观察每个烹饪动作（对齐中间层），自然学得更快、更扎实，还省了食材！Think of it like learning to cook: most students only taste the final dish and try to copy it. OPRD is like standing next to the chef and watching every single step — how they chop, season, and stir — so you learn the real technique, not just the end result. And you waste way less ingredients doing it!
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练小型AI模型时，通常让它模仿大模型的"输出"——即每个词的概率分布，就像只让学生抄老师的答案，而不理解解题过程。这种方式存在两个问题：一是大词汇表（如15万词）带来的随机噪声使训练不稳定；二是大模型内部的"思考过程"完全被丢弃，白白浪费了宝贵的知识。 When training a smaller AI model to learn from a larger one, the usual approach is to make the small model copy the big model's word probabilities — like a student who only copies answers without understanding how to solve the problem. This has two weaknesses: the huge vocabulary (150,000+ words) makes the training noisy and unstable, and all the rich "thinking steps" happening inside the big model's layers are thrown away entirely.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 创新点1：跳过输出层，直接对齐大小模型的"中间隐藏状态"——相当于让学生学老师的解题思路，而不只是答案。 • 创新点2：从根本上消除了大词汇表带来的采样噪声，理论上更稳定。 • 创新点3：训练速度提升1.44倍，内存占用减少54%，实用性极强。 • 创新点4：在数学竞赛类推理任务上，小模型最终追上了大模型的表现，而传统方法会陷入瓶颈。 • Novel idea: Align the student and teacher's internal hidden layers instead of just matching output word probabilities — learning the "thinking process," not just the answer. • Eliminates sampling noise from huge vocabularies at its root, making training theoretically more stable. • 1.44x faster training and 54% less memory usage compared to top-k output distillation. • The student model actually closes the gap to the teacher on hard math benchmarks, where prior methods plateau.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在数学竞赛类推理任务上展开，使用了AIME 2024、AIME 2025和AIMO等高难度数学基准测试。基线对比方法包括标准在线策略蒸馏（OPD）及其top-k变体。评估指标为数学题解答的准确率，并同时比较训练速度和内存占用。 Experiments focused on hard mathematical reasoning benchmarks: AIME 2024, AIME 2025, and AIMO. The baselines were standard on-policy distillation (OPD) and its top-k variant. The main metric was accuracy on competition-level math problems. Training speed (iterations per second) and GPU memory usage were also measured to evaluate practical efficiency.
📊 实验
结果		 • OPRD在AIME 2024/2025和AIMO上成功缩小了学生与教师模型之间的差距，而标准OPD基线则在低于教师模型的水平上停滞不前。 • 训练速度比top-k OPD快1.44倍。 • 内存使用比top-k OPD减少54%。 • 学生模型最终在基准测试上接近或追平教师模型的得分，是传统输出空间蒸馏无法做到的。 • OPRD closes the student-teacher performance gap on AIME 2024, AIME 2025, and AIMO, while standard OPD baselines plateau below the teacher's scores. • Training is 1.44x faster than top-k OPD. • Memory usage is reduced by 54% compared to top-k OPD. • The student model's final benchmark scores approach or match the teacher's, a result that output-space distillation methods fail to achieve.
🏁 结论 本文证明了将知识蒸馏从"输出层"提升到"隐藏层"不仅在理论上消除了采样噪声，在实践中也让小模型真正追上了大模型的推理性能，同时大幅提升了训练效率。 This paper proves that distilling knowledge through a model's internal hidden layers — rather than its output probabilities — eliminates training noise, enables small models to actually match large model performance on hard reasoning tasks, and does so faster and with far less memory.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

OPRD让小模型（学生）和大模型（老师）在同一批生成的文本（rollouts）上运行，然后直接比较它们在选定中间层的"隐藏状态向量"，用均方误差等损失函数拉近两者的差距。这完全绕过了最后的"输出词概率"这一步，因此不受词汇表大小的影响。整个对齐过程在网络的多个层同时进行，让学生能从老师的多层"思维"中学习，而非仅从最终答案中学习。 OPRD runs both the small (student) and large (teacher) models on the same generated text samples. Instead of comparing their final word-probability outputs, it compares their internal "hidden state" vectors at several chosen middle layers — think of it as comparing two students' scratch work, not just their final answers. A standard distance loss pulls the student's internal states closer to the teacher's. Because this comparison happens before the final vocabulary projection step, there's no noise from huge word lists, and the student absorbs richer, layer-by-layer structural knowledge from the teacher.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

让小模型更高效地学习大模型的能力，意味着我们可以用更低的成本部署高性能AI，使强大的推理能力惠及更多用户和设备。这一方法对于在手机、边缘设备上运行高质量AI模型具有重要的现实意义。 Making small models truly match large ones more efficiently means powerful AI reasoning can be deployed cheaply on phones and edge devices, not just expensive servers. This could democratize access to high-quality AI reasoning for everyday applications and lower the cost of running capable AI systems at scale.

MMAE: A Massive Multitask Audio Editing Benchmark MMAE：大规模多任务音频编辑基准

🧑‍🔬 Ziyang Ma, Ruiqi Yan, Ruiyang Xu, Jie Fang et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 首个全面评估AI音频编辑能力的大规模多任务基准
The first comprehensive benchmark for evaluating AI-powered instruction-based audio editing across diverse real-world tasks.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像给AI音频编辑器设计了一套"驾照考试"。在MMAE出现之前，测试AI就像让人不经考试直接上路——完全不知道它能不能应对复杂路况。现在终于有了有评分标准的正式考试，结果发现大多数AI"司机"在第一个路口就翻车了。Think of it like the first-ever standardized driving test for AI audio editors. Before MMAE, it was like letting anyone drive without any exam — you had no idea if the AI could handle a simple straight road or a complicated roundabout. Now there's finally a real test with clear grading, and it turns out most AI "drivers" crash at the first intersection.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 AI音频编辑技术发展很快，已能根据文字指令修改声音、语音和音乐，但目前没有一个全面的"考试题库"来评测这些模型到底有多好。现有的测试范围太窄，要么只测一种音频类型，要么只测简单操作，研究者根本无法准确比较不同模型的优劣，也不知道它们在哪些地方会出问题。 AI audio editing is booming — models can now edit sounds, speech, and music based on text instructions — but there's no good "test" to measure how well they actually work. Existing tests are narrow, covering only one type of audio or simple tasks, so researchers have no reliable way to compare models or find where they fail.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次将7种音频模态（音效、语音、音乐及其混合）、6级任务复杂度、8种操作类型整合到单一基准中，覆盖范围前所未有。 • 创新性地采用"评分细则"框架，把每个自由形式的编辑任务拆解成17,741个可验证的小标准，像老师的详细评分表一样，实现精准多维评估。 • 通过人机协作精心构建2,000个高保真样本，质量远超现有基准。 • 揭示惊人发现：即便是最先进的AI音频编辑模型，在复杂混合模态任务上的精确匹配率竟为绝对0%，暴露出巨大的技术瓶颈。 • First benchmark to cover 7 audio modalities (sound effects, speech, music, and mixtures), 6 levels of task complexity, and 8 operation types — all in one place. • Uses a clever "rubric-based" scoring system that breaks each free-form editing task into small, checkable criteria (like a teacher's detailed marking scheme), enabling precise, multi-dimensional grading. • Built through human-agent collaboration, producing 2,000 high-quality samples with 17,741 verifiable sub-criteria — far more rigorous than prior benchmarks. • Exposes a shocking finding: even the best current AI audio editors fail nearly all complex tasks (0% exact match on mixed-modality tasks).
🧪 实验
内容		 研究人员使用MMAE基准对多个主流AI音频编辑模型进行了全面评测。基准覆盖7种音频模态、6个复杂度级别（从基础单次编辑到多跳推理和多轮对话编辑）以及8种操作类型。评估同时采用评分细则框架（逐一核查全部17,741条标准）和精确匹配率（EMR）指标——即模型输出是否完美满足某任务的所有评分标准。 The researchers evaluated several leading AI audio editing models using the MMAE benchmark. The benchmark covers 7 audio modality types, 6 complexity levels (from basic single edits to multi-hop reasoning and multi-round dialogue editing), and 8 operation types. Evaluation used both the rubric-based scoring system (checking 17,741 individual criteria across all samples) and the Exact Match Rate (EMR) metric, which checks whether a model's output perfectly satisfies all criteria for a given task.
📊 实验
结果		 在所有被测模型中，精确匹配率（EMR，即模型完美完成任务所有要求的概率）平均始终低于5%。在复杂的混合模态任务上（如同时包含语音、音乐和音效的音频编辑），EMR降至绝对0%，即没有任何模型能成功完成哪怕一次。这一结果在所有主流模型上普遍成立，表明当前AI音频编辑系统距离可靠、实用的商业应用还相差甚远。 Across all tested models, the Exact Match Rate (EMR) — meaning the model perfectly nailed every requirement of a task — consistently stayed below 5% on average. On complex mixed-modality tasks (e.g., editing audio that contains speech, music, and sound effects together), the EMR dropped to an absolute 0%, meaning no model succeeded even once. These numbers hold across all leading systems evaluated, confirming that current AI audio editors are far from reliable or production-ready for complex real-world use cases.
🏁 结论 MMAE是首个全面、标准化的AI音频编辑基准，揭示了即便是当前最先进的模型在复杂任务上也几乎完全失败——混合模态编辑的成功率为0%。它为整个领域提供了清晰的诊断工具和评估标准，为未来技术进步指明方向。 MMAE is the first comprehensive, standardized benchmark for AI audio editing, revealing that even the best current models fail catastrophically on complex tasks — with 0% success on mixed-modality edits. It provides the field with a clear diagnostic tool and evaluation standard to guide future improvements.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队为AI音频编辑器设计了一套庞大而精细的"考试体系"。首先，他们绘制了一张结构化的任务地图，涵盖所有类型的音频编辑任务（例如添加背景音乐、改变说话者口音、将语音与音效混合），并按复杂度和操作类型分级。接着，通过人类专家和AI助手协作，制作了2,000道音频编辑题目及标准答案。在评分环节，他们没有简单地判断对错，而是为每道题设计了详细的评分细则：将每个任务拆解成许多具体小标准（如"语音音量是否保持不变？"、"背景噪音是否被正确去除？"），逐条评分，从而全面揭示模型的优势和不足之处。 The team built a huge, carefully organized "exam" for AI audio editors. First, they defined a taxonomy — a structured map of all the different kinds of audio editing tasks (e.g., adding background music, changing a speaker's accent, mixing speech with sound effects), organized by complexity and operation type. Then, using a mix of human expert curation and AI-assisted generation, they created 2,000 audio editing problems with reference solutions. To score the AI's answers, instead of a simple right/wrong check, they designed a rubric system: each task is broken down into many small, specific criteria (like "Did the speech volume stay the same?" or "Was the background noise removed correctly?"), and the AI's output is graded on each criterion separately. This gives a much richer picture of where a model succeeds or stumbles.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{EMR} = \frac{\text{Number of tasks where ALL criteria are satisfied}}{\text{Total number of tasks}} \times 100\%\]
💡 精确匹配率（EMR）：衡量模型完美完成一个任务所有评分细则的比例。只要有一条标准没达到，该任务就不计入分子，因此这是非常严格的评估指标。 Exact Match Rate (EMR): the percentage of tasks where the AI model satisfies every single grading criterion perfectly. If even one small requirement is missed, the whole task counts as failed — making this a very strict measure of performance.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着AI语音助手和音频制作工具逐渐融入日常生活，了解AI能否真正可靠地编辑音频，对开发者和用户都至关重要。MMAE就像AI音频编辑器的"驾照考试"——没有它，我们根本无从判断哪些工具真正安全、可用。 As AI voice assistants and audio production tools become part of everyday life, knowing whether AI can truly edit audio reliably is critical for developers and users alike. MMAE acts like a standardized driving test for AI audio editors — without it, we'd have no way to know which tools are actually safe and capable to use.

dots.tts Technical Report dots.tts 技术报告

🧑‍🔬 Shi Lian, Changtao Li, Bohan Li, Hankun Wang et al.

图 Figure · Page 4 of paper

🎯 20亿参数开源语音合成模型，达到最优克隆质量与超低延迟。
A 2B-parameter open-source TTS model that achieves state-of-the-art voice cloning and speech quality with ultra-low latency.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像培养了一位拥有完美记忆力的顶级配音演员——他永远不会忘记第一幕定下的口音和情绪，听几秒钟就能模仿任何人的声音，而且不到一眨眼的时间就能开口说话。这就是dots.tts。Think of it like training a world-class voice actor who has a perfect memory — they never forget the character's accent or mood from scene one, can mimic any voice after hearing it briefly, and can start delivering their lines in under a tenth of a second. That's dots.tts.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的AI语音合成系统经常产生机械感、前后不一致的声音，在长句子中尤其容易"跑偏"，而且很难准确克隆特定人的声音。此外，这些系统开始生成音频的速度往往很慢，导致语音助手等实时应用体验卡顿、不自然。 Existing AI text-to-speech systems often produce robotic, inconsistent, or drifting speech — especially over long sentences — and struggle to accurately clone a specific person's voice. They also tend to be slow to start generating audio, which makes real-time applications like voice assistants feel laggy and unnatural.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 专门训练的AudioVAE将语音压缩成"智能"空间，让模型更容易预测下一帧声音，同时保留语义和情感。 • 全历史条件机制让模型始终"记得"之前说过的一切，防止声音随时间漂移或前后不一致。 • 自纠正后训练阶段让模型无需人工标注奖励数据，就能自我发现并修正错误。 • CFG感知MeanFlow蒸馏将首包延迟压缩到最低54毫秒——比人类眨眼还快。 • A specially trained AudioVAE compresses speech into a "smart" space that makes future sounds easier to predict, preserving meaning and tone. • Full-history conditioning means the model always "remembers" everything it said before, preventing the voice from drifting or sounding inconsistent. • A self-corrective training stage lets the model catch and fix its own mistakes without needing human-labeled reward data. • CFG-aware MeanFlow distillation slashes the time before the first word is spoken to as little as 54 milliseconds — faster than a human blink.
🧪 实验
内容		 模型在Seed-TTS-Eval标准测试集上进行评估，该测试集包含中文、英文和困难中文三个子集，衡量指标为词错误率（WER，越低越好）和说话人相似度（SIM，越高越好）。此外还在其他公开基准上测试了生成稳定性、声音克隆准确度和情感表达能力。延迟测试分为输出流式和双流式两种推理模式。对比对象为其他领先的开源和商业语音合成系统。 The model was evaluated on Seed-TTS-Eval, a standard benchmark with Chinese, English, and hard Chinese test sets, measuring Word Error Rate (WER, lower is better) and Speaker Similarity (SIM, higher is better). It was also tested across other public benchmarks for stability, voice cloning accuracy, and emotional expressiveness. Latency was measured in both output-streaming and dual-streaming inference modes. Comparisons were made against other leading open-source and commercial TTS systems.
📊 实验
结果		 在Seed-TTS-Eval上，dots.tts取得最佳平均性能：中文/英文/困难中文三个子集的词错误率分别为0.94%/1.30%/6.60%，说话人相似度得分分别为81.0/77.1/79.5，综合排名第一。首包延迟在输出流式模式下为85毫秒，双流式模式下仅54毫秒，实现近乎即时的语音输出。在稳定性、声音克隆和情感表达等多项开源基准测试中均持续领先。 On Seed-TTS-Eval, dots.tts achieves the best average performance: WERs of 0.94% / 1.30% / 6.60% on Chinese / English / hard-Chinese sets, and SIM scores of 81.0 / 77.1 / 79.5 respectively — ranking #1 overall among evaluated systems. First-packet latency reaches 85 ms in output-streaming mode and just 54 ms in dual-streaming mode, enabling near-instant speech output. The model consistently tops open-source leaderboards across stability, voice cloning, and emotional expressiveness benchmarks.
🏁 结论 dots.tts证明，通过精心设计的连续自回归语音合成模型——结合结构化语音空间、长程记忆、自纠正和快速蒸馏——可以同时实现顶级音质、稳健的声音克隆和实时低延迟生成。通过完全开源代码和模型权重，它树立了新的开源最优水准，大幅降低了高质量语音AI实际部署的门槛。 dots.tts proves that a carefully designed continuous autoregressive TTS model — combining a structured speech space, long-range memory, self-correction, and fast distillation — can simultaneously achieve top-tier voice quality, robust cloning, and real-time low-latency generation. By releasing all code and model weights openly, it sets a new open-source state-of-the-art and lowers the barrier for practical, high-quality speech AI deployment.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

首先，团队训练了一个特殊的音频压缩器（AudioVAE），同时优化多个目标——将语音压缩成一个紧凑有序的空间，让含义相似的声音靠近在一起，从而更容易预测下一步。其次，在生成每一段新音频时，模型会把已经生成的所有历史内容都作为条件输入（而非只看短窗口），确保声音风格和语境始终一致。第三，主训练完成后，模型经历自纠正阶段：自己生成样本、发现问题、再训练改进，无需任何人工反馈。最后，通过名为MeanFlow的蒸馏技术，把原本需要多步的生成过程压缩成极少步骤，大幅降低音频开始播放前的延迟。 First, the team built a special audio compressor (AudioVAE) trained with multiple goals at once — it packs speech into a compact, organized space where sounds that mean similar things sit close together, making next-step prediction easier. Second, when generating each new chunk of audio, the model feeds in the entire history of what it has already produced (not just a short window), so it never "forgets" the voice style or context. Third, after the main training is done, the model goes through a self-correction phase: it generates samples, spots where things went wrong, and trains itself to do better — no human feedback needed. Finally, a distillation technique called MeanFlow condenses the slow multi-step generation process into very few steps, dramatically cutting the delay before audio starts streaming.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

高质量、低延迟、开源的语音合成技术，将催生新一代听起来真正像人的语音助手、无障碍辅助工具和内容创作应用，而无需依赖昂贵的商业API。在如此高性能水平上的开源，将加速全球研究进展，让开发者更快地构建更好的语音产品。 High-quality, low-latency, open-source TTS enables a new generation of voice assistants, accessibility tools, and content creation apps that sound genuinely human — without needing expensive proprietary APIs. Open-sourcing a model at this performance level accelerates research and lets developers worldwide build better voice products faster.

🎯 让大模型在隐空间"默想"而非逐字输出推理过程
NF-CoT uses normalizing flows to let LLMs reason in compact continuous hidden states instead of verbose text.

❓ 待解决
的问题		 现在的AI模型（比如ChatGPT）为了答对难题，会先"大声思考"——把每一步推理都写成文字。这就像考试时必须把所有草稿都写在答题纸上，哪怕脑子里的想法还很模糊。这样既浪费时间，又浪费算力，还逼着模型把不成熟的想法硬凑成文字。 Current AI models like ChatGPT improve their answers by "thinking out loud" — writing every reasoning step as words before giving an answer. This is like a student who must write down every scratch calculation, even the half-baked ones, before moving on. This wastes time and tokens, and forces fuzzy or uncertain ideas to be pinned down as specific words before they're ready.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • NF-CoT让模型用紧凑的连续向量"默想"，而不是写出完整的推理文字，大幅降低中间推理开销。 • 借助归一化流这一数学工具，隐藏思维有了精确的概率分布，模型可以像采样文字一样采样"想法"。 • 整个设计与现有大模型架构完全兼容（支持KV缓存、从左到右生成），无需重新设计模型结构。 • Instead of writing reasoning in words, NF-CoT lets the model "think" in compact, continuous hidden vectors — like mental notes rather than full sentences. • It uses a mathematical tool called a normalizing flow to make these hidden thoughts well-defined, probabilistic, and compatible with how modern LLMs already work (KV-cache, left-to-right generation). • Unlike prior latent-reasoning attempts, NF-CoT keeps exact probability scores for its hidden thoughts, enabling proper training and sampling without breaking the model's architecture.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

NF-CoT在语言模型内部加入了一个特殊的"流头"，与正常的文字生成头并排工作。当模型到达推理位置时，流头不输出文字，而是生成一个压缩的隐向量——就像一个"脑内便签"。这个隐向量通过归一化流（一种可逆数学变换）生成，能为任何连续值精确计算概率。文字位置仍走正常语言模型头。两种位置共享同一套从左到右的处理流程，原有的KV缓存等加速机制完全不受影响。训练时先从真实思维链文本中蒸馏出压缩思维，之后模型学会用这些压缩思维提升答题质量，还能在隐空间直接做强化学习微调。 NF-CoT inserts a special "flow head" inside the language model alongside the normal text-generating head. When the model reaches a reasoning position, instead of outputting a word, the flow head generates a dense hidden vector — like a compressed thought. This hidden vector is produced and interpreted using a normalizing flow, a reversible math transformation that can assign exact probabilities to any continuous value. Text positions still use the normal language model head. Both types of positions share the same left-to-right causal processing, so the model's existing speed optimizations (like KV-cache) continue to work. The hidden thoughts are first distilled from real chain-of-thought text during training, then the model learns to use those compressed thoughts to boost answer quality — and can even be fine-tuned further with reinforcement learning directly in the hidden space.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\log p(x) = \log p_Z(f(x)) + \log \left|\det \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right|\]
💡 归一化流的核心公式：通过一个可逆变换f，将复杂分布的对数概率转化为简单基础分布的对数概率加上变换的雅可比行列式对数值，从而精确计算任意隐思维向量的概率。 The core normalizing flow formula: the log-probability of a hidden thought vector x equals the log-probability under a simple base distribution plus a log-determinant correction term, allowing exact probability scores for any continuous hidden thought.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

今天的AI助手在给出答案前要生成大量推理文字，既耗时又耗钱。NF-CoT提供了一种让"内心思考"更快更省的方法，有望让强推理AI在大规模部署时更经济实惠，普通用户也能受益。 AI assistants today burn enormous compute (and your money) generating long intermediate reasoning texts before every answer. NF-CoT shows a way to make that internal thinking faster and cheaper without sacrificing quality — which could make powerful reasoning AI more affordable and accessible at scale.
