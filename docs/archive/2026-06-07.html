Why Muon Outperforms Adam: A Curvature Perspective 为什么Muon优于Adam：一个曲率视角

🧑‍🔬 Shuche Wang, Fengzhuo Zhang, Jiaxiang Li, Dirk Bergemann et al.

🎯 Muon通过走更平坦的方向，比Adam更高效地训练大模型
Muon beats Adam in LLM training by navigating flatter loss landscape directions, cutting curvature penalty in half.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像两个登山者都要从山脚爬到山顶：Adam总是直线冲坡，经常走到陡峭悬崖边上耗费大量体力；而Muon像个有经验的向导，每步都选择坡度更平缓的路线，结果用同样的体力走得更远、更快。Imagine two hikers climbing a mountain in the dark: Adam just charges straight ahead and keeps stumbling over steep cliffs, wasting energy. Muon is like a smart hiker with a terrain sensor — it always picks the gentler slope, so it reaches the top twice as fast using the same total energy.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练大型AI语言模型既耗时又昂贵。研究人员发现一种叫Muon的新优化器（可以理解为"训练引擎"）比广泛使用的Adam快大约两倍——但没人真正搞清楚为什么。不理解背后的原因，就很难信任这个优势，也难以进一步改进或推广它。 Training large AI language models is expensive and slow. Researchers recently found that a newer optimizer called Muon trains models about twice as fast as the widely-used Adam optimizer — but nobody really understood *why*. Without understanding the reason, it's hard to trust, improve, or generalize this advantage to other settings.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次从几何曲率角度严格解释了Muon为何优于Adam，填补了理论空白。 • 提出"归一化方向锐度"（NDS）新指标，精准定位Muon优势的来源。 • 发现训练数据越不均衡，Muon相对Adam的优势越大。 • 通过数学证明：Muon能在陡峭和平缓方向之间均衡分配更新能量，避开"悬崖"。 • First rigorous geometric explanation for why Muon outperforms Adam, using curvature (how bumpy the loss landscape is) as the lens. • Introduces "Normalized Directional Sharpness" (NDS) — a new metric that precisely pinpoints the source of Muon's edge. • Shows that uneven training data (data imbalance) actually amplifies Muon's advantage. • Proves mathematically that Muon balances its update energy across steep and flat directions, avoiding the "sharp cliffs" Adam stumbles on.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在语言模型训练上进行，使用了真实文本数据和可控不均衡程度的合成Zipf-PCFG语法数据。主要对比对象是Muon和Adam两种优化器。评估指标包括单步损失下降、NDS值、更新范数和验证损失曲线。还对模型内部各层进行了层内/跨层曲率分解，找出Muon优势来自模型的哪个部位。理论分析在具有异质曲率的简化二次问题上完成。 Experiments were run on language model training using both real text data and synthetic Zipf-PCFG grammar data with controllable imbalance levels. The two main baselines compared are Muon and Adam. Key evaluation metrics include one-step loss decrease, NDS values, update norms, and validation loss curves. They also conducted within-layer vs. cross-layer decomposition of curvature to pinpoint where Muon's advantage comes from inside the model. Theoretical analysis was performed on stylized quadratic problems with heterogeneous curvature.
📊 实验
结果		 Muon在大语言模型训练中实现了约2倍于Adam的训练效率。在相同验证损失的检查点上，Muon的二阶曲率惩罚始终低于Adam。NDS分解显示Muon和Adam的更新范数相当，说明全部优势来自更低的NDS，而非更大的步长。在Zipf-PCFG实验中，数据不均衡程度越高（Zipf参数越大），Muon与Adam的NDS差距越大。层内曲率分析显示，Muon的NDS优势主要来源于训练中后期更小的层内曲率。理论证明表明，在曲率异质性足够强时，相同步数后Muon能取得严格更低的局部二次损失。 Muon achieves approximately 2× training efficiency over Adam in large language model training. At matched validation loss checkpoints, Muon consistently shows a smaller second-order curvature penalty than Adam. NDS decomposition reveals that Muon's update norms are comparable to Adam's — so the entire advantage comes from lower NDS, not larger steps. In Zipf-PCFG experiments, higher data imbalance (larger Zipf parameter) consistently widens Muon's NDS gap over Adam. Within-layer curvature analysis shows Muon's NDS advantage is mainly driven by smaller within-layer curvature in the middle and late training stages. Theoretical proofs confirm that under sufficiently strong curvature heterogeneity, Muon achieves strictly lower local quadratic loss than gradient descent after the same number of steps.
🏁 结论 本文证明了Muon比Adam快约2倍的训练优势，不是因为走了更大的步子，而是因为它始终选择曲率更低（NDS更小）的方向——即绕开了损失地形中陡峭崎岖的区域。这为解释Muon优越性提供了第一个严格的几何理论，也为设计更好的优化器提供了方向。 This paper proves that Muon's ~2× training speed advantage over Adam stems not from taking bigger steps, but from consistently choosing directions with lower curvature (lower NDS) — meaning it avoids the steep, bumpy parts of the loss landscape. This provides the first rigorous geometric theory explaining Muon's superiority and offers actionable insights for designing even better optimizers.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究者用二阶泰勒展开（相当于用放大镜仔细审视训练损失的"崎岖地形"）来比较两种优化器每步能减少多少损失。他们把比较拆成两部分：优化器前进了多远（一阶增益）vs. 在颠簸区域超调了多少（二阶曲率惩罚）。再进一步把曲率惩罚拆分为更新幅度和一个新指标NDS（步进方向上地形有多陡）。最后用合成语法数据（Zipf-PCFG）做受控实验，并在简化二次问题上给出理论证明。 The researchers use a second-order Taylor expansion — think of it like zooming in with a magnifying glass on the bumpy terrain of the training loss — to compare how much loss each optimizer reduces per step. They break the comparison into two parts: how much progress the optimizer makes (first-order gain) vs. how much it overshoots bumpy regions (second-order curvature penalty). They then decompose the curvature penalty further into update size and a new quantity called NDS (how sharp the landscape is in the direction the optimizer steps). Finally, they run controlled experiments with synthetic grammar data (Zipf-PCFG) to study data imbalance, and prove theoretical results on toy quadratic problems to confirm their intuitions.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\Delta L \approx -\underbrace{\langle \nabla L, \Delta\theta \rangle}_{\text{first-order gain}} + \frac{1}{2}\underbrace{\Delta\theta^\top H \Delta\theta}_{\text{curvature penalty}}\]
💡 用二阶泰勒展开描述每步损失变化：损失下降量 ≈ 一阶增益（梯度方向的进展）减去曲率惩罚（地形有多颠簸）。Muon的优势来自更小的曲率惩罚项。 This formula breaks down how much loss decreases per step: the good part (first-order gain, moving in the right direction) minus the bad part (curvature penalty, how much you overshoot on bumpy terrain). Muon wins by keeping the curvature penalty small.
\[\text{NDS} = \frac{\Delta\theta^\top H \Delta\theta}{\|\Delta\theta\|^2}\]
💡 归一化方向锐度（NDS）：衡量优化器步进方向上地形有多陡。分子是曲率惩罚，分母是更新范数的平方。Muon的NDS更低，说明它选择的方向更平坦。 Normalized Directional Sharpness (NDS): measures how steep the loss landscape is in the direction the optimizer steps. A lower NDS means you're walking on flatter ground — Muon consistently achieves this compared to Adam.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

训练像GPT这样的大型AI模型需要数百万美元和数月的算力——任何能真正将训练成本减半的优化器都极具价值。搞清楚Muon为何有效，意味着研究者可以设计出更好的训练算法，让强大的AI变得更快、更便宜。 Training large AI models like GPT costs millions of dollars and months of compute time — any optimizer that genuinely cuts training cost in half is enormously valuable. Understanding *why* Muon works means researchers can build even better training algorithms, potentially making powerful AI faster and cheaper to develop.

的问题		 大多数AI训练方法只追求"平均表现"，就像一个学生只关心平均分，而不管偶尔的严重失误。但现实中我们更关心：自动驾驶汽车不能有极端危险情况，数学解题AI要找到最优解法，金融AI要避免极端亏损。现有训练方法没有简单的方式直接优化这些"非平均"目标。 Most AI training methods try to make the agent perform well "on average." ⚙️ Method · 方法详解

OrderGrad的工作方式是：先收集AI当前行为产生的一批结果（奖励），将它们从好到坏排序，然后根据你关心的目标给每个排名分配不同的权重——比如只关心最优结果时，给排名靠前的结果高权重；想避免最坏情况时，给排名靠后的结果高权重。这些加权后的奖励直接替换掉原来的奖励，用于更新AI的参数。训练循环本身不需要任何根本性改变，只是在奖励上做了一步简单的"排序+加权"变换。 OrderGrad works by first collecting a batch of outcomes (rewards) from the AI's current behavior, then sorting them by rank (best to worst). It then assigns different weights to each rank — for example, giving heavy weight to only the top results if you want "best-of-K" behavior, or heavy weight to the bottom results if you want to avoid worst cases. These weighted rewards replace the standard rewards in the gradient update step. This means the AI learns to shift its behavior toward whatever part of the distribution you care about, without any fundamental change to how the training loop works.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\nabla J_w = \mathbb{E}\left[\sum_{k=1}^{K} w_k \nabla \log \pi_\theta(a_{(k)} | s) \cdot R_{(k)}\right]\]
💡 OrderGrad的核心梯度公式：将K个结果按奖励从小到大排序后，用对应的排名权重w_k加权每个动作的对数概率梯度，再乘以对应奖励R_(k)。通过改变权重向量w，可以恢复CVaR、中位数、top-m等各种目标。 The core OrderGrad gradient: sort K outcomes by reward rank, then weight each action's log-probability gradient by rank weight w_k times its reward R_(k). By changing the weight vector w, you recover objectives like CVaR, median, top-m, or best-of-K.
\[\hat{L}_w(\mathbf{R}) = \sum_{k=1}^{K} w_k R_{(k)}\]
💡 L统计量（L-statistic）：将K个奖励排序后按权重w_k加权求和。这是OrderGrad优化的目标函数，不同的权重向量对应不同的风险或性能目标。 The L-statistic objective: a weighted sum of sorted rewards, where w_k is the weight for the k-th ranked reward. Different weight vectors recover different objectives like trimmed mean, VaR, CVaR, or best-of-K.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

现实世界中"平均好"往往不够用——医疗AI不能在极端情况下严重出错，编程助手有时需要找到最优解而不是凑合的解。OrderGrad给开发者提供了一个简单的"调节旋钮"，可以在不重建训练系统的情况下，将AI行为向安全、鲁棒或极限性能方向调整。 In the real world, "good on average" is often not good enough — a medical AI shouldn't fail catastrophically on edge cases, and a coding assistant should sometimes find the brilliant solution, not just the mediocre one. OrderGrad gives developers a simple dial to tune AI behavior toward safety, robustness, or peak performance without rebuilding their training systems.

🎯 用"留二法"基线让AI推理训练信号更稳、更准
A smarter training signal (L2O baseline) makes AI reasoning models learn more efficiently by reducing noisy gradients.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像足球教练评价球员，不只看"你进球了吗"，而是看"你比队友平均水平高多少"——这种相对、居中的评分让球员更清楚地知道什么行为真正有效。L2O基线对AI训练做的正是同样的事。Imagine a soccer coach grading players not just by "did you score?" but by "how much better did you do than the average of the rest of the team?" That relative, centered score teaches players more clearly what actually helped — that's exactly what the L2O baseline does for AI training.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练AI推理模型时，通常只在最终答案正确时才给奖励，这种"全有或全无"的信号非常稀疏、嘈杂，模型很难从中有效学习。研究者希望直接优化模型"多次尝试中至少答对一次"的概率（即max@K），但现有方法使用了不同的信号和基准，关系混乱，难以比较和改进。 When training AI models to reason better, we often reward them only if they get the final answer right — but this "all-or-nothing" signal is very noisy and hard for the model to learn from. Researchers want to directly optimize how often the model gets at least one correct answer out of many tries (max@K), but different existing methods use inconsistent signals, making it hard to compare or improve them.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 发现现有主流方法虽然数学上无偏，但产生的训练信号"不居中"，引入了额外噪声。• 提出"留二法"（L2O）基线，既保持数学无偏性，又让批次内的优势值恰好居中，降低梯度方差。• 推导出统一理论框架，将现有所有max@K优势估计器纳入同一体系。• 新方法MaxPO时间复杂度为二次方，可无缝集成到标准的分组强化学习LLM训练流程中。 • Discovered that a leading existing method (Dr. GRPO / related approaches) is mathematically unbiased but produces an "off-center" training signal, which adds unnecessary noise. • Introduced a new "Leave-Two-Out" (L2O) baseline that keeps the math correct AND centers the signal, reducing gradient variance. • Derived a unified theoretical framework that connects all existing max@K advantage estimators under one roof. • The new method (MaxPO) runs efficiently in quadratic time and plugs directly into standard group-based LLM training pipelines.
🧪 实验
内容		 作者在数学推理基准测试上对MaxPO进行了验证，与GRPO等现有LLM后训练策略梯度方法进行比较。评估了标准数学题数据集上的梯度方差（验证理论主张）和最终任务准确率，并通过消融实验对比居中与非居中优势估计器，以单独评估L2O基线的效果。 The authors tested MaxPO on mathematical reasoning benchmarks, comparing it against existing policy-gradient methods for LLM post-training including GRPO and other max@K baselines. They evaluated on standard math problem datasets, measuring both gradient variance (to verify theoretical claims) and final task accuracy. They also performed ablation studies comparing centered vs. non-centered advantage estimators to isolate the effect of the L2O baseline.
📊 实验
结果		 实验表明，L2O基线相比非居中替代方案显著降低了梯度方差，验证了理论预测。MaxPO在数学推理基准上优于非居中基线方法。虽然摘要未列出全部具体数字，但实验部分确认了相比GRPO类方法在训练稳定性和最终准确率上的持续提升。 The paper reports that the L2O baseline measurably reduces gradient variance compared to non-centered alternatives, confirming the theoretical prediction. MaxPO outperforms non-centered baselines on mathematical reasoning benchmarks. While exact benchmark numbers are not fully enumerated in the abstract, the empirical section confirms consistent improvements in training stability and final accuracy over GRPO-style methods across evaluated math reasoning tasks.
🏁 结论 本文证明，在max@K策略梯度训练中对优势信号进行居中处理，既有理论依据又有实际效果——"留二法"基线同时实现了无偏性和低方差。MaxPO为用强化学习训练AI推理模型提供了更清晰、统一、高效的基础方案。 This paper proves that centering the advantage signal in max@K policy gradient training is both theoretically justified and practically beneficial, with the Leave-Two-Out baseline offering the best of both worlds — unbiasedness and low variance. MaxPO provides a clean, unified, and more efficient foundation for training AI reasoning models with reinforcement learning.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

核心思路是让训练反馈信号"居中"。就像老师按正态分布打分一样——不是说"你得了70分"，而是说"你比班级平均高10分"，相对比较更有意义、噪声更少。作者设计了"留二法"（L2O）基线：评估某个答案有多好时，从对比组中同时去掉该答案和另一个答案，再对剩余答案取平均。这个巧妙设计确保了一个训练批次内所有反馈分数之和恰好为零（完美居中），数学上证明可以降低梯度更新的方差。他们还证明这等价于max@K目标下的规范最优优势估计器。 The key insight is about "centering" the feedback signal given to the model during training. Think of it like grading on a curve: instead of just saying "you scored 70 out of 100," you say "you scored 10 points above the class average" — the relative comparison is more informative and less noisy. The authors design a Leave-Two-Out (L2O) baseline: when estimating how good a particular answer is, they leave out that answer AND one other answer from the comparison group, then average the rest. This clever trick ensures the feedback scores across a training batch always sum to zero (perfectly centered), which mathematically reduces the variance of the gradient updates. They prove this is equivalent to finding the canonical best advantage estimator for the max@K objective.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\hat{A}^{\text{L2O}}(a_i) = r_i - \frac{1}{K-2}\sum_{j \neq i, j \neq i^*} r_j\]
💡 留二法优势估计：用样本i的奖励减去排除自身和另一个样本后其余样本的平均奖励，使批次内优势值精确居中 Leave-Two-Out advantage: score an answer by subtracting the average reward of all other answers except itself and one more, ensuring the batch advantages sum to exactly zero
\[\sum_{i=1}^{K} \hat{A}^{\text{L2O}}(a_i) = 0\]
💡 L2O优势估计器的居中性质：一个批次内所有样本的优势值之和恰好等于零，消除了系统性偏移带来的梯度噪声 The centering property of L2O: the advantages across a batch sum to exactly zero, removing systematic bias from gradient updates

的问题		 AI模型在各种考试和测评中表现优异，但现实中却很难真正替代人类完成有经济价值的工作。现有的基准测试就像学校的选择题——只考察记忆和单点能力，并不能反映AI在真实职场中的表现。我们缺少一把"尺子"来衡量AI是否真的能胜任需要多步骤、长周期的专业工作。 AI models ace popular tests like SAT or coding contests, but that hasn't translated into them actually doing useful work in real industries like law, finance, or healthcare. Existing benchmarks are like school quizzes — they test memorization, not real job performance. We have no good way to measure whether an AI can truly handle the kind of complex, multi-step work that humans get paid to do.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队与超过250名来自真实行业的专业人士合作，设计出能反映实际有偿工作的任务——比如起草法律文书、分析财务报表、完整处理一个客服工单。每个任务都有明确、可核实的答案，方便客观打分。任务按照美国政府的职业分类体系整理为55个子领域、13个行业集群。随后，用主流AI智能体框架和不同的大模型"大脑"来运行这些任务，统计完整通过率。 The authors teamed up with over 250 professionals from real industries to design tasks that mirror actual paid work — think filing a legal brief, analyzing financial statements, or resolving a customer support ticket end-to-end. Each task has a clear, verifiable answer so you can objectively score whether the AI succeeded or failed. Tasks are organized into 55 subfields under 13 industry clusters, inspired by how the U.S. government classifies occupations. AI agents are then run through these tasks using standard "harness" frameworks and different AI backbone models, and their full pass rates are measured.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

大多数AI测评就像模拟考试，与真实工作脱节——ALE通过衡量AI能否完成有薪酬的真实工作来改变这一局面，这才是评估经济价值的真正标准。这为企业、研究人员和政策制定者提供了更诚实的参照系，帮助判断AI何时真正具备专业部署的能力。 Most AI benchmarks are like practice tests that don't reflect real jobs — ALE changes that by measuring whether AI can actually do work people get paid for, which is the true test for economic impact. This gives companies, researchers, and policymakers a much more honest scoreboard for deciding when AI is truly ready for professional deployment.
