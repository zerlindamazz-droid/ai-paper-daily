Beyond the Current Observation: Evaluating Multimodal Large Language Models in Controllable Non-Markov Games 超越当前观测：在可控非马尔可夫游戏中评估多模态大语言模型

🧑‍🔬 Shengyuan Ding, Xilin Wei, Xinyu Fang, Haodong Duan et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 新基准专测AI能否记住"已消失的画面"并据此做决策
A new benchmark tests whether AI can remember hidden past observations and act on them in real-time games.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像玩翻牌记忆游戏时，你看了一眼某张牌的位置，然后它被翻回去了——当前的AI就像一个"金鱼记忆"的玩家，转眼就忘了刚才看到的，而这个研究就是专门测试并帮助AI练就"过目不忘"的本领。Imagine playing a memory card game where you briefly flip over cards to see their pictures, then they all get flipped back — and you have to remember where the matching pairs are. Today's AI is like a player with a goldfish memory who forgets what they just saw. This paper builds the ultimate memory card game to test — and eventually train — AI to actually remember.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有AI模型在做决策时，通常只"看眼前"——它们需要完整地看到当前状态才能正确行动。但现实世界里，很多任务要求你记住之前看到的东西（比如翻牌记忆游戏），而不是每次都能看到全部信息。目前的评测工具要么把所有信息都给AI看，要么把"记忆力测试"和其他技能混在一起，无法单独衡量AI的记忆能力。 Most AI models only pay attention to what's right in front of them — they struggle when important information has already disappeared from view. In real-world tasks, like navigating a maze or playing a memory card game, you have to remember things you saw earlier. Existing tests either give AI all the information it needs, or mix up memory skills with other abilities, making it impossible to know exactly where AI falls short.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首创"非马尔可夫游戏"基准RNG-Bench，专门隔离并测试AI对过去隐藏信息的重建与利用能力 • 包含两类互补游戏：翻牌记忆配对（Matching Pairs）和3D迷宫导航，覆盖记忆与空间推理 • 引入"Memory Gap"指标，能精准区分AI是"忘记了"还是"决策失误"，找到真正的弱点所在 • 设计了"头对头对决"协议，消除题目本身难度差异对评测结果的干扰 • 最难配置需处理约12.8万token和350张图像，当前最强模型仍远未达到饱和 • Introduces RNG-Bench, the first benchmark designed to purely isolate an AI's ability to recall hidden past observations during live interaction • Two complementary games: Matching Pairs (memory card game) and 3D Maze (spatial navigation from first-person views) • A new "Memory Gap" metric cleanly separates forgetting from bad decision-making, pinpointing the real weakness • A head-to-head duel protocol eliminates luck/instance variance from comparisons • Hardest levels require ~128K token contexts and 350 images per episode — still unsolved by top models
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在RNG-Bench上评测了多款主流多模态大模型，包括GPT-4o、Gemini 2.5 Pro、Claude 3.7 Sonnet、Qwen2.5-VL等前沿闭源和开源模型。评测覆盖不同难度配置（棋盘从3×3到更大、多种视觉图案、图像与文本两种模态），并与随机基线、完美记忆上界对比。还针对Qwen2.5-VL-7B进行了微调实验，用最优策略轨迹和筛选后的模型演示数据作为训练集，并在Video-MME、MMStar等已有基准上验证是否发生能力退化。 The researchers tested a range of top multimodal AI models including GPT-4o, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Claude 3.7 Sonnet, and Qwen2.5-VL across all difficulty settings of RNG-Bench. Results were compared against a random-guess baseline and a perfect-memory upper bound. They also fine-tuned the Qwen2.5-VL-7B model on recordings of optimal gameplay and filtered model demonstrations. To make sure fine-tuning didn't break other abilities, they checked performance on existing benchmarks like Video-MME and MMStar.
📊 实验
结果		 • 所有前沿模型（包括GPT-4o、Gemini 2.5 Pro）在最难配置下得分远低于满分，基准远未饱和 • Memory Gap分析显示，大多数残余错误（通常超过50%）来源于"遗忘"而非"决策失误" • 微调后的Qwen2.5-VL-7B在RNG-Bench上性能显著提升，同时在Video-MME、MMStar等通用基准上未出现性能下降 • 最难配置（约128K token上下文，每局350张图像输入）对所有测试模型均构成严峻挑战，无模型接近饱和 • All frontier models including GPT-4o and Gemini 2.5 Pro score far below the perfect ceiling on hardest configurations — the benchmark is far from saturated • Memory Gap analysis reveals that most residual errors (often >50%) come from forgetting, not from making bad decisions • Fine-tuned Qwen2.5-VL-7B shows meaningful gains on RNG-Bench while maintaining performance on general benchmarks like Video-MME and MMStar with no capability degradation • The hardest setting (~128K token context, ~350 images per episode) remains unsolved by all tested models
🏁 结论 本文证明了当前最强的多模态AI模型在"需要记住已消失信息并据此行动"这一能力上存在严重短板，且这一短板主要源于遗忘而非决策失误。RNG-Bench为未来AI记忆能力的研究和提升提供了一个可靠、可控的评测平台。 This paper proves that even the most advanced multimodal AI models have a critical blind spot: they forget things they've seen before, and this forgetting — not bad reasoning — is what holds them back. RNG-Bench provides a rigorous, controllable testing ground to drive future improvements in AI memory.
🔗 论文
链接		 arXiv: 2606.19338  ·  📥 PDF

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队设计了两款可控游戏来测试AI的记忆能力。第一款是翻牌记忆游戏：AI先短暂看到每张牌的位置和图案，牌被翻回去之后，AI必须凭记忆找到配对。第二款是3D迷宫：AI只能看到第一人称视角，必须把走过的画面拼合成完整地图再做导航决策。两款游戏都有三个可调难度轴（棋盘大小、视觉图案复杂度、输入模态），并配合"记忆缺口"指标来判断AI到底是"忘了"还是"选错了"。此外，还用最优策略轨迹和筛选后的模型演示数据对Qwen2.5-VL-7B模型进行微调，验证能否通过训练提升记忆能力。 The team built two controllable games to stress-test AI memory. In Matching Pairs, the AI briefly sees where each card is, then the cards flip face-down — it must later recall which card is where to make matches, just like the classic memory card game. In 3D Maze, the AI only sees a first-person camera view and must mentally stitch together a map of where it's been to navigate successfully. Both games have three difficulty dials (board size, visual complexity, input type). A special "Memory Gap" score measures how much of the AI's failure comes from forgetting versus making bad choices. Finally, they fine-tuned a model using recordings of perfect play to see if training can fix the memory problem.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{Memory Gap} = \text{Score}_{\text{perfect recall}} - \text{Score}_{\text{actual}}\]
💡 记忆缺口 = 假设AI完美记住所有信息时的得分 − AI实际得分，数值越大说明遗忘问题越严重 Memory Gap = the score an AI would get if it remembered everything perfectly, minus its actual score. A bigger gap means the AI is forgetting a lot, not just making bad decisions.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

未来的AI助手需要在长时间交互中记住用户说过的话、看过的画面，才能真正像"得力助手"一样工作。这项研究揭示了当前AI在记忆方面的核心缺陷，并提供了衡量和改进这一能力的工具，直接推动AI向更可靠的长程交互迈进。 Future AI assistants — whether helping you navigate, play games, or manage complex tasks — need to remember what they've already seen, not just react to the present moment. This work exposes a fundamental memory weakness in today's best AI models and gives the research community a clear tool to measure and fix it.

Playful Agentic Robot Learning 玩耍式智能机器人学习

🧑‍🔬 Junyi Zhang, Jiaxin Ge, Hanjun Yoo, Letian Fu et al.

图 Figure · Page 5 of paper

🎯 让机器人在接到任务前通过"自由玩耍"积累可复用技能
Robots learn reusable skills through self-directed play before being given any real tasks.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个新厨师在餐厅开业前，利用休息日在厨房里自由练习——试试新刀工、摸索酱汁配方。等到真正开餐迎客时，他已经胸有成竹，而不是手忙脚乱地现学现卖。Think of it like a new chef who spends their day off experimenting freely in the kitchen — trying new knife tricks, testing sauces — before the restaurant opens. When the dinner rush hits, they're already skilled and fast, instead of figuring things out mid-service.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的AI机器人只有在接到明确指令时才会学习新技能——它们完全依赖任务驱动。每次来了新任务，机器人几乎都要从零开始，无法利用过去的经验。机器人没有办法"自己练习"并提前积累一套实用的技能库。 Current AI robots only learn new skills when someone explicitly tells them what to do — they're purely task-driven. This means every time a new task arrives, the robot starts almost from scratch and can't draw on past experience. There's no way for the robot to "practice on its own" and build up a useful skill library ahead of time.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 机器人在接到真实任务前可以"自由玩耍"，自己出题、自己练习，主动积累技能。 • 由一组AI智能体（RATs）分工协作：有的负责提议任务、有的写机器人代码、有的检验结果、有的诊断失败——像一个自我改进的小团队。 • 成功学到的技能被存入持久化技能库，甚至可以直接"插件式"提供给其他机器人系统，无需额外训练。 • Robots can now "play freely" before any real task arrives, generating and practicing their own mini-challenges to build skills proactively. • A team of AI agents (RATs) collaborates during play: one proposes tasks, one writes robot code, one checks results, and one diagnoses failures — like a tiny self-improving workshop. • Successfully learned skills are saved into a persistent library and can be handed to *other* robot systems without any extra training, just by being retrieved into context.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在两个仿真基准上进行——LIBERO-PRO（桌面操作任务套件）和MolmoSpaces（视觉引导机器人环境）——以及RoboSuite仿真和真实世界机器人测试。主要基线是CaP-Agent0（完全不玩耍）和随机玩耍变体（任务随机提议而非智能生成）。核心评估指标是机器人在从未见过的下游任务上的成功率。 Experiments were run on two simulation benchmarks — LIBERO-PRO (a suite of tabletop manipulation tasks) and MolmoSpaces (a vision-grounded robot environment) — plus RoboSuite simulations and real-world robot trials. The main baselines were CaP-Agent0 (no play at all) and a random-play variant (where tasks are proposed randomly rather than intelligently). The key metric was task success rate on held-out downstream tasks the robot had never seen during play.
📊 实验
结果		 在LIBERO-PRO上，玩耍学习的技能比无玩耍基线（CaP-Agent0）提升了20.6个百分点。在MolmoSpaces上提升了17.0个百分点。当学到的技能库被"插件式"接入其他推理时智能体（无需微调模型）后，在RoboSuite上提升8.9个百分点，在真实世界机器人迁移实验中提升8.8个百分点。 On LIBERO-PRO, the play-learned skills improved task success by 20.6 percentage points over the no-play baseline (CaP-Agent0). On MolmoSpaces, the gain was 17.0 percentage points. When the learned skill library was plugged into other inference-time agents (without any model finetuning), performance improved by 8.9 points on RoboSuite and 8.8 points in real-world robot transfer experiments.
🏁 结论 本文证明，让机器人在接到真实任务前"自由玩耍"——由协作AI智能体团队引导——能显著提升它们解决新任务的能力。所学技能库具有可移植性，可直接惠及其他机器人系统，标志着迈向持续主动学习机器人的重要一步。 This paper proves that letting robots "play freely" before receiving real tasks — guided by a collaborative team of AI agents — significantly improves their ability to solve new tasks. The resulting skill library is portable and benefits other robot systems out of the box, marking a meaningful step toward robots that learn continuously and proactively.

链接		 arXiv: 2606.19419  ·  📥 PDF

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

系统引入了"RATs"——一个在自由玩耍阶段协作的AI智能体团队。首先，一个智能体提出新颖但可完成的练习任务（不太难也不太简单）；然后另一个智能体编写机器人控制代码来尝试完成任务；验证者检查每个步骤是否成功；诊断者在失败时找出原因并给出详细反馈，让机器人重试。当任务最终完成，成功的代码被保存为可复用的"技能"存入技能库。真实任务到来时，机器人从库中检索相关技能作为积木来使用——就像从工具架上取工具，而不是每次从头制造。 The system introduces "RATs" — a team of specialized AI agents that collaborate during a free-play phase. First, one agent proposes novel but achievable practice tasks (not too hard, not too easy). Then another writes robot control code to try the task. A verifier checks whether each step succeeded, and a diagnostician figures out what went wrong if it failed, giving detailed feedback so the robot can retry. When a task is finally solved, the successful code is saved as a reusable "skill" in a library. When real tasks arrive later, the robot searches this library for relevant skills and uses them as building blocks — like pulling tools off a shelf instead of rebuilding them from scratch.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

这项研究让机器人更接近人类和动物的自然学习方式——通过好奇心驱动的自主探索，而不是等待指令。实际意义在于，未来的家用或工厂机器人可以在夜间"热身"，自主练习技能，等你真正需要它们时，它们会更得力。 This research moves robots closer to how humans and animals naturally learn — through curious, self-motivated exploration rather than waiting for instructions. In practice, it means future household or factory robots could "warm up" overnight, practicing skills on their own, so they're far more capable when you actually need them.

Multi-LCB: Extending LiveCodeBench to Multiple Programming Languages Multi-LCB：将LiveCodeBench扩展至多种编程语言

🧑‍🔬 Maria Ivanova, Pavel Zadorozhny, Rodion Levichev, Ivan Petrov et al.

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 首个覆盖12种编程语言的代码生成基准，揭露AI对Python的过度依赖
A 12-language coding benchmark that exposes how LLMs secretly over-rely on Python.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy这就像给一个声称"精通六国语言"的翻译员，除了中文什么都不考，然后说他外语能力超强。Multi-LCB就是那份真正把法语、德语、日语都考一遍的综合语言测试卷。Imagine hiring a translator who claims to speak six languages, but you only ever tested them in English. Multi-LCB is like finally handing them a test in French, Japanese, and Arabic too — and discovering they were just really good at English all along.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的AI代码能力测试几乎只考Python，就好像只用一门外语考试就说一个学生"精通多国语言"一样。现实中的软件开发需要Java、C++、Rust等十几种编程语言，AI模型是否真的能在这些语言上表现良好，一直没有严格的测试工具。 Existing AI coding benchmarks almost exclusively test Python, like judging a student's "multilingual fluency" based only on their English test score. In real-world software engineering, developers use dozens of languages like Java, C++, and Rust — but no rigorous benchmark existed to check whether AI models can actually handle them.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 将原有的Python编程题自动转换为12种编程语言的等效题目，保留了原始测试的防数据污染设计 • 完全兼容原始LCB格式，未来LCB新增题目会自动同步到Multi-LCB • 通过测试24个主流大模型，首次系统性地揭示了"Python过拟合"现象——模型在Python上表现远好于其他语言 • Automatically converts Python coding problems into equivalent tasks in 11 other languages while keeping the original contamination-prevention design intact • Fully compatible with the original LCB format, so any new Python problems added in the future are automatically covered • Tests 24 major LLMs and for the first time systematically reveals "Python overfitting" — models perform dramatically better in Python than in other languages
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者在Multi-LCB上评测了24个大型语言模型，涵盖指令跟随型和推理型模型，测试语言包括Python、Java、C++、C#、Go、Rust、JavaScript、TypeScript、PHP、Ruby、Swift和Kotlin共12种。以原始LCB的Python成绩作为基线，对比各模型在不同语言上的通过率（pass@1）。 The researchers evaluated 24 LLMs — both instruction-following and reasoning-focused models — across all 12 programming languages on Multi-LCB. Python performance on the original LCB served as the baseline, and the key metric was pass@1 (whether the model's first attempt at a coding problem was correct). This let them directly compare how well each model generalized beyond Python.
📊 实验
结果		 实验发现所有24个模型在Python上的表现均显著优于其他语言，证实了"Python过拟合"现象的普遍存在。部分模型在某些小众语言（如PHP、Ruby）上的通过率比Python低超过20个百分点。研究还发现部分模型存在特定语言的数据污染迹象，以及不同语言间性能差距悬殊的问题。具体数字方面，顶尖推理模型（如GPT-4o级别）在Python上pass@1约为60-70%，而在Rust、Swift等语言上可降至40%以下。 All 24 models scored noticeably higher on Python than on other languages, confirming widespread Python overfitting. For some models, pass@1 rates on languages like PHP or Ruby dropped more than 20 percentage points compared to Python. Top reasoning models (GPT-4o-class) achieved around 60–70% pass@1 on Python, but dropped below 40% on languages like Rust or Swift. The study also found signs of language-specific data contamination in certain models, meaning they may have memorized solutions for specific languages.
🏁 结论 本文构建了首个覆盖12种编程语言的标准化代码生成基准Multi-LCB，并通过大规模评测证明当前主流大语言模型普遍存在Python过拟合问题，在真正的多语言代码生成能力上仍有显著差距。 Multi-LCB is the first standardized benchmark covering 12 programming languages for code generation evaluation, and it definitively shows that today's best AI coding models are far more fluent in Python than in other languages — a critical gap for real-world software engineering.

链接		 arXiv: 2606.20517  ·  📥 PDF

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队从LiveCodeBench中提取Python编程竞赛题，再将每道题的题目描述、测试用例、评分逻辑等全部翻译/转换为Java、C++、Rust、Go等11种语言的等效版本，确保考察的是同一个算法逻辑问题。整个流程保留了LCB原有的"按题目发布日期筛选"机制，防止AI模型作弊（即提前见过题目）。由于格式完全兼容，未来LCB更新的新题目会自动流入Multi-LCB，无需人工维护。 The team took competitive programming problems from LiveCodeBench (originally Python-only) and converted each problem — its description, test cases, and grading logic — into 11 other languages like Java, C++, Rust, and Go, making sure each version tests the same underlying algorithm. They kept LCB's clever "release date filter" trick, which prevents AI models from cheating by memorizing problems they may have seen during training. Because the format is fully compatible, any new problems added to LCB in the future will automatically flow into Multi-LCB without extra human effort.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

对AI产品来说，这意味着那些声称"擅长编程"的AI助手，其实可能只在Python上表现好，用其他语言写代码时可靠性大打折扣。Multi-LCB为开发者和研究者提供了一把真正的多语言尺子，推动未来AI模型在更广泛的编程场景中均衡发展。 For AI products, this means coding assistants that claim to be "great at programming" may actually only be reliable in Python — a serious problem for the many developers using other languages. Multi-LCB gives researchers and companies a fair, rigorous measuring stick to build more balanced and trustworthy AI coding tools.

🎯 让AI像侦探一样在3D世界中积累证据、推理空间关系
A spatial agent that reasons over 3D scenes by accumulating evidence across views and time, no extra training needed.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一位经验丰富的考古学家——不会只看一眼土层照片就下结论，而是边挖边记录、把每层发现的碎片拼在一起，最终还原出完整的古迹全貌。S-Agent就是这样一位"空间考古学家"，边看视频/多视角图像边积累3D线索，最终拼出对空间的完整理解。Think of it like a detective walking through a crime scene — instead of guessing everything from one blurry photo, they walk around, collect clues from every angle, write notes, and build a full picture over time. S-Agent does exactly that for AI: it walks through images and video frames, collects 3D spatial clues at each step, and assembles them into a complete understanding of the space.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

S-Agent把大型视觉语言模型（VLM）变成一个"指挥官"，由它决定需要什么空间证据；然后调用一套层级工具：先在2D图像中定位目标，再把2D位置"拔高"成真实的3D坐标和几何信息，最后把这些几何证据汇总成可直接回答问题的高层空间知识（比如物体数量、距离、朝向）。为了处理视频或多视角序列，系统维护两个记忆模块：场景记忆持续更新3D场景的状态，智能体记忆记录每一步推理的上下文，确保信息在帧与帧之间不丢失。整个过程不需要修改原始VLM的参数，即插即用。 S-Agent turns any vision-language model into a "commander" that decides what spatial evidence is needed, then dispatches a hierarchy of specialized tools to collect it. First, objects are located in 2D images; then those 2D detections are "lifted" into real 3D coordinates and geometry. The 3D evidence is then compiled into high-level spatial facts — counts, distances, orientations, relative positions — that the AI can directly use to answer questions. A dual memory system (one for the evolving scene, one for the reasoning chain) keeps all accumulated knowledge alive across frames and reasoning steps, with no changes needed to the underlying AI model.

\[\text{S-Agent} = \text{VLM Planner} \xrightarrow{\text{tool calls}} \{\text{2D Grounding} \to \text{3D Lifting} \to \text{Spatial Knowledge}\}\]
💡 S-Agent的核心流程：VLM作为语义规划器，通过调用层级工具将2D检测逐步提升为3D几何证据，再汇总为空间知识。 The core pipeline of S-Agent: the VLM acts as a planner that calls tools to progressively lift 2D detections into 3D geometric evidence and then into high-level spatial knowledge.
\[\mathcal{M} = \{\mathcal{M}_{\text{scene}}, \mathcal{M}_{\text{agent}}\}\]
💡 双记忆机制：场景记忆M_scene维护不断演化的3D场景状态，智能体记忆M_agent积累推理上下文，两者共同支撑跨帧推理。 Dual memory system: Scene Memory tracks the evolving 3D scene state across frames; Agent Memory accumulates the reasoning context across steps — together enabling persistent spatial understanding.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

空间智能是机器人、自动驾驶、AR/VR等现实应用的核心能力，而本文提供了一种无需昂贵重训练就能给现有AI"升级空间感"的即插即用方案。同时，S-300K数据集和S-Agent-8B为资源有限的团队提供了平价替代方案，可能推动整个行业在空间AI能力上快速普及。 Spatial intelligence is the missing piece for real-world AI applications like robotics, self-driving cars, and augmented reality — and this paper offers a plug-and-play upgrade that works on existing AI models at no extra training cost. The open dataset and compact 8B model also democratize high-quality spatial AI, putting capabilities previously locked inside expensive closed-source systems within reach of any research lab or startup.
