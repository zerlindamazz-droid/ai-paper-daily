ArcANE: Do Role-Playing Language Agents Stay in Character at the Right Time? ArcANE：角色扮演语言智能体能在正确时机保持角色一致性吗？

🧑‍🔬 Woojung Song, Nalim Kim, Sangjun Song, Chaewon Heo et al.

图 Figure · Page 4 of paper

🎯 让AI角色扮演随故事发展而"成长"，而非一成不变
A benchmark testing if AI roleplay agents evolve with characters, not just recall their facts.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像让一个演员扮演《哈利·波特》中的赫敏——一个好演员不只背台词，他知道赫敏在第一册时又怕又兴奋，在第七册时已历经沧桑、无所畏惧。ArcANE就是在测试AI演员"对不对戏份"，不管剧本给没给这场戏的台词。Think of it like casting an actor to play Hermione Granger at different points in the story. A great actor knows Hermione in Book 1 is nervous and rule-following, while Hermione in Book 7 is battle-hardened and fearless — and they'd respond differently to the same situation. ArcANE tests whether AI actors know which version of the character to play, even for scenes the script never wrote.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有AI角色扮演模型就像只背了一本书的演员——它们能记住角色说过什么，却不理解角色在故事不同阶段的心理变化。当有人问"如果角色处于某个情节之外的场景会怎么做"，AI往往给出错误的、不符合人物当时状态的回答。这就像让一个只看过电影结局的人假装自己是中途某个阶段的角色——他们会穿帮。 Existing AI roleplay models are like actors who only memorized their character's lines, not their inner journey. They can recall facts about a character, but they don't understand how that character's mindset shifts as the story progresses. So when asked "what would this character do in a brand-new situation?", the AI responds based on the wrong emotional or moral state — like an actor forgetting they're supposed to be playing the villain in Act 1, not the reformed hero from Act 3.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首创"角色弧线感知"评测框架：把故事切分为不同心理阶段，测试AI能否在每个阶段给出符合角色当时状态的回答 • 涵盖17部小说、80个主要角色，包含原著内和原著外的全新场景，真正考验AI的理解深度而非死记硬背 • 发现"角色弧线上下文"这一策略全面碾压其他信息输入方式，尤其在原著没有的场景中优势最为显著 • 基于该数据集微调出ArcANE-8B/32B模型，进一步放大了弧线理解的优势 • First benchmark to test character arc awareness — splitting stories into psychological phases and probing whether AI stays true to each phase • Spans 17 novels and 80 major characters, testing both in-text situations and brand-new scenarios the original story never covered • Giving the AI a "character arc summary" consistently outperforms all other context strategies across all tested models • Fine-tuned models (ArcANE-8B/32B) show even stronger arc-aware performance, especially on novel scenarios
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在17部经典小说、80个主要角色上构建评测集，为每个角色设计跨故事阶段的相同场景问题，分为"原著内场景"和"原著外场景"两类。测试了6个不同的大语言模型（包括闭源和开源），以及6种上下文输入策略。评估指标采用GPT-4评分来判断模型回答是否与该阶段角色的心理状态一致。此外，还在同一数据上微调了8B和32B参数规模的开源模型作为进一步验证。 The team built an evaluation set across 17 novels and 80 major characters, designing scenario probes that repeat the same situation across different story phases, covering both situations that appear in the source text and entirely new ones that don't. Six large language models (both closed and open-source) were tested under six different context-feeding strategies. GPT-4 was used as a judge to score whether each model response matched the character's psychological state at the correct story phase. Additionally, open-source models at 8B and 32B scale were fine-tuned on the benchmark data as a further experimental condition.
📊 实验
结果		 在所有6个模型和6种上下文策略的组合实验中，提供"角色弧线摘要"的策略始终排名第一，且在原著外场景（检索式方法完全失效的地方）优势最为突出。微调后的ArcANE-8B和ArcANE-32B进一步拉大了弧线感知优势，尤其在原著外场景的得分提升幅度最为显著（论文中报告了跨模型和跨条件的一致性优势，具体数值见各实验表格）。 Across all combinations of 6 models and 6 context strategies, providing a character arc summary ranked #1 every single time — no exceptions. The performance gap was largest on out-of-text scenarios, where retrieval-based methods had nothing useful to find. Fine-tuned ArcANE-8B and ArcANE-32B models further widened this arc advantage, with the biggest gains specifically on novel, out-of-text scenarios (consistent advantage reported across all models and conditions in the paper's evaluation tables).
🏁 结论 本文证明：让AI理解角色的"心理成长轨迹"（角色弧线）远比让它背诵原文更重要，尤其面对原著未曾涉及的新情境时。ArcANE基准和配套微调模型为构建真正能"入戏"的AI角色扮演智能体提供了坚实基础。 This paper proves that teaching AI the psychological journey of a character — not just the facts of the story — is the key to authentic, consistent roleplay, especially in new situations the original story never described. ArcANE sets a new standard for evaluating and building AI agents that truly "stay in character at the right time."

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队先为每个角色构建"角色弧线"——用一条心理轴把故事分成若干阶段，描述角色在每个阶段的内心状态。然后，他们设计同一个场景（比如"如果角色面对背叛会怎么做"），分别在不同故事阶段向AI提问，看AI的回答是否随角色心理变化而变化。每个问题都有6种不同的上下文输入方式（比如：不给任何背景、给原文片段、给角色弧线摘要等），通过对比找出哪种方式最有效。最后，研究者还用这套数据直接微调了开源大模型，让它们专门学会"随弧线调整角色回应"。 The team first builds a "character arc" for each character — a psychological timeline that divides the story into phases and describes how the character's inner state evolves. Then they craft the same scenario (e.g., "How would this character react to betrayal?") and ask the AI to answer it at different story phases, checking whether the response changes appropriately. They test six different ways of feeding context to the AI — from nothing at all, to raw story text, to a compact arc summary — to see which helps most. Finally, they fine-tune open-source models directly on this arc-structured data to create specialized roleplay agents.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着AI角色扮演产品（如AI陪伴、互动小说、游戏NPC）快速普及，用户期望AI能真实还原复杂人物的心理深度，而不只是复读台词。这项研究为业界提供了衡量和提升这一能力的具体工具，有助于让AI角色更真实、更有共情力。 As AI roleplay products — like AI companions, interactive fiction, and game NPCs — grow rapidly, users expect characters that feel psychologically real and consistent, not just trivia-spouting chatbots. This research gives the industry a concrete tool to measure and improve that depth, pushing AI characters toward genuine emotional authenticity.

🧠 LLM Agents · 大语言模型智能体🏷️ Benchmarking · 基准测试🏷️ Planning · 规划能力💡 Reasoning · 推理
AdaPlanBench: Evaluating Adaptive Planning in Large Language Model Agents under World and User Constraints AdaPlanBench：评估大语言模型智能体在世界约束与用户约束下的自适应规划能力

🧑‍🔬 Jiayu Liu, Cheng Qian, Zhenhailong Wang, Bingxuan Li et al.

图 Figure · Page 3 of paper

🎯 新基准测试揭示：LLM在动态双重约束下的自适应规划仍远未成熟
A new benchmark reveals LLMs still struggle to adaptively replan when real-world and user constraints emerge mid-task.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像你请朋友帮你做一顿饭，但你没一次性告诉他所有要求——他每炒一道菜，你才说"哦对了，我不吃辣"或"那个锅坏了别用"。一个聪明的朋友能记住这些反馈、调整接下来的步骤；而现在的AI，往往做着做着就"忘了"你之前说过的话，这篇论文就是专门来测哪个AI"记性最好、最会随机应变"的。Imagine asking a friend to help plan your dinner, but you only tell them "I don't like spicy food" AFTER they suggest a spicy dish, and "the blender is broken" only after they plan a smoothie. A great friend remembers all these complaints and keeps adjusting — this paper is basically a report card testing which AI can do exactly that, and the grades weren't great!
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 当AI助手帮你规划任务时（比如做饭、安排行程），现实中往往会出现各种"临时限制"——有些是客观世界的规则（比如某件东西坏了），有些是你个人的偏好（比如不喜欢某种食材）。这些限制并不是一开始就全告诉AI的，而是随着任务推进才逐渐暴露。现有的AI测试基准大多假设所有限制一开始就已知，根本没法测出AI在"边干边发现新限制"时的真实表现。 When an AI assistant helps you plan something — like cooking a meal or organizing your day — real-life complications pop up along the way. Some are physical facts ("the oven is broken"), and some are personal preferences ("I don't eat spicy food"). These surprises aren't revealed all at once; they surface one by one as the AI proposes steps. Existing tests for AI planning assume all rules are known upfront, so they don't measure how well AI can adapt when new constraints keep appearing mid-task.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次提出"双重约束"自适应规划基准：同时测试AI应对客观世界限制（如物品不可用）和用户个人偏好限制的能力，两者缺一不可 • 动态交互协议：约束只在AI提出违规计划时才会"被揭示"，迫使AI从错误反馈中学习并不断修正计划 • 可扩展的约束构建流水线：基于307个家庭任务自动生成多样化的约束组合，兼顾真实性与可扩展性 • 系统性发现：用户约束比世界约束更难处理，且随着约束数量增加，AI表现显著下滑 • First benchmark to jointly test AI on both physical world constraints AND personal user preference constraints — a more realistic dual challenge • Hidden constraint reveal mechanic: rules only appear when the AI makes a violating plan, forcing it to learn from mistakes and revise iteratively • Scalable pipeline to automatically generate diverse constraint combinations across 307 household tasks • Key finding: user preference constraints are harder for AI than world constraints, and performance drops as more constraints pile up
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在10个主流大语言模型（包括GPT-4o、Claude、Gemini等顶尖模型）上进行了系统评测，使用AdaPlanBench的307个任务作为测试集。评估指标包括：最终计划的整体准确率、每类约束（世界/用户）的成功率，以及随约束数量增加时的性能变化曲线。同时分析了AI常见的失败模式，如遗忘之前揭示的约束、物理常识错误等。 The team tested ten leading large language models — including top models like GPT-4o, Claude, and Gemini — on all 307 tasks in AdaPlanBench. They measured overall planning accuracy, success rates broken down by constraint type (world vs. user), and how performance changed as the number of constraints increased. They also analyzed the types of mistakes AI models commonly made, such as forgetting previously revealed constraints or lacking basic physical common sense.
📊 实验
结果		 • 最强模型（最佳LLM）的整体准确率仅为67.75%，远未达到实用水平 • 随着约束数量增加，所有模型性能均显著下滑 • 用户约束（个人偏好类）比世界约束（物理限制类）更难处理，是模型失败的主要来源 • 常见失败原因：AI在多轮交互中遗忘早前揭示的约束，以及物理常识薄弱导致判断错误 • The best-performing LLM achieved only 67.75% accuracy overall — far from reliable for real-world use • All models showed clear performance drops as more constraints accumulated during a task • User preference constraints proved significantly harder than world/physical constraints across all models • Common failure modes: forgetting previously revealed constraints across turns, and weak physical commonsense reasoning
🏁 结论 本文证明了当前最先进的大语言模型在面对逐步揭示的双重约束（世界约束+用户约束）时，自适应规划能力仍然严重不足，最佳模型准确率不超过68%。AdaPlanBench为这一能力的系统性评测提供了可复用的基础设施，并明确指出了用户偏好适应和物理常识推理是亟需突破的短板。 This paper proves that even the best current AI models are far from reliable at adapting plans when new constraints keep emerging — the top model only got 67.75% right. AdaPlanBench provides a reusable testing ground for this skill and pinpoints user preference handling and physical commonsense reasoning as the biggest gaps to fix.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队构建了一个名为AdaPlanBench的互动测试平台，基于307个家庭场景任务（如整理房间、准备早餐）。每个任务都被自动添加两类约束：一类是"世界约束"（模拟现实中的物理限制，比如某个工具不可用），另一类是"用户约束"（模拟用户的个人喜好，比如不想用某种方式）。测试时，AI不会提前知道这些约束，只有当它提出的计划违反了某条约束时，该约束才会被揭示。AI必须根据反馈不断修改计划，直到计划完全合规。这模拟了真实生活中"边走边发现问题"的情形，对AI的推理和记忆能力提出了很高要求。 The researchers built an interactive testing platform called AdaPlanBench using 307 everyday household tasks (like tidying a room or making breakfast). Each task is automatically equipped with two types of hidden rules: "world constraints" (physical limitations, e.g., a tool is unavailable) and "user constraints" (personal preferences, e.g., avoid a certain method). During the test, the AI doesn't know these rules upfront. Rules are only revealed when the AI proposes a plan that breaks one of them. The AI must then revise its plan based on that feedback — and keep going until all rules are satisfied. This mimics how real-world tasks unfold, where you discover problems only after you try something.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

真实世界的AI助手（如智能家居管家、个人规划助手）必须能在任务执行过程中灵活应对不断出现的新限制，而不是只能处理"已知全部规则"的理想情况。这项研究用具体数字揭示了当前AI的真实短板，为下一代更可靠、更实用的AI规划系统指明了改进方向。 Real-world AI assistants — like smart home helpers or personal scheduling agents — must handle surprises and adapt on the fly, not just follow a perfectly pre-specified rulebook. This research gives the AI community a concrete, measurable way to track and improve this critical skill, pushing us closer to AI that actually works reliably in messy, real-world situations.

VideoKR: Towards Knowledge- and Reasoning-Intensive Video Understanding VideoKR：面向知识密集型与推理密集型的视频理解

🧑‍🔬 Lin Fu, Zheyuan Yang, Yang Wang, Tingyu Song et al.

图 Figure · Page 3 of paper

🎯 首个专为深度视频推理打造的大规模训练数据集
A large-scale training dataset that teaches AI to truly reason about expert-domain videos, not just guess from text.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像培养一个医学生，你不能只给他看搞笑视频，而要让他反复研究真实手术录像，再用越来越难的临床问题考他。VideoKR对AI做的正是这件事：它是一套专业"硬核"课程，把一个什么都懂一点的通才AI，训练成真正能看懂专业视频、独立推理的"领域专家"。Think of it like training a medical student — you don't just show them funny YouTube videos; you make them study thousands of real surgery recordings and then quiz them with increasingly hard clinical questions. VideoKR does exactly this for AI: it's the curriculum of tough, real-world expert videos that turns a generalist AI into a genuine expert reasoner.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的AI视频模型在回答普通视频问题时看起来不错，但它们其实经常"走捷径"——仅靠文字线索猜答案，根本没有真正"看懂"视频。一旦遇到医学影像、科学实验或工程操作等专业领域视频，这些模型就会表现得很差，因为它们既缺乏专业知识，又没有把"看到的"和"知道的"联系起来的深度推理能力。而且，目前根本没有一个专门为解决这一问题而构建的大规模高质量训练数据集。 Current AI video models are surprisingly good at answering questions about everyday videos, but they often cheat — they guess the answer from text clues alone without actually "watching" the video. When asked about expert-level content (like medical scans, scientific footage, or engineering processes), these models fall apart because they lack both the domain knowledge and the deep reasoning skills to connect what they see to what they know. There was no large, high-quality training dataset specifically built to fix this gap.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首个专注于知识密集型视频推理的大规模训练语料库，包含31.5万条样本和14.5万段专业领域视频。 • 设计了"人在回路"的逐级递进式问题生成流水线，确保每道题都需要真正看懂视频才能回答，杜绝文字捷径。 • 构建了全新的专家标注评测基准VideoKR-Eval，专门测试模型的真实视频理解与领域知识推理能力。 • First large-scale training corpus (315K examples, 145K videos) focused specifically on knowledge- and reasoning-intensive video understanding across expert domains. • A human-in-the-loop pipeline that gradually ramps up reasoning difficulty, ensuring questions can't be answered by text shortcuts alone — the model must actually understand the video. • A new evaluation benchmark (VideoKR-Eval) with expert-annotated questions that test genuine video comprehension and domain knowledge, not just pattern matching.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究人员使用VideoKR对视频语言模型（包括Qwen2.5-VL-7B）进行了"SFT→GRPO"两阶段后训练，并在自建的VideoKR-Eval专家评测集以及多个通用视频推理基准（如Video-MME、MVBench、EgoSchema、TemporalBench）上进行了全面评测。他们将结果与当前最优的后训练方法进行了对比，并通过消融实验逐一验证数据流水线各组成部分的贡献。 The researchers post-trained video language models (including Qwen2.5-VL-7B) on VideoKR using a standard SFT→GRPO pipeline. They evaluated models on VideoKR-Eval (their new expert benchmark) and a suite of existing general video reasoning benchmarks including Video-MME, MVBench, EgoSchema, and TemporalBench. They compared against prior state-of-the-art post-training approaches to measure both knowledge-intensive and general video reasoning performance, and ran ablation studies to isolate the contribution of each component of their data pipeline.
📊 实验
结果		 在知识密集型视频推理基准VideoKR-Eval上，经VideoKR训练的模型超越了同等规模的所有先前后训练方法，达到最优水平。在通用视频推理基准（Video-MME、MVBench等）上，模型表现依然具有竞争力，没有出现"顾此失彼"的性能下降。消融实验表明，技能导向的数据设计和人在回路的质量控制是性能提升的两大关键因素。 Models trained on VideoKR outperform prior post-training approaches on knowledge-intensive video reasoning benchmarks. On VideoKR-Eval, VideoKR-trained models achieve state-of-the-art performance among post-trained models of comparable scale. On general video benchmarks, VideoKR-trained models remain competitive — for example, achieving strong scores on Video-MME and MVBench without sacrificing general ability. Ablation studies confirm that both the skill-oriented data design and the human-in-the-loop quality control are key contributors to the performance gains.
🏁 结论 VideoKR证明了：精心设计的、富含专业知识的训练数据——而非单纯更大的模型——才是让AI真正看懂并推理专业视频的核心驱动力。该数据集和评测基准为未来视频推理研究奠定了坚实基础。 VideoKR proves that carefully designed, knowledge-rich training data — not just bigger models — is the real key to making AI reason deeply about expert-domain videos. The dataset and benchmark together provide a solid foundation for future research in video reasoning.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队建立了一个有人类专家参与的"智能数据工厂"。首先，他们收集了14.5万段来自医学、科学、工程等专业领域的CC授权视频。然后设计了一套流水线，自动生成从"基础观察"到"复杂多步推理"逐级递进的问题和逐步思考的答案（即思维链）。人类审核员会检查每道题，确保它真的需要"看懂视频"才能作答，且推理步骤正确无误。最后，他们用这套数据分两阶段训练视频AI模型：先做监督微调（SFT），再用强化学习（GRPO）奖励模型通过真正推理得出正确答案。 The team built a smart data factory with human experts in the loop. They first collected 145K videos from expert domains (like medicine, science, and engineering) that are CC-licensed and freely usable. Then, they designed a pipeline that generates questions and step-by-step reasoning answers (called Chain-of-Thought rationales) at progressively harder difficulty levels — starting from basic observation up to complex multi-step inference. Human reviewers checked each example to make sure the question genuinely required watching the video and that the reasoning chain was correct. Finally, they trained video AI models on this new dataset using a two-stage recipe: first supervised fine-tuning (SFT), then a reinforcement-learning step (GRPO) that rewards the model for getting the right answer through genuine reasoning.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

的问题		 现有的AI测试大多只考察模型能否一次性答对问题，或者在短时间内完成简单任务。但真实的科学和工程工作不是这样的——它需要反复做实验、看结果、调整方案、再试一次，就像工程师每天的工作节奏。目前没有任何测试能衡量AI是否具备这种"持续迭代改进"的能力。 Most AI benchmarks test whether a model can answer a question in one shot or complete a short task quickly. 的亮点		 • AutoLab是首个专门针对"超长时域"AI任务的基准，要求智能体在固定时间预算内持续优化方案，而非给出一次性答案。 • 涵盖四大领域的36个真实专家设计任务：系统优化、谜题与挑战、模型开发、CUDA内核优化。 • 关键发现：决定成功的不是智能体第一次尝试的质量，而是它是否能坚持反复实验、测量、改进——"坚持"比"聪明"更重要。 • AutoLab is the first benchmark specifically designed for "ultra long-horizon" AI tasks, where agents must keep improving a solution over an extended time budget — not just give one answer. • It covers 36 realistic, expert-designed tasks across four domains: system optimization, puzzles & challenges, model development, and CUDA kernel optimization. • A key finding: what predicts success is not how good an agent's first attempt is, but whether it keeps experimenting, measuring, and improving — persistence beats initial cleverness.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

AutoLab搭建了一个"闭环优化"环境：AI智能体从一个"能用但不够好"的工程方案出发，在固定的时钟时间预算内，反复尝试修改→运行测试→读取结果→根据反馈做下一轮改进，完全模拟人类工程师在截止日期前的工作方式。最终不只看智能体有没有改进方案，还看改进了多少、时间用得是否高效。 AutoLab sets up a closed-loop environment where an AI agent starts with a working-but-suboptimal solution to a real engineering problem. The agent is given a fixed wall-clock time budget and must repeatedly try changes, run tests to measure performance, read the results, and use that feedback to make the next improvement — exactly like a human engineer working under a deadline. The benchmark then measures not just whether the agent improved the solution, but how much it improved and how efficiently it used its time.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着AI越来越多地被用于真实科学研究、药物发现和软件工程，能够随时间持续改进方案——而不是只回答一次——至关重要。这篇论文为AI领域提供了衡量并弥补这一能力缺口的具体方法，是迈向真正能加速人类创新的AI的关键一步。 As AI is increasingly deployed to help with real scientific research, drug discovery, and software engineering, being able to keep improving a solution over time — not just answer once — is critical. This paper gives the AI field a concrete way to measure and fix this gap, which is a key step toward AI that can genuinely accelerate human innovation.
