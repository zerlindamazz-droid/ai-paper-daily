RREDCoT: Segment-Level Reward Redistribution for Reasoning Models RREDCoT：面向推理模型的段级奖励再分配方法

🧑‍🔬 Mykyta Ielanskyi, Kajetan Schweighofer, Lukas Aichberger, Sepp Hochreiter

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 让AI在推理每一步都能获得有效反馈，训练更高效
RREDCoT redistributes training rewards across reasoning steps, making AI learn smarter without extra computation.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一位篮球教练看比赛录像：不是赛后只说"我们赢了"或"我们输了"，而是把关键进攻和失误逐个标注出来，让球员知道哪些动作要强化、哪些要改正。RREDCoT对AI推理过程做的正是同一件事——精准标出哪些"思维动作"有效，让训练更有针对性。Think of it like a basketball coach reviewing game footage: instead of just saying "we won" or "we lost" at the end, the coach rewinds and marks exactly which plays were brilliant and which were mistakes — so players know precisely what to practice. RREDCoT does the same for AI reasoning steps, pinpointing the good moves so training is far more focused.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 当我们训练AI一步步推理时（就像做数学题要写出解题过程），AI只有在最后才能得到"分数"，它不知道哪些推理步骤是有用的、哪些是在浪费时间。这就像学生只知道期末总分，却不知道每道题的对错。现有的训练方法在处理这个问题时噪声大、不稳定，尤其是推理链很长的时候。 When we train AI models to reason step-by-step (like showing their work on a math problem), the AI only gets a "grade" at the very end — it doesn't know which thinking steps were helpful and which were wasteful. This is like a student getting a final exam score but never knowing which answers along the way were correct. Current training methods try to handle this but are noisy and unreliable, especially for long reasoning chains.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • RREDCoT不是在最后才给AI一个总分，而是识别出推理链中哪些片段真正关键，给这些步骤更高的奖励分数。 • 方法让模型"自己评价自己"，不需要额外生成样本，节省了大量计算资源。 • 有效降低了标准训练方法中"反馈噪声大"的问题，使训练过程更稳定、推理结果更准确。 • Instead of giving the AI one big reward at the end, RREDCoT figures out which parts of the reasoning chain actually mattered and gives those steps higher credit — all without running extra computations. • The model uses itself as a judge to estimate how valuable each reasoning segment is, making the process efficient and self-contained. • This approach reduces the "noisy feedback" problem that plagues standard training methods, leading to more stable and accurate learning.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者在数学推理基准测试集上评估了RREDCoT，这些数据集专门用来测试AI能否解决多步骤问题。他们将RREDCoT与流行的GRPO训练算法以及多种奖励归因方法（包括蒙特卡洛采样）进行了比较。实验还考察了不同的推理链分割方式（如按句子、按逻辑步骤）和不同的片段价值估计方式，以分析哪些设计选择最为关键。 The researchers tested RREDCoT on math reasoning benchmarks — standard test suites used to evaluate whether AI can solve multi-step problems. They compared it against the popular GRPO training algorithm and several other reward attribution methods including Monte Carlo sampling. The experiments varied how the reasoning chain is segmented (e.g., by sentence, by logical step) and how the model estimates segment value, to understand what design choices matter most.
📊 实验
结果		 在数学推理基准测试中，RREDCoT超越了GRPO基线和基于蒙特卡洛采样的奖励再分配方法。在不引入额外生成开销的前提下，模型在多步骤推理任务上取得了更高的准确率，兼顾了性能提升和计算效率。不同分割粒度下的实验结果表明，段级奖励分配始终优于词级或整体序列级的奖励分配方式。 RREDCoT outperformed the GRPO baseline and Monte Carlo-based reward redistribution methods on math reasoning benchmarks. The method achieved improved accuracy on multi-step reasoning tasks while requiring no additional generation overhead, demonstrating both better performance and greater efficiency. Specific gains varied by segmentation granularity, with segment-level redistribution consistently outperforming token-level or whole-sequence-level reward assignment.
🏁 结论 RREDCoT证明了：让推理模型自己为各推理步骤重新分配训练奖励，比仅在最终结果给出单一奖励更能带来稳定、高效的学习效果。这为训练前沿AI推理模型提供了一种实用且低成本的改进方案。 RREDCoT proves that letting a reasoning model redistribute its own training rewards — by identifying which thinking steps mattered most — leads to better and more stable learning than giving a single reward at the end. This offers a practical, low-cost improvement to how we train state-of-the-art AI reasoning models.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

RREDCoT把AI的推理过程切分成若干片段（类似"思维段落"），然后让模型自己估计每个片段对最终正确答案的贡献有多大。根据这些估计，重新分配奖励分数——对有用的片段加分，对无用的片段减分。关键在于，这个过程不需要额外生成任何新的回答，计算成本很低。相比之下，传统的蒙特卡洛采样法需要反复生成大量额外样本才能估计每步的价值，代价高昂。 RREDCoT works by splitting the AI's reasoning chain into segments (like paragraphs of thinking), and then asking the model itself to estimate how much each segment contributed to the final correct answer. It uses these estimates to redistribute the reward — bumping up the score for helpful segments and lowering it for unhelpful ones. Crucially, it does this without generating any extra responses, so it's computationally cheap. This is in contrast to Monte Carlo sampling, which would require generating many extra completions to estimate the value of each step.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着ChatGPT等"推理型"AI在科学、教育和软件开发中扮演越来越重要的角色，如何高效、准确地训练这些模型至关重要。RREDCoT让训练过程更聪明、更省钱，有望加速更强推理AI的研发与落地。 As AI systems like ChatGPT and other "reasoning" models become central to science, education, and software development, training them efficiently and accurately is critical. RREDCoT makes that training process smarter and cheaper, potentially accelerating how quickly better AI reasoners can be developed and deployed.

PC Layer: Polynomial Weight Preconditioning for Improving LLM Pre-Training PC层：用多项式权重预处理提升大语言模型预训练效果

🧑‍🔬 Senmiao Wang, Tiantian Fang, Haoran Zhang, Yushun Zhang et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 用多项式预处理层稳定LLM训练，零推理开销
A plug-in "PC layer" stabilizes LLM training by reshaping weight matrices with polynomial math, adding zero inference cost.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像在演奏会前给吉他调音：PC层自动调紧或放松每根琴弦（权重），让它们保持平衡——演奏（训练）的效果更好。演奏结束后，你只需正常弹奏，调音的额外工作消失了，但音色的提升留了下来。Think of it like tuning a guitar before a concert: the PC layer automatically tightens or loosens each string (weight) so they're all in balance — the music (training) sounds better. When the concert is over, you just play normally; the tuning effort disappears but the improved sound stays.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练大语言模型（LLM）时，模型内部的权重矩阵很容易变得"病态"——就像一个严重失衡的跷跷板，某些方向的信号被无限放大，另一些方向却完全被忽略。这种不平衡会让训练变得不稳定、收敛慢，甚至导致模型效果变差。 Training large language models (LLMs) is notoriously unstable — the weight matrices inside the model can become "ill-conditioned," meaning some directions in the data get amplified wildly while others are ignored. This is like trying to balance a seesaw where one side is a thousand times heavier than the other. Poor conditioning slows down learning and can cause training to diverge or produce worse models.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 创新的"PC层"通过低阶多项式预处理器包裹每个权重矩阵，让其奇异值（衡量权重平衡性的指标）在整个训练过程中保持在健康范围内。• 训练完成后，额外的预处理数学计算可以合并回原始权重，部署的模型与原始架构大小和速度完全相同，零推理开销。• 理论上证明了：在深度线性网络中，均匀约束各层奇异值能保证梯度下降以几何速度收敛到全局最优解。 • Novel "PC layer" wraps each weight matrix in a low-degree polynomial preconditioner that keeps its singular values (a measure of balance) in a healthy range throughout training. • After training is done, the extra math can be folded back into the original weights — so the deployed model is exactly the same size and speed as before. • Theoretical proof shows that keeping singular values uniformly bounded guarantees fast (geometric) convergence to the best solution for deep linear networks.
🧪 实验
内容		 作者在FineWeb-Edu数据集上从头预训练了一个Llama-1B模型（10亿参数的标准语言模型架构）。他们将PC层与标准Transformer基线进行对比，使用了两种优化器：AdamW（最常见的LLM优化器）和Muon（一种更新的二阶风格优化器）。实验跟踪了训练损失曲线，并在一系列语言理解基准测试上评估了最终模型的下游任务质量。 The authors pre-trained a Llama-1B model (a standard 1-billion-parameter language model architecture) from scratch using the FineWeb-Edu dataset. They compared the PC layer against the standard Transformer baseline using two optimizers: AdamW (the most common LLM optimizer) and Muon (a newer second-order-style optimizer). They tracked training loss curves and evaluated the final models on a suite of language understanding benchmarks to measure downstream task quality.
📊 实验
结果		 使用AdamW时，PC层相比标准Transformer基线降低了训练损失。配合Muon优化器，PC层取得了更强的性能提升，表明两者具有互补效果。在下游语言基准测试中，PC层模型持续优于各自的基线。具体而言，在1B参数Llama模型上，PC层在整个训练过程中实现了可测量的损失下降，并在ARC、HellaSwag、MMLU等任务上取得了更高的准确率。 Using AdamW, the PC layer reduced training loss compared to the standard Transformer baseline. With the Muon optimizer, the PC layer achieved even stronger gains, demonstrating complementary benefits. On downstream language benchmarks, PC layer models consistently outperformed their respective baselines. While the paper reports specific loss curves and benchmark scores, representative improvements include measurable loss reductions across training steps and improved accuracy on tasks such as ARC, HellaSwag, and MMLU for the 1B-parameter Llama model.
🏁 结论 本文证明了用简单的多项式预处理器包裹LLM权重矩阵，能在不增加任何推理开销的前提下稳定训练过程并提升模型最终质量。研究既提供了一个实用、易于集成的技术，也建立了理论基础，说明了为什么控制权重条件数对深度网络优化至关重要。 This paper proves that wrapping LLM weight matrices in a simple polynomial preconditioner keeps training stable and improves final model quality — with zero cost at inference time. It establishes both a practical, easy-to-add technique and a theoretical foundation showing why controlling weight conditioning matters for deep network optimization.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

PC层在模型的每个权重矩阵外面套上一个小巧的数学"包装器"。这个包装器使用低阶多项式函数（类似一个简单的曲线公式）来重塑权重矩阵的行为——具体来说，它挤压权重矩阵的"奇异值"（可以理解为矩阵的伸缩因子），让它们保持平衡，不会爆炸性增大或缩减到零。训练期间，梯度通过多项式包装器和核心权重共同流动；训练结束后，包装器被数学合并回权重，推理时不产生任何额外计算。作者还将该方法与两种流行的优化器——AdamW和更新的Muon——配合使用，证明了其广泛兼容性。 The PC layer adds a small mathematical "wrapper" around each weight matrix in the model. This wrapper uses a low-degree polynomial function (like a simple curved formula) to reshape how the weight matrix behaves — specifically, it squeezes the "singular values" (think of these as the stretch factors of the matrix) so they stay balanced and don't blow up or shrink to zero. During training, gradients flow through both the polynomial wrapper and the core weight; afterwards, the wrapper is mathematically absorbed back into the weight, leaving no extra computation at inference time. The authors also pair this approach with two popular optimizers — AdamW and the newer Muon — to show it's broadly compatible.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

训练大型AI模型需要花费数百万美元的算力——任何能让训练更稳定、更高效的技术都具有巨大的现实价值。由于PC层在推理时零开销，它是一种"免费午餐"式的改进，构建LLM的公司可以直接采用，完全不需要改动已部署的产品。 Training large AI models costs millions of dollars in compute — any technique that makes training more stable and efficient has enormous real-world value. Because the PC layer adds zero overhead at inference time, it's a "free lunch" improvement that companies building LLMs could adopt without changing their deployed products at all.

Pretraining Recurrent Networks without Recurrence 无需递归的循环网络预训练

🧑‍🔬 Akarsh Kumar, Phillip Isola

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 让循环神经网络摆脱时序反向传播，实现并行高效预训练
A new training method lets recurrent networks learn without the painful sequential backpropagation that usually slows and breaks them.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy想象你要教一个新员工记会议纪要：与其让他从头到尾坐完所有会议、自己摸索该记什么，不如先给他看一份专家整理好的完美摘要，让他照着练习。SMT就是这个思路：用聪明的"专家"（Transformer）告诉RNN每一步的记忆应该长什么样，再让RNN按图索骥地学会怎么做到。Imagine teaching someone to take great meeting notes by first showing them a perfectly written summary made by an expert, then asking them to practice matching it — instead of making them sit through every meeting from scratch and figuring out what matters on their own. That's SMT: use a smart "expert" (Transformer) to show the RNN exactly what good memory looks like, then let the RNN learn to do it step by step.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 循环神经网络（RNN）非常适合处理文本、音频或视频这类序列数据，因为它们能把"记忆"从一步传递到下一步。但训练它们却很头疼：标准方法必须一步一步地走完整个序列，速度极慢。更糟的是，学习信号在长序列中要么消失、要么爆炸，导致RNN很难把序列开头的信息和结尾联系起来——就像读了一本长篇小说，却记不住第一章的细节。 Recurrent neural networks (RNNs) are great at processing sequences — like text or video — because they carry a "memory" from one step to the next. But training them is a nightmare: the standard method (called backpropagation through time) has to go step-by-step through the whole sequence, making it extremely slow. Worse, signals used for learning tend to either vanish or blow up over long sequences, so RNNs struggle to connect distant pieces of information — like remembering a detail from the beginning of a long story to answer a question at the end.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 巧妙解耦：把"该记什么"和"怎么更新记忆"分成两个独立问题，先用Transformer搞清楚该记什么，再单独训练RNN怎么存储。 • 无需展开时序：训练时RNN不再需要沿时间轴展开，梯度信号只需走一步，永远稳定，不会消失或爆炸。 • 完全并行训练：每一步记忆更新彼此独立，可以同时训练所有步骤，速度堪比Transformer。 • 适用多种架构：方法在Mamba、Hawk、GRU等多种主流RNN架构上都取得了提升，覆盖语言建模和图像序列任务。 • Clever separation: Instead of training the RNN directly, SMT splits the job into two parts — first figure out WHAT needs to be remembered, then separately teach the RNN HOW to update its memory. • No unrolling needed: The RNN never has to be "unrolled" through time during training, so the gradient (learning signal) travels only one step — always stable, never vanishing or exploding. • Fully parallel training: Because each memory update step is independent, all steps in a sequence can be trained at the same time, just like Transformers — giving huge speed benefits. • Works on real architectures: The method improves multiple existing RNN designs (like Mamba, Hawk, and GRU) on language and image sequence tasks.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者在多种RNN架构（Mamba、Hawk、Griffin、GRU）上，将SMT与标准BPTT训练进行对比。实验涵盖语言建模任务（使用大规模文本数据集SlimPajama）和图像像素级序列建模任务（使用ImageNet64）。评估指标包括语言模型困惑度（perplexity）和图像序列的每字节比特数（bits-per-byte），模型规模最大达到13亿参数，比较SMT预训练与纯BPTT训练的效果。 The researchers tested SMT against standard BPTT training on several RNN architectures including Mamba, Hawk, Griffin, and GRU. Experiments covered language modeling on the SlimPajama dataset (a large text corpus) and pixel-level image sequence modeling on ImageNet64. They evaluated model perplexity (a standard measure of how well a language model predicts text) and bits-per-byte for image sequences, at scales up to 1.3 billion parameters, comparing SMT-pretrained models to those trained purely with BPTT.
📊 实验
结果		 SMT在所有测试的RNN架构上均一致优于BPTT预训练。在语言建模任务上，SMT预训练的Mamba在各个模型规模下均取得更低的困惑度。在SlimPajama数据集的13亿参数规模实验中，SMT相对BPTT基线有显著的困惑度改善。在ImageNet64图像像素序列建模上，SMT训练的模型也取得了更低的每字节比特数。SMT预训练的模型在专门测试长距离记忆的任务上表现出明显更强的长程依赖捕捉能力。同时，由于完全并行的时间轴训练，SMT相比顺序执行的BPTT还大幅缩短了实际训练时间。 SMT consistently outperformed BPTT pretraining across all tested RNN architectures. On language modeling, SMT-pretrained Mamba achieved lower perplexity than BPTT-trained Mamba at every model size tested. At the 1.3B parameter scale on SlimPajama, SMT showed meaningful improvements in perplexity over BPTT baselines. On ImageNet64 pixel sequence modeling, SMT-trained models also achieved lower bits-per-byte than their BPTT counterparts. Notably, SMT-pretrained models showed substantially better long-range dependency capture, with improvements in tasks specifically designed to test memory over long distances. SMT also enables faster training due to full time-parallelism, reducing the wall-clock training time compared to sequential BPTT.
🏁 结论 SMT证明了一件反直觉的事：训练循环神经网络最好的方式，竟然是完全不用递归方式去训练它——而是借助Transformer生成理想的记忆目标作为监督信号。这从根本上改变了RNN的训练和扩展方式，有望让RNN在长序列任务上与Transformer一较高下，同时保留其高效推理的天然优势。 SMT proves that you can train powerful recurrent networks faster and better by never actually training them recurrently — instead using a Transformer to generate ideal memory targets as supervision. This fundamentally changes how RNNs can be scaled and trained, potentially letting them compete with Transformers on long-sequence tasks while retaining their efficient inference advantage.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

SMT分两个阶段工作。第一阶段，用一个Transformer（它擅长一次性看完整个序列）来计算序列中每个位置理想的"记忆摘要"——只保留预测未来所必需的信息，就像一个聪明的编辑标注出哪些过去的内容最重要。第二阶段，这些摘要变成标签，RNN用简单的监督学习来训练：给定当前记忆和新输入，预测下一步的记忆应该是什么。因为每次只走一步，学习信号永远不需要穿越很长的距离，梯度问题彻底消失。整个过程也不需要展开时间轴，所有时间步可以并行处理。 SMT works in two stages. First, a Transformer (which is good at looking at the whole sequence at once) is trained to figure out the perfect "memory summary" at each point in the sequence — capturing only what's needed to predict the future. Think of this as a smart editor highlighting exactly which past sentences are relevant. Second, those highlighted summaries become labels, and the RNN is trained with simple supervised learning: given the current memory and the new input, predict what the memory should look like next. Because each update is just one step, the gradient never has to travel far, eliminating vanishing/exploding gradient problems. The RNN also never needs to be unrolled, so all time steps can be processed in parallel — just like training a Transformer.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[(m_t, x_{t+1}) \rightarrow m_{t+1}\]
💡 SMT的核心监督学习目标：给定当前时刻的记忆状态$m_t$和下一个输入$x_{t+1}$，预测下一时刻的理想记忆$m_{t+1}$。这个标签由Transformer预先算好，RNN只需学会这一步转换，无需展开整个序列。 The core supervised learning target of SMT: given the current memory state $m_t$ and the next input token $x_{t+1}$, predict what the ideal next memory $m_{t+1}$ should be. These labels are pre-computed by a Transformer, so the RNN only needs to learn this single-step transition — no sequence unrolling required.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

RNN在处理长序列（如长文档、视频流）时比Transformer便宜得多，但训练难题一直制约着它的发展。SMT有望开启新一代既高效运行又训练充分的AI模型，这对于在算力有限的设备（如手机或边缘硬件）上部署AI具有重要实际意义。 RNNs are much cheaper to run than Transformers for long sequences (like processing long documents or video), but their training difficulties have held them back. SMT could unlock a new generation of efficient AI models that are both fast to run AND properly trained — which matters for deploying AI on devices with limited compute, like phones or edge hardware.
