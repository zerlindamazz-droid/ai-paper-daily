EvoArena: Tracking Memory Evolution for Robust LLM Agents in Dynamic Environments EvoArena：追踪记忆演化以构建动态环境中的鲁棒LLM智能体

🧑‍🔬 Jundong Xu, Qingchuan Li, Jiaying Wu, Yihuai Lan et al.

图 Figure · Page 4 of paper

🎯 让AI智能体像人一样记住"世界在变"，而不是活在过去
A benchmark and memory system that teaches AI agents to adapt as the world keeps changing.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个新员工入职后，公司规定每个月都在变——聪明的员工会把每次变化都记在本子上（"上个月规定A，这个月改成B了"），而不是每次都把本子撕掉重写。EvoMem就是给AI配备了这样一本"变化日志本"，让它知道世界是怎么一步步变成现在这样的。Imagine a new employee whose company changes its rules every month. A smart employee keeps a running changelog notebook — "last month it was rule A, this month it changed to rule B" — instead of tearing out the old pages each time. EvoMem gives AI agents exactly that kind of changelog notebook, so they always know how the world got to where it is today.

❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的AI智能体在考试中表现很好，但这些考试都假设"世界是静止不变的"。现实中，软件会更新、规则会改变、人的喜好也会变——AI却常常还用旧知识来回答新问题，就像一个员工从不看公司最新通知，永远按老规矩办事。目前没有好的方法来测试和训练AI应对这种"世界在变化"的能力。 Most AI agents are tested in frozen, unchanging scenarios — but the real world never stops changing. Software gets updated, social norms shift, and terminal commands evolve. Current AI agents have no good way to track these changes, so they keep using outdated knowledge and make mistakes. There's also no standard benchmark to measure how well agents handle an evolving environment.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 提出EvoArena：首个专门测试AI智能体应对"动态变化"能力的基准，涵盖终端命令、软件操作、社交偏好三大领域 • 提出EvoMem：一种"补丁式记忆"机制，像软件更新日志一样记录知识的变化历史，而不是直接覆盖旧记忆 • 发现当前最强AI智能体在动态环境下平均准确率只有39.6%，揭示了行业中被忽视的重大短板 • EvoMem不仅提升了新基准的表现，还在已有的GAIA、LoCoMo等经典基准上也有提升，说明其通用性强 • Introduces EvoArena, the first benchmark designed to test AI agents across progressively changing environments in three real-world domains • Proposes EvoMem, a "patch-based" memory that logs knowledge changes as structured update histories — like a changelog — instead of overwriting old facts • Reveals that today's best agents score only 39.6% on dynamic tasks, exposing a major blind spot in current AI evaluation • EvoMem also boosts performance on established benchmarks (GAIA, LoCoMo), showing it generalizes beyond just evolving scenarios

内容		 研究者构建了EvoArena基准，包含终端、软件、社交偏好三个领域的动态变化任务，并与GAIA、LoCoMo等已有基准对比。实验测试了多种主流LLM智能体（包括RAG等常见记忆方法作为基线），评估指标包括单任务准确率和连续子任务的"链级准确率"。同时进行了机制分析，通过检查记忆内容来理解EvoMem为何有效。 The researchers built EvoArena with tasks across three evolving domains and tested multiple mainstream LLM agents using common memory methods (like RAG) as baselines. ⚙️ Method · 方法详解

EvoMem的核心思路是：不要删除旧记忆，而是用"补丁"的方式把新变化叠加上去，同时保留变化的历史轨迹。这就好比软件更新日志——你不会扔掉旧版本说明书，而是记录"从版本A到版本B改了什么"。当AI需要回答问题时，它可以沿着这条演化轨迹推理，理解"事情是如何一步步变成现在这样的"。EvoArena则设计了三类逐步演化的任务（终端命令、软件操作、社交偏好），每个任务都是一系列连锁更新，测试AI能否跟上这些变化并完成连续任务。 EvoMem works like a software changelog: instead of erasing old memory when something changes, it writes a "patch" that records exactly what changed and how. The AI keeps a structured history of updates, so when answering a question, it can trace back through the evolution of facts — understanding not just "what is true now" but "how things got here." EvoArena tests agents on three domains (terminal commands, software tools, social preferences) where tasks arrive as progressive waves of updates, and agents must complete chains of related subtasks by correctly tracking all the changes.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{Chain Accuracy} = \frac{\text{# fully correct task chains}}{\text{# total task chains}}\]
💡 链级准确率：衡量AI能否连续完成一整串相关演化子任务，全部答对才算成功，比单题正确率更严格 Chain-level accuracy: measures whether an agent can correctly complete an entire sequence of related evolving subtasks — all or nothing, stricter than single-task accuracy

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

现实世界中的AI助手（如客服机器人、编程助手）每天都面对不断更新的软件、政策和用户偏好，如果AI不能跟上这些变化，就会给出过时甚至错误的建议。这项研究提供了测试和改进AI"跟上变化"能力的工具，是让AI真正可信赖地走进日常生活的重要一步。 AI assistants used in real products — like coding helpers or customer service bots — face constantly shifting software, rules, and preferences. If they can't adapt, they give outdated or wrong answers. This work gives the field a way to measure and fix that blind spot, making AI agents more trustworthy in real deployment.

MiniMax Sparse Attention MiniMax 稀疏注意力机制

🧑‍🔬 Xunhao Lai, Weiqi Xu, Yufeng Yang, Qiaorui Chen et al.

图 Figure · Page 1 of paper

🎯 用稀疏注意力让大模型处理百万token上下文快14倍
Sparse attention cuts LLM long-context compute by 28x while matching full-attention quality.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个聪明的学生在考试前不死记硬背整本教材，而是先快速浏览目录和重点标注，只精读最相关的章节——MSA让AI模型也学会了"有重点地阅读"，而不是每次都把所有内容重读一遍，速度自然快了十几倍。Think of it like a speed-reader who skims the chapter titles and highlights of a 1,000-page book before diving in — instead of reading every single word, they zoom in only on the most relevant pages. MSA teaches the AI to do exactly that, skipping over 96% of the text that doesn't matter and focusing only on what's truly important.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的大语言模型在处理超长文本时面临巨大的计算压力。每当上下文长度翻倍，计算量就会翻四倍（即所谓"二次方代价"），这使得在真实场景中处理几十万甚至上百万字的文档变得极其昂贵甚至不可能。现实应用如AI助手处理长篇代码库、持续记忆或复杂任务流，都迫切需要突破这个瓶颈。 Current AI language models struggle badly when the text they need to read gets very long. The problem is that the core "attention" mechanism has a quadratic cost — double the text length and the compute quadruples. This makes handling hundreds of thousands or millions of words (like an entire codebase or a book) wildly expensive at real-world deployment scale. Practical AI use cases like coding assistants, long-document agents, and persistent memory all desperately need a cheaper solution.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 提出了一种"分块稀疏注意力"机制（MSA），模型不再对所有文字一视同仁，而是智能挑选最相关的信息块来计算注意力，大幅降低计算量。 • 设计了一个轻量级"索引分支"，可以为每个查询组独立打分并选出最重要的Top-k信息块，同时保持高效的块级GPU执行效率。 • 与GPU底层执行路径联合设计，使用无需指数运算的Top-k选择和KV外积稀疏注意力，最大化张量核心利用率，将理论加速转化为实际墙钟时间提升。 • Introduces MiniMax Sparse Attention (MSA): instead of reading every word equally, the model smartly picks only the most relevant chunks of text to focus on, slashing compute by over 28x at 1M-token context. • A lightweight "Index Branch" scores and selects the top-k most relevant key-value blocks per query group independently, enabling fine-grained sparse retrieval without sacrificing block-level GPU efficiency. • Co-designed with a custom GPU kernel using exp-free Top-k selection and KV-outer sparse attention, turning theoretical compute savings into real wall-clock speedups of 14x on prefill and 7.6x on decoding.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在一个拥有1090亿参数、支持原生多模态的生产级大模型上进行，上下文长度最长测试到100万个token。将MSA与标准的GQA（分组查询注意力）进行对比，评估维度包括：各类主流语言理解和推理benchmark的准确率、预填充和解码阶段的实际墙钟时间速度、以及注意力计算量（FLOPs）的减少比例。硬件平台为H800 GPU集群。 Experiments were run on a production-scale 109-billion-parameter natively multimodal model, tested at context lengths up to 1 million tokens. MSA was compared against standard Grouped Query Attention (GQA) as the baseline. Evaluations covered: accuracy on a wide range of standard language and reasoning benchmarks, real-world wall-clock speed for both the prefill (reading) and decoding (generating) phases, and theoretical compute reduction measured in FLOPs. All speed tests were conducted on NVIDIA H800 GPUs.
📊 实验
结果		 • 在100万token上下文下，每个token的注意力计算量减少了28.4倍，同时在各类benchmark上与标准GQA性能相当（无明显精度损失）。 • 使用联合设计的GPU内核，预填充（输入处理）阶段实现了14.2倍的实际加速，解码（输出生成）阶段实现了7.6倍的实际加速。 • 该方案已成功部署于公开发布的生产级多模态模型MiniMax-M3。 • At 1 million token context length, per-token attention compute is reduced by 28.4x compared to standard GQA, with no meaningful drop in accuracy across standard benchmarks. • With the co-designed GPU kernel on H800 hardware, prefill (input processing) achieves a 14.2x wall-clock speedup, and decoding (text generation) achieves a 7.6x wall-clock speedup. • The system has been validated at production scale on the publicly released MiniMax-M3 multimodal model (109B parameters).
🏁 结论 本文证明了稀疏注意力机制可以在不损失模型质量的前提下，将百万token上下文的注意力计算量压缩28倍以上，并通过联合设计的GPU内核将这一理论优势转化为真实的14倍预填充加速。这为在生产环境中部署超长上下文大语言模型提供了切实可行的工程路径。 This paper proves that sparse attention can cut the compute cost of 1-million-token contexts by over 28x without hurting model quality, and that a carefully co-designed GPU kernel can translate this into a real 14x speedup in practice. It provides a production-ready engineering blueprint for deploying ultra-long-context LLMs at scale.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

MSA的核心思路是：不让模型对每一个词都"平等关注"，而是先快速扫描所有信息块并打分，然后只精细处理最相关的Top-k块。具体来说，有一个轻量级的"索引分支"负责给所有Key-Value信息块快速打分、排序，从中选出最重要的一小部分；然后"主分支"只在这些被选中的块上做精确的注意力计算，完全跳过不相关的部分。为了让这个方案在GPU上真正跑得快（而不只是理论上少了计算），作者还专门设计了配套的GPU内核，使用不需要指数运算的Top-k选择以及KV外积稀疏注意力，提高了GPU张量核心的利用率，让理论上的计算量减少直接转化为实际速度提升。 MSA works by splitting attention into two stages: a fast "scout" and a precise "reader." First, a lightweight Index Branch quickly scans all key-value blocks and scores their relevance, selecting only the top-k most important blocks for each query group. Then the Main Branch performs full, exact attention computation — but only on those selected blocks, completely skipping everything else. To make sure these savings translate into real speed on actual GPUs (not just on paper), the team co-designed a custom GPU kernel. This kernel uses an exp-free Top-k algorithm and a special KV-outer sparse attention layout that keeps the GPU's tensor cores busy and efficient, even when processing non-contiguous memory blocks.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V\]
💡 标准注意力公式：Q（查询）与所有K（键）做点积打分，再加权求V（值）的加权和。其计算复杂度与序列长度的平方成正比，这是长上下文计算爆炸的根源。 The standard attention formula: query Q scores against all keys K, then takes a weighted sum of values V. Its cost grows with the square of sequence length — the root cause of the long-context compute explosion MSA is designed to fix.
\[\text{MSA}: \text{select Top-}k \text{ blocks} \rightarrow \text{Attention}(Q, K_{\text{top-}k}, V_{\text{top-}k})\]
💡 MSA的核心思想：先用索引分支为所有KV块打分，只保留Top-k个最相关的块，再在这个极小的子集上做精确注意力计算，将计算量从O(n²)降至O(n·k)。 MSA's key idea: score all KV blocks with the Index Branch, keep only the top-k most relevant ones, then run exact attention only on that tiny subset — reducing cost from O(n²) to O(n·k).

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

超长上下文能力正在成为AI助手、编程工具和企业应用的核心需求，但高昂的计算成本是最大障碍。MSA让处理百万字级别的文档变得切实可行，将直接推动AI在医疗记录分析、法律文书审查、超长代码库理解等高价值场景中的广泛落地。 The ability to process very long texts is a must-have for next-generation AI assistants, coding tools, and enterprise applications — but the cost has been prohibitive. MSA makes million-token AI processing genuinely affordable, potentially unlocking AI use cases in medical records, legal documents, and massive codebases that were previously impractical.

Redesign Mixture-of-Experts Routers with Manifold Power Iteration 用流形幂迭代重新设计混合专家路由器

🧑‍🔬 Songhao Wu, Ang Lv, Ruobing Xie, Yankai Lin

图 Figure · Page 5 of paper

🎯 让MoE路由器更精准地匹配专家，训练更高效的大模型
Smarter MoE routing by aligning router vectors with each expert's most expressive direction.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一家医院的分诊台（路由器）要把病人派给最合适的医生（专家）。以前分诊台凭感觉派单，现在研究者给每位医生做了一张超精准的"专长名片"，分诊台只需对照名片就能精准派单，既不浪费医生资源，也让病人得到最好的治疗。Imagine a hospital triage desk (the router) sending patients to the right specialist (the experts). Before, the desk was guessing based on vague descriptions. This paper gives each specialist a perfectly accurate "skill card," so the desk can instantly match every patient to the ideal doctor — no guessing, no wasted consultations.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 大型AI模型常用"混合专家（MoE）"架构——就像一个公司有很多专业部门，每次只调用最合适的几个来处理任务。但负责"派单"的路由器（Router）缺乏科学的设计原则，往往不能准确判断哪个专家最适合当前任务，导致资源浪费和效果下降。现有方法没有明确告诉路由器"你应该长什么样子才能最好地代表这个专家"。 Large AI models often use a "Mixture-of-Experts" (MoE) setup — think of it like a call center where different specialists handle different questions. A "router" decides which specialists to call each time. The problem is that no one told the router HOW to best represent each specialist, so its decisions are often imprecise, wasting computing power and hurting model quality.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次提出将路由器的每一行对齐到对应专家矩阵的"主奇异方向"（即最能代表该专家的数学方向），让路由更精准 • 提出"幂迭代后收缩"（Power-then-Retract）新范式，既提升路由对齐质量，又保证训练稳定性和计算效率 • 从理论上证明了MPI能让路由器收敛到专家的主奇异方向，并在1B到11B参数规模的模型上实验验证 • First principled design rule for MoE routers: align each router row with the expert's most expressive mathematical direction (principal singular vector) • New "Power-then-Retract" training paradigm that improves routing quality while keeping training stable and efficient • Theoretical proof that the method converges correctly, validated across model scales from 1B to 11B parameters
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者在1B、3B和11B参数规模的MoE语言模型上进行了预训练实验，使用标准语言建模数据集。他们将MPI方法与标准MoE基线（无特殊路由设计）以及其他路由改进方法进行对比，评估指标包括语言模型困惑度（perplexity）以及多个主流NLP下游基准测试的准确率。 The team pretrained MoE language models at three scales — 1B, 3B, and 11B parameters — using standard language modeling datasets. They compared their MPI method against a standard MoE baseline and other router improvement techniques, measuring performance via language model perplexity (lower = better) and accuracy on a suite of popular NLP benchmark tasks.
📊 实验
结果		 MPI在所有测试规模（1B、3B、11B）上均持续降低了语言模型困惑度，并在多个下游NLP基准任务上超越了标准MoE基线及其他路由改进方法。随着模型规模增大，改进效果依然稳定，证明该方法具有良好的可扩展性。具体数字因论文摘要未完整披露，但作者强调在所有规模上均观察到一致的性能提升。 MPI consistently reduced language model perplexity across all tested scales (1B, 3B, 11B) and outperformed both the standard MoE baseline and competing router improvement methods on multiple downstream NLP benchmarks. The gains held up as model size scaled, demonstrating robust scalability. While exact per-benchmark numbers are detailed in the full paper, the authors report consistent improvements at every scale tested.
🏁 结论 本文证明了为MoE路由器提供明确的数学设计原则（对齐专家主奇异方向）可以显著提升大模型的质量和效率。MPI方法简单、有理论保障、易于集成，为未来MoE模型设计提供了一个有力的新工具。 This paper proves that giving MoE routers a principled mathematical design rule — aligning them with each expert's principal singular direction — consistently improves model quality across scales. The MPI method is theoretically grounded, easy to integrate, and sets a new standard for how MoE routers should be designed.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究者发现，每个专家矩阵都有一个"最重要的方向"（主奇异方向），它最能概括这个专家的能力。他们提出让路由器的每一行尽量对齐这个方向，就像给每个专家做了一张"最精准的名片"。具体做法是"幂迭代后收缩"：先用幂迭代让路由器向量不断靠近专家的主方向，再通过一个收缩步骤限制向量的大小，防止训练过程中数值爆炸或不稳定。整个过程可以无缝嵌入现有MoE训练流程，几乎不增加额外计算开销。 The key insight is that every expert matrix has one "most important direction" — mathematically called the principal singular vector — that best summarizes what the expert does. The researchers propose making each router row point in that same direction, like giving every specialist a perfectly accurate business card. They do this with a two-step trick called "Power-then-Retract": first, a power iteration step nudges the router vector toward the expert's principal direction; then, a retraction step keeps the vector's size in check so training stays stable. This plug-in method slots into existing MoE training with minimal extra cost.

\[\mathbf{r}_i \leftarrow \text{Retract}\left(\mathbf{E}_i \mathbf{r}_i\right)\]

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

SpatialClaw的核心思路是：给AI配备一个"始终开着的Python编程环境"，里面预先加载好了输入的图像帧和一堆感知工具（比如深度估计、目标检测、几何计算等）。然后AI每次只写一小段代码（一个"单元格"），运行后可以看到文字和视觉结果，再根据这些中间信息决定下一段代码写什么。这就像一个侦探每查一条线索就更新自己的判断，而不是一开始就把所有推理写死。整个过程不需要重新训练AI模型，只需要更换"接口方式"就能大幅提升表现。 SpatialClaw gives the AI a persistent Python coding environment that stays "live" throughout the whole task. Loaded into this environment are the input images/video frames and a toolkit of perception and geometry functions (like depth sensing, object detection, and 3D math). At each step, the AI writes just one small block of code, runs it, and sees both text and visual outputs before writing the next block. This iterative, reactive approach means the AI can change its strategy mid-task based on what it discovers — much more flexible than planning everything upfront. No retraining of the underlying AI model is needed at all.

空间理解是机器人、自动驾驶、AR/VR等现实应用的基础能力，AI在这方面一直很弱。SpatialClaw展示了一种无需重新训练就能大幅提升空间推理能力的方法，意味着现有AI产品可以低成本快速升级，对整个行业具有实际价值。 Spatial understanding is essential for real-world AI applications like robots, self-driving cars, and augmented reality — all of which need to know where things are and how they move. SpatialClaw shows you can dramatically improve this capability without retraining expensive AI models, making it a practical and low-cost upgrade path for existing AI products.
