How LoRA Remembers? A Parametric Memory Law for LLM Finetuning LoRA 如何记忆？大语言模型微调的参数记忆定律

🧑‍🔬 Ziwen Xu, Haiwen Hong, Linsong Yu, Benglei Cui et al.

图 Figure · Page 4 of paper

🎯 揭示LoRA微调的记忆容量定律，并提出针对性优化策略提升记忆效率
A power law governs how much LoRA can memorize, enabling smarter fine-tuning via threshold-guided token-level optimization.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像给学生做练习题——以前老师不知道这个学生最多能记多少单词，只能盲目刷题。现在科学家发现了一条"记忆定律"，就像发现了这个学生的"大脑容量公式"，还设计了专门盯着"还没背会的单词"反复强化的刷题策略，让复习效率大幅提升。Think of it like a student cramming for an exam: before this paper, nobody knew how many facts the AI "student" could hold in its head, so training was just random cramming. Now scientists found a formula for the AI's "brain capacity," and built a smart study plan that focuses extra practice only on the facts the student keeps getting wrong — making memorization far more efficient.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 AI大模型需要不断学习新知识才能跟上真实世界的变化，但现有的"轻量微调"方法（如LoRA）究竟能记住多少东西、有没有上限，却从来没有人系统量化过。就像你想往一个杯子里倒水，却不知道杯子有多大——这让工程师无法合理分配训练资源，也无法预判模型什么时候会"记不住"。 AI models like ChatGPT need to keep learning new facts to stay up-to-date. A popular tool called LoRA lets engineers cheaply update these models — but nobody really knew how much information LoRA can actually hold, or what limits its memory. Without knowing the "size of the cup," it's hard to know how much water you can pour in, leading to wasted training effort.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次提出"参数记忆定律"：用数学公式（幂律）精确描述LoRA的记忆容量上限，就像物理学中的定律一样可预测。 • 发现token级别的"相变"现象：当模型预测某个词的概率超过0.5时，会发生质变，模型从"猜不准"直接跳变为"能完整背诵"。 • 提出MemFT优化策略：自动把训练资源向那些"还没记住的词"倾斜，像老师专门给薄弱知识点加练，提升记忆效率和保真度。 • First-ever "Parametric Memory Law": a clean math formula (power law) that predicts exactly how much LoRA can memorize given its size and training data length. • Discovery of a sharp phase transition at the token level: once a model's confidence on a word crosses 50%, it flips from "can't recall" to "perfect verbatim recall" — a crisp on/off switch. • MemFT optimization strategy: automatically redirects training budget toward words the model hasn't memorized yet, like a tutor who focuses extra practice on your weak spots.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验使用了多组受控记忆任务，系统改变LoRA的秩（rank）、训练数据量和序列长度，测量记忆损失（Delta L）。基线对比包括标准LoRA微调及不同参数配置。MemFT的效果则在记忆保真度（逐字召回率）和训练效率（达到记忆目标所需步数）两个维度上进行评估。 The researchers ran controlled memorization experiments, systematically varying LoRA's rank (its "size"), training data volume, and sequence length, measuring memory loss (ΔL) across many configurations. They compared MemFT against standard LoRA fine-tuning baselines under identical budgets. Evaluation focused on two things: verbatim recall accuracy (did the model reproduce text word-for-word?) and training efficiency (how many steps to reach a memory target?).
📊 实验
结果		 参数记忆定律在所有测试配置中均与实测结果高度吻合（R²接近1），验证了幂律的强鲁棒性。MemFT相比标准LoRA微调在记忆保真度上显著提升，在相同训练预算下可更快达到逐字召回目标，具体数字依配置不同提升幅度在数个百分点至十余个百分点之间；在token级相变分析中，p>0.5的充分条件在贪心解码下100%对应逐字召回。 The Parametric Memory Law fit observed results with near-perfect accuracy (R² close to 1) across all tested configurations, confirming the power law is robust. MemFT outperformed standard LoRA fine-tuning on memory fidelity, achieving verbatim recall targets faster under the same training budget — with improvements ranging from several to over ten percentage points depending on setup. The token-level phase transition finding was validated: p > 0.5 prediction probability was confirmed as a 100% sufficient condition for verbatim recall under greedy decoding.
🏁 结论 本文证明了LoRA的记忆能力可以用一条精确的幂律数学定律来描述，并在此基础上提出的MemFT策略能有效提升微调效率和记忆保真度，为大模型知识更新提供了理论基础和实用工具。 This paper proves that LoRA's memorization capacity follows a precise mathematical power law, and that smarter training (MemFT) guided by a token-level threshold can substantially improve how faithfully and efficiently LLMs absorb new knowledge.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队把LoRA当作一个"受控记忆探针"，系统地测量不同参数量和序列长度下的记忆效果，从中提炼出一条幂律公式，描述损失下降与参数量、序列长度的关系。他们还进一步分析每个词（token）的预测概率，发现0.5是一个关键门槛——过了这个门槛，模型就能逐字背诵。基于此，他们设计了MemFT：在训练过程中动态追踪哪些词还低于门槛，将更多"训练力气"集中在这些词上，直到它们被真正记住。 The team used LoRA as a controlled "memory probe" — systematically varying its size and the length of text it tries to memorize, then measuring how well it remembers. From many experiments, they derived a power-law formula linking memory quality to model parameters and text length. They also zoomed in to the word level, discovering that a prediction confidence of 50% is a magic threshold for perfect recall. Building on this, they built MemFT, which tracks which words are still below the threshold during training and dynamically shifts extra training effort toward those words — like giving harder flashcards more repetitions.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

知道模型"能记多少"就像知道硬盘容量，这对AI产品的设计和资源规划至关重要。MemFT让同样的计算成本能让模型记住更多，直接降低AI知识更新的成本，对医疗、法律等需要及时更新知识的场景尤其有价值。 Knowing how much an AI can memorize is like knowing a hard drive's capacity — it's essential for designing reliable AI products and planning compute budgets wisely. MemFT means the same training cost can teach AI more facts, cutting costs for applications like medical or legal AI that need frequent knowledge updates.

Unlocking the Working Memory of Large Language Models for Latent Reasoning 解锁大语言模型的工作记忆以实现隐式推理

🧑‍🔬 Lukas Aichberger, Sepp Hochreiter

图 Figure · Page 5 of paper

🎯 用"记忆块"替代思维链，让AI在脑中默想完成推理
RiM lets LLMs reason silently using memory blocks instead of writing out every thinking step.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一个数学高手做题时不需要把草稿写满整张纸——他可以在脑子里默算，直接写出答案。RiM就是给AI装上了这种"默算能力"，让它把中间思考"藏在心里"，而不是全部打出来。Imagine a math whiz who doesn't need to fill up scratch paper — they just think it through in their head and write the final answer. RiM gives AI that same superpower: reasoning quietly "in its head" using invisible memory notes, instead of typing out every single thinking step.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有AI模型（比如ChatGPT）在解决复杂问题时，通常需要把每一步思考过程都写出来，就像做数学题时必须把草稿纸上的每一步都打出来。这种方式既慢又浪费，而且把"思考过程"和"最终回答"混在了一起，不够高效。人类思考时并不总需要把每个念头都说出来——我们可以在脑子里默想，直接得出答案。AI目前还缺乏这种"内心独白"能力。 Today's AI models that reason well (like chain-of-thought systems) have to write out every single thinking step before giving an answer — like a student who must show ALL scratch work on paper before writing the final answer. This is slow and wasteful, and it blurs the line between "thinking" and "talking." Humans can hold thoughts internally without saying them out loud. Current AI lacks this kind of silent, internal reasoning ability.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 提出"记忆块"（Memory Blocks）概念：用固定的特殊符号序列充当AI的"工作记忆"，无需生成中间文字即可完成内部推理。 • 两阶段课程训练：先让记忆块预测显式推理步骤来"热身"，再丢掉监督信号、让模型自己迭代精炼答案，逐步解锁真正的隐式推理。 • 单次前向传播完成推理：由于记忆块是固定的而非逐词生成的，大幅提升计算效率，避免了自回归生成的时间开销。 • Introduces "memory blocks" — fixed sequences of special tokens that act like an AI's working memory, enabling internal reasoning without generating any visible text steps. • Uses a clever two-stage training curriculum: first teach the memory blocks to predict explicit reasoning steps, then remove that crutch and let the model refine answers on its own. • Because memory blocks are fixed (not generated token-by-token), the entire reasoning process runs in a single forward pass — much faster than writing out a chain of thought.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在多个主流推理基准上进行，包括数学推理（GSM8K、MATH）和逻辑推理任务。对比的基线包括标准的链式思维（Chain-of-Thought）方法、以及其他隐式推理方法（如Coconut等）。测试覆盖了不同厂商和不同规模的语言模型，评估指标主要是答题准确率，并同时衡量计算效率（前向传播次数）。 The team tested RiM on popular reasoning benchmarks including math word problems (GSM8K, MATH) and logical reasoning tasks. They compared it against standard chain-of-thought prompting and other latent/implicit reasoning methods like Coconut. Experiments covered multiple LLM families and model sizes to show generalizability. The main metric was answer accuracy, with additional attention to how many forward passes (compute steps) were needed.
📊 实验
结果		 RiM在多个推理基准上达到或超越了现有隐式推理方法（如Coconut）的准确率，同时完全避免了自回归思维链的生成开销。在GSM8K等数学基准上，RiM以单次前向传播完成推理，相较于需要多步自回归生成的方法显著降低了计算量，且在不同规模和不同系列的语言模型上均保持了一致的性能提升。 RiM matches or outperforms existing latent reasoning methods (including Coconut) across multiple reasoning benchmarks such as GSM8K and MATH, while requiring only a single forward pass instead of multiple autoregressive generation steps. The gains hold consistently across different LLM families and model sizes, demonstrating that the approach is broadly applicable rather than tuned for one specific model.
🏁 结论 本文证明了大语言模型可以通过"记忆块"这种隐式工作记忆机制完成有效推理，无需将每一步思考过程外化为文字。RiM为推理效率和质量的兼顾提供了新路径，是隐式推理方向的重要进展。 This paper proves that LLMs can be trained to reason effectively using internal memory blocks — without writing out any intermediate steps. RiM shows that silent, latent reasoning is not only possible but competitive with chain-of-thought methods, opening a more efficient path for AI reasoning.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

RiM的核心思路是：在AI回答问题之前，先插入几组"记忆块"——这些是特殊的占位符号，AI可以把中间想法"存"在里面，而不是写成文字。训练分两步：第一步，用真实的推理步骤文字来"教"记忆块该存什么，让它知道怎么用；第二步，去掉这些文字指导，让AI只靠记忆块自己反复精炼最终答案。由于记忆块是固定长度且不需要逐字生成，整个推理只需一次前向计算，就像直接"脑算"而不用在草稿纸上写字。 RiM inserts special placeholder tokens called "memory blocks" into the model before it generates an answer. Think of them as invisible sticky notes in the AI's head where it can jot down thoughts without showing them to you. Training happens in two stages: first, the model is taught to use these blocks by predicting real written reasoning steps after each one (training wheels); then the written steps are removed and the model learns to silently refine its answer using only the memory blocks. Since the blocks don't need to be generated one token at a time, the whole process is much faster — like thinking in your head instead of writing everything on a whiteboard.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{RiM: } [M_1] \rightarrow [M_2] \rightarrow \cdots \rightarrow [M_k] \rightarrow \hat{y}\]
💡 RiM的推理流程：模型依次通过k个记忆块（M₁到Mₖ），每个记忆块在内部积累推理信息，最终直接输出答案ŷ，无需生成中间文字步骤。 RiM's reasoning pipeline: the model passes through k memory blocks (M₁ to Mₖ) in sequence, accumulating internal reasoning at each step, then directly outputs the final answer ŷ — no written intermediate steps needed.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

对AI产品来说，推理效率直接关系到成本和响应速度——如果AI能"默想"而非"写出"每一步，就能更快、更便宜地回答复杂问题。长远来看，这项研究让AI的思维方式更接近人类，推动了对"AI如何真正理解问题"这一核心命题的探索。 For real-world AI products, faster and cheaper reasoning means smarter assistants that can handle complex questions without running up huge compute bills. More broadly, this research pushes AI closer to human-like thinking — reasoning quietly inside, not just narrating every step out loud.

Reinforcement Learning with Robust Rubric Rewards 基于鲁棒评分标准奖励的强化学习

🧑‍🔬 Ya-Qi Yu, Hao Wang, Fangyu Hong, Xiangyang Qu et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 用细粒度评分标准让视觉语言大模型训练更准确可靠
A smarter rubric-based reward system that makes vision-language AI training more accurate and reliable.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像参加烹饪比赛：以前裁判只说"过关/淘汰"，而RLR³给厨师一张详细评分表——"口味9分、摆盘7分、是否按菜谱做：是"——厨师知道哪里该改进。而且为了公平，裁判品菜时绝对不许偷看菜谱！Think of it like training for a cooking competition: instead of a judge just saying "pass or fail," RLR³ gives the chef detailed scorecards — "flavor: 9/10, presentation: 7/10, followed recipe: yes" — so the chef knows exactly what to improve. And to keep things fair, the judge is never allowed to peek at the recipe while tasting!
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有AI训练方法（用"可验证奖励"）在答案非对即错的任务上（如数学题）效果很好，但现实中很多任务（如看图说话、解释推理过程）需要从多个维度评分，没有唯一正确答案。现有系统要么评分太粗糙，要么被AI"钻空子"刷高分，导致训练出来的模型其实并没有真正变聪明。 Existing AI training methods using "verifiable rewards" work great when answers are clearly right or wrong — like math problems. But many real-world AI tasks (like describing an image or explaining reasoning steps) have multiple aspects to judge, and there's no single checkable answer. Current systems either oversimplify the grading or let the AI "game" the scoring system by exploiting loopholes, leading to inflated scores that don't reflect true performance.
💡 解决
的亮点		 - 将奖励验证从"任务级（对/错）"扩展到"标准级（多维评分）"，让训练反馈更细致丰富。 - 提出"最小暴露策略"，防止AI评分器看到不该看的答案，堵住作弊漏洞。 - 引入层次化聚合，让核心评分标准比附加标准更有权重，并解决了分组训练中分数扎堆的问题。 - 受控审计证明，该方法显著减少了虚假高分（假阳性）。 - Extends reward verification from task-level (pass/fail) to criterion-level (multi-aspect rubric scoring), enabling richer feedback during training. - Introduces a "minimal exposure" strategy that prevents the AI grader from accidentally seeing answers it shouldn't, reducing cheating/gaming. - Uses hierarchical aggregation to weight essential criteria more than bonus ones, and fixes score clustering problems in group-based training. - Confirmed through controlled audits that the method significantly reduces false positives (bogus high scores).
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者使用大型视觉语言模型Qwen3-VL-30B-A3B进行实验，在涵盖视觉问答、数学推理、图表理解、多步推理等15个不同基准测试上评估RLR³。对比基线包括原始基础模型和官方"指令到思维"版本。他们还专门做了受控审计实验，测量评分系统产生虚假高分（假阳性）的频率，以验证最小暴露策略和确定性验证的实际效果。 The researchers tested RLR³ by fine-tuning Qwen3-VL-30B-A3B, a large vision-language model, and evaluated it across 15 diverse benchmarks covering tasks like visual question answering, mathematical reasoning, chart understanding, and multi-step reasoning. They compared against the base model and the official "instruct-to-thinking" version as baselines. They also ran controlled audits specifically measuring how often the scoring system produced false positives (inflated scores), to verify that their minimal exposure and deterministic verification strategies actually work.
📊 实验
结果		 RLR³在全部15个基准测试上均优于RLVR基线。相比基础模型Qwen3-VL-30B-A3B提升了4.7分，甚至超过了官方基础模型与"指令思维"版本之间的性能差距——也就是说，RLR³的训练效果超过了Qwen官方精心调优的版本。受控审计还确认，确定性验证和最小暴露策略显著减少了评分中可被利用的假阳性。 RLR³ consistently outperformed RLVR baselines across all 15 benchmarks. It achieved a 4.7-point improvement over the base Qwen3-VL-30B-A3B model, and even surpassed the performance gap between the official base and instruct-to-thinking model variants — meaning RLR³ training alone exceeded what Qwen's own carefully curated fine-tuning achieved. Controlled audits confirmed that deterministic verification and minimal exposure strategies significantly reduced exploitable false positives in scoring.
🏁 结论 RLR³证明：在强化学习训练中，给AI提供详细的多维度评分反馈（而非简单的对错信号）能显著提升视觉语言模型的能力，在15个基准测试上超越了标准基线和官方精调版本。 RLR³ proves that giving AI models detailed, multi-criteria rubric-based feedback during reinforcement learning — rather than simple right/wrong signals — leads to stronger, more reliable vision-language performance, outperforming both standard baselines and official model fine-tunes across 15 benchmarks.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

RLR³给AI训练配备了一份详细的"评分细则"（就像老师的评分表），而不是简单地判断对错。对每条评分标准，系统会选择最合适的评分路径：能客观核查的（如"数对了几个物体"），用精确的确定性验证器；较主观的（如"推理是否清晰"），用AI裁判打分。为防止作弊，评分时对提取器隐藏正确答案、对裁判隐藏原始图片。最后，通过层次化方式汇总所有维度的分数——核心项比加分项权重更高——并修正分数集中在同一区间的问题。 RLR³ gives the AI a detailed "rubric" — like a teacher's grading checklist — instead of a single pass/fail score. For each criterion on the rubric, it picks the best evaluation path: if the criterion is objectively checkable (like "did the model count the objects correctly?"), it uses a precise deterministic verifier; if it's more subjective (like "is the reasoning clear?"), it uses an AI judge. To prevent gaming, it hides the correct answer from the extractor and hides the image from the judge, so neither can cheat. Finally, it combines all criterion scores in a smart hierarchy — must-pass items count more than bonus items — and adjusts scoring to avoid everyone getting the same grade.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[r = \text{HierAgg}\left(\{r_i^{\text{ess}}\}, \{r_j^{\text{add}}\}\right)\]
💡 最终奖励分数由层次化聚合函数计算，分别对必要标准分数和附加标准分数赋予不同权重后合并。 The final reward score is computed by a hierarchical aggregation function that combines scores from essential criteria and additional criteria with different weights.
\[r_i = \begin{cases} \text{DeterministicVerifier}(e_i, g_i) & \text{if verifiable} \\ \text{LLM-Judge}(c_i, o) & \text{if non-verifiable} \end{cases}\]
💡 对每条评分标准，系统根据其是否可客观核查，选择用确定性验证器或AI裁判来打分。 For each rubric criterion, the system routes to either a deterministic verifier (for objectively checkable items) or an LLM judge (for subjective items).

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

现实中大多数AI任务——如分析医疗图像、批改作文、回答复杂图片问题——都无法用简单的对错来评判。RLR³展示了如何用更丰富、更可靠的反馈训练AI，为开发真正理解复杂、多步骤任务的智能助手铺平了道路。 Most real-world AI tasks — like analyzing medical images, grading student essays, or answering complex questions about photos — can't be judged with a simple right/wrong check. RLR³ shows how to train AI systems with richer, more reliable feedback, paving the way for smarter assistants that genuinely understand nuanced, multi-step tasks.
