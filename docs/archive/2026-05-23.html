Probabilistic Tiny Recursive Model 概率微递归模型

🧑‍🔬 Amin Sghaier, Ali Parviz, Alexia Jolicoeur-Martineau

图 Figure · Page 5 of paper

🎯 向微递归模型注入高斯噪声，实现测试时随机探索
Injecting Gaussian noise into tiny recursive models unlocks test-time stochastic exploration at 7M parameters.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 微递归模型（TRM）通过反复优化隐状态来解决复杂推理任务，参数量极少，但其确定性递归过程容易陷入次优解且无法逃脱。现有补救方法依赖针对特定任务设计的输入扰动加投票聚合，缺乏通用性，需要针对每个任务单独设计。 Tiny Recursive Models (TRM) are compact, efficient models that solve hard reasoning puzzles by iteratively refining a latent state — but their deterministic recursion can trap them in suboptimal solutions with no way to escape. The existing workaround uses task-specific input perturbations and majority voting, which is not generalizable across tasks and requires manual design per benchmark.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 任务无关的随机探索框架：在每个深度递归步骤注入高斯噪声，使并行轨迹能够探索多样化的解空间，无需重新训练。 • 复用Q头进行智能选优：不依赖多数投票，而是直接复用模型原有的Q头（原本用于早停）作为质量评分器，从并行轨迹中挑选最优解。 • 极致效率：仅用700万参数，以不到前沿大模型0.0001倍的成本，在多项任务上超越GPT-4级别模型。 • Novel task-agnostic stochastic framework: Gaussian noise is injected at every deep recursion step, enabling parallel solution trajectories to explore diverse "solution basins" without any retraining. • Smart selection via existing Q head: Rather than using naive majority voting, PTRM reuses the model's built-in Q head (originally for early stopping) as a quality scorer to pick the best trajectory among parallel runs. • Extreme efficiency: Achieves near-frontier-LLM-beating accuracy using only 7M parameters at less than 0.0001× the cost of large models like GPT-4-class systems.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在两个主要基准上进行：Sudoku-Extreme（极高难度数独变体）和Pencil Puzzle Bench（多样化逻辑谜题集合）。基线模型包括原始确定性TRM、任务特定扰动+投票版TRM，以及GPT-4级别的前沿大语言模型。评估指标为谜题求解准确率（%）。所用模型仅有700万参数，PTRM纯粹在测试阶段应用，无需重新训练。 Experiments were conducted on two main benchmarks: Sudoku-Extreme (a highly challenging variant of Sudoku) and Pencil Puzzle Bench (a diverse collection of logic puzzles). Baselines include the original deterministic TRM, the task-specific perturbation+voting variant of TRM, and frontier LLMs (GPT-4-class). Evaluation metrics are puzzle-solving accuracy (%). The model used has only 7M parameters, and PTRM is applied purely at test time without any retraining.
📊 实验
结果		 在Sudoku-Extreme上，PTRM将准确率从87.4%（基线TRM）提升至98.75%，提升11.35个百分点。在Pencil Puzzle Bench上，准确率从62.6%跃升至91.2%，提升28.6个百分点。PTRM在Pencil Puzzle Bench上的准确率几乎是前沿大语言模型的两倍（91.2% vs. 55.1%），同时仅使用700万参数，计算成本不足大模型的0.0001倍。 On Sudoku-Extreme, PTRM improves accuracy from 87.4% (baseline TRM) to 98.75%, a gain of +11.35 percentage points. On Pencil Puzzle Bench, accuracy jumps from 62.6% to 91.2%, a gain of +28.6 percentage points. PTRM also nearly doubles the accuracy of frontier LLMs on Pencil Puzzle Bench (91.2% vs. 55.1%), while using only 7M parameters at less than 0.0001× the computational cost of those large models.
🏁 结论 本文证明了向微递归模型的递归过程中注入高斯噪声，即可在不重新训练的情况下，通过测试时随机探索大幅提升复杂推理任务的求解准确率。PTRM以极低的参数量和计算成本，在多项逻辑谜题基准上超越了参数量大数千倍的前沿大语言模型，为小模型的测试时计算扩展提供了一种通用且高效的新范式。 This paper demonstrates that injecting Gaussian noise into the recursive latent states of Tiny Recursive Models enables powerful, task-agnostic test-time stochastic exploration without retraining, dramatically improving puzzle-solving accuracy. PTRM establishes a new efficiency frontier, outperforming frontier LLMs on logic benchmarks at a tiny fraction of their parameter count and cost.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

PTRM在TRM推理阶段的每个递归步骤中注入小幅高斯噪声，将原本确定性的迭代过程转变为随机过程。多条并行轨迹同时运行，由于噪声的差异，每条轨迹探索不同的解空间区域。模型原有的Q头（价值估计器，最初用于判断何时提前停止递归）被复用为轨迹质量评分器，从中选出最优答案。整个过程无需修改训练流程，无需任务特定增强，可直接跨不同谜题类型开箱即用。 PTRM extends TRM by injecting small Gaussian noise vectors into the latent state at each recursion step during inference, turning the deterministic iteration into a stochastic process. Multiple parallel trajectories are run simultaneously, each exploring a different region of the solution space due to the noise. The model's existing Q head — a value estimator originally designed for deciding when to stop recursing early — is repurposed to score each trajectory and select the most promising final answer. This requires no modification to training, no task-specific augmentation, and works out of the box across different puzzle types.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\hat{z}_{t+1} = f_\theta(z_t + \epsilon_t, x), \quad \epsilon_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I)\]
💡 PTRM的核心操作：在每个递归步骤中，向隐状态 z_t 注入从均值为0、标准差为σ的高斯分布采样的噪声 ε_t，再输入递归函数 f_θ 得到下一步隐状态，实现随机探索。 The core PTRM operation: at each recursion step, Gaussian noise ε_t (sampled from N(0, σ²I)) is added to the current latent state z_t before passing it through the recursive function f_θ, enabling stochastic trajectory exploration.
\[\hat{k} = \arg\max_k Q_\phi(z_k^{(i)})\]
💡 轨迹选优公式：对于每条并行轨迹 i，使用Q头 Q_φ 对各递归步骤的隐状态评分，选取得分最高的步骤 k 对应的输出作为最终答案。 Trajectory selection: the Q head Q_φ scores each latent state across recursion steps and parallel trajectories; the step k with the highest Q value is selected as the final answer output.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着AI领域越来越关注通过测试时计算扩展来增强推理能力，PTRM有力证明了小型高效模型配合智能随机推理即可媲美甚至超越大型语言模型。这挑战了"规模决定推理能力"的假设，为构建极低成本却高性能的推理系统开辟了实际可行的道路。 As the AI field increasingly focuses on test-time compute scaling as a path to stronger reasoning, PTRM offers a compelling proof-of-concept that small, efficient models with smart stochastic inference can rival or beat massive LLMs on structured reasoning tasks. This challenges the assumption that scale is necessary for hard reasoning and opens a practical path toward extremely cheap yet capable reasoning systems.

CODA: Rewriting Transformer Blocks as GEMM-Epilogue Programs CODA：将Transformer模块重写为GEMM-Epilogue程序

🧑‍🔬 Han Guo, Jack Zhang, Arjun Menon, Driss Guessous et al.

图 Figure · Page 7 of paper

🎯 将Transformer中内存瓶颈算子融合进GEMM尾部，大幅减少数据搬运开销。
CODA fuses memory-bound Transformer operators into GEMM epilogues, slashing data movement overhead.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练大型Transformer模型时，除了大量矩阵乘法（GEMM）之外，还有许多辅助算子——如归一化、激活函数、残差相加和规约操作。这些算子频繁地将大型中间张量读写到GPU全局显存，计算量却很小，造成严重的"数据搬运瓶颈"。随着GEMM速度不断提升，这些内存受限算子在总训练时间中的占比越来越高，成为亟需解决的痛点。 Training large Transformer models involves not just heavy matrix multiplications (GEMMs) but also many smaller surrounding operations — normalization, activation functions, residual additions, and reductions. These "surrounding" operators repeatedly read and write large tensors to GPU global memory while doing very little arithmetic, creating a data-movement bottleneck that slows down otherwise well-optimized training pipelines. As GEMMs get faster with new hardware, these memory-bound operators become a proportionally larger fraction of total training time.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 核心洞察：许多非注意力Transformer算子可以被代数重参数化，使其在GEMM输出tile仍在片上SRAM时就地执行，避免反复读写全局显存。 • CODA提供一组小而可组合的Epilogue原语（缩放、规约、逐元素变换、累加），几乎可覆盖标准Transformer块前向和反向传播中除注意力之外的全部计算。 • 该抽象层的表达能力足以让人类工程师和LLM均能编写高性能CODA内核，真正兼顾框架级开发效率与硬件级执行效率。 • Key insight: Many non-attention Transformer operators can be algebraically reparameterized to execute *while* the GEMM output tile is still in on-chip SRAM, before it is flushed to global memory — eliminating redundant memory round-trips. • CODA provides a small, composable set of epilogue primitives (scaling, reductions, pairwise transforms, accumulation) that can be combined to cover nearly all forward and backward pass operations in a standard Transformer block, excluding attention. • The abstraction is expressive enough that both human engineers and LLMs can author high-performance CODA kernels, bridging framework-level productivity with hardware-level efficiency.
🧪 实验
内容		 作者在代表性Transformer训练任务上评估CODA，覆盖标准Transformer块的前向和反向传播。基线对比包括PyTorch eager模式、torch.compile以及手工优化的专家库（如FlashAttention风格的融合内核）。实验在现代NVIDIA GPU上，针对多种序列长度和批量大小，测量端到端内核执行时间和内存吞吐。此外还评估了人工编写与LLM自动生成的CODA内核程序，以验证该抽象的易用性与可达性。 The authors evaluate CODA on representative Transformer training workloads, covering both forward and backward passes of standard Transformer blocks. They benchmark fused CODA kernels against state-of-the-art baselines including PyTorch eager mode, torch.compile, and expert-written libraries such as FlashAttention-style fused kernels and other hand-optimized implementations. Experiments measure end-to-end kernel execution time and memory throughput across a range of sequence lengths and batch sizes on modern NVIDIA GPUs. Both human-authored and LLM-authored CODA kernel programs are evaluated to assess the abstraction's accessibility.
📊 实验
结果		 CODA内核在Transformer训练任务上相比PyTorch eager模式和torch.compile基线取得显著加速。融合的CODA Epilogue大幅降低了内存受限算子的开销，在归一化+激活+残差等融合场景中，相比未融合基线加速比达1.5–2×甚至更高。端到端Transformer块吞吐也有明显提升，接近手工优化专家内核的性能水平。LLM自动生成的CODA程序性能与人工编写版本相当或接近，验证了该抽象的表达能力和自动化潜力。在所有测试的序列长度和模型规模上，CODA均稳定优于torch.compile的融合效果。 CODA kernels achieve significant speedups over PyTorch eager mode and torch.compile baselines across Transformer training workloads. Fused CODA epilogues reduce memory-bound operator overhead substantially, with reported speedups in the range of 1.5–2× or more on individual operator groups (e.g., normalization + activation + residual fusions) compared to unfused baselines. End-to-end Transformer block throughput improvements are also demonstrated, approaching the performance of hand-optimized expert kernels. LLM-authored CODA programs match or closely approach human-written kernel performance, validating the abstraction's expressiveness and automation potential. Specific numbers vary by configuration, but CODA consistently outperforms torch.compile fusion across tested sequence lengths and model sizes.
🏁 结论 CODA证明，将Transformer非注意力计算表达为GEMM-Epilogue程序既有理论依据，又在实践中行之有效，可达到接近专家手工内核的性能，同时足够易用以支持LLM辅助编写。这确立了GEMM+Epilogue编程范式作为连接高层框架生产力与底层GPU效率的可行路径。 CODA demonstrates that expressing Transformer non-attention computations as GEMM-epilogue programs is both theoretically principled and practically effective, achieving near-expert kernel performance while remaining accessible enough for LLM-assisted authoring. This establishes GEMM-plus-epilogue programming as a viable paradigm for bridging high-level framework productivity and low-level GPU efficiency in Transformer training.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

CODA提出一种GPU内核抽象：固定GEMM主循环，并在每个输出tile生成后、写回全局显存之前，立即在片上共享内存或寄存器上执行一个可组合的Epilogue阶段。Epilogue阶段由少量原语构成：缩放、行/列规约、逐元素变换和累加——这些操作直接作用于片上的数据，无需额外的内存传输。通过将Transformer子计算（如LayerNorm、RMSNorm、SwiGLU、Dropout、残差相加及梯度计算）代数重写为GEMM-Epilogue形式，CODA消除了独立内存受限内核的多次启动开销。该框架同时支持人工编写和LLM自动生成的内核描述，兼顾灵活性与效率。 CODA introduces a GPU kernel abstraction that fixes the GEMM computation (the "mainloop") and attaches a composable epilogue stage that executes immediately after each output tile is produced, while that tile is still resident in on-chip shared memory or registers. The epilogue stage is built from a small vocabulary of primitives: scaling operations, row/column reductions, pairwise (element-wise) transformations, and accumulation — all of which map to the data already in fast on-chip memory. By algebraically rewriting Transformer sub-computations (e.g., LayerNorm, RMSNorm, SwiGLU, dropout, residual additions, gradient computations) into this GEMM-epilogue form, CODA eliminates the need for separate memory-bound kernel launches. The constrained interface intentionally limits expressivity to preserve the performance structure of hand-tuned GEMMs, while still being rich enough to cover nearly the full Transformer block. Both human-written and LLM-generated kernel descriptions are evaluated within the framework.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[Y = \text{Epilogue}(\text{GEMM}(A, B))\]
💡 CODA的核心范式：将矩阵乘法（GEMM）的输出tile在片上直接送入Epilogue阶段处理，避免写回再读取全局显存。 CODA's core paradigm: the output of a GEMM tile is passed directly into an Epilogue stage while still on-chip, avoiding a round-trip through global memory before applying normalization, activation, or other operators.
\[\hat{x}_i = \frac{x_i}{\sqrt{\frac{1}{d}\sum_{j=1}^{d} x_j^2 + \epsilon}}\]
💡 RMSNorm归一化公式——CODA将此类规约+缩放操作融合进GEMM Epilogue，使其在tile级别完成，无需额外内存内核。 RMSNorm normalization formula — CODA fuses this reduction-plus-scaling into the GEMM epilogue at the tile level, eliminating a separate memory-bound kernel launch.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着LLM训练规模不断扩大，GPU利用率的每一个百分点都至关重要——CODA直接解决了非GEMM算子的内存带宽瓶颈，而这一问题随着矩阵计算单元越来越快会愈发凸显。通过让高性能内核融合对人类开发者和LLM都变得可及，CODA有望大幅降低构建高效定制训练基础设施的门槛。 As LLM training scales up, every percentage point of GPU utilization matters — CODA directly attacks the overlooked memory-bandwidth bottleneck from non-GEMM operators that grows more painful as matrix units get faster. By making high-performance kernel fusion accessible to both human developers and LLMs, CODA could significantly lower the barrier to building efficient custom training infrastructure.

HRM-Text: Efficient Pretraining Beyond Scaling HRM-Text：超越规模扩展的高效预训练

🧑‍🔬 Guan Wang, Changling Liu, Chenyu Wang, Cai Zhou et al.

图 Figure · Page 3 of paper

🎯 用百倍少的数据训出媲美主流大模型的高效语言模型
A 1B hierarchical recurrent LLM matches 2-7B models using 100-900x fewer training tokens.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 训练大型语言模型需要海量算力和数千亿互联网文本，门槛极高，大多数研究者根本无法从零开始做预训练研究。目前基于Transformer的预训练范式几乎没有留下可以用更少资源实现高性能的架构创新空间。我们需要一种全新方法，能用远少于现有方案的数据和算力达到有竞争力的性能。 Training large language models from scratch requires enormous compute and hundreds of billions of internet-scale tokens, making foundational LLM research inaccessible to most labs and researchers. Current Transformer-based pretraining paradigms offer little room for architectural or objective innovation without matching the huge resource budgets of frontier labs. A new approach is needed that can achieve competitive performance with drastically fewer tokens and compute.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • **分层循环模型（HRM）：** 用双层循环架构取代Transformer，将"慢速战略层"和"快速执行层"解耦，灵感来自大脑额顶叶回路的多时间尺度处理机制。 • **MagicNorm + 深度信用分配预热：** 两种全新稳定训练技术，专门解决深度循环网络在语言建模中的梯度传播和训练不稳定问题。 • **纯指令对预训练 + PrefixLM掩码：** 完全不使用原始网络文本，只在指令-回复对上用任务完成目标训练，大幅提升样本效率。 • **Hierarchical Recurrent Model (HRM):** Replaces Transformers with a two-level recurrent architecture that separates slow "strategic" computation from fast "execution" computation, inspired by the brain's frontoparietal loop. • **MagicNorm + Warmup Deep Credit Assignment:** Two novel stabilization techniques that make training deep recurrent networks for language modeling reliable and convergent. • **Instruction-only pretraining with PrefixLM:** Trains exclusively on instruction-response pairs (not raw web text) with a task-completion objective, yielding far better sample efficiency than standard next-token prediction on raw corpora.
🧪 实验
内容		 在五个标准推理与知识基准上评估：MMLU（综合知识）、ARC-Challenge（科学推理）、DROP（阅读理解与算术）、GSM8K（小学数学）、MATH（竞赛数学）。基线模型涵盖LLaMA、Mistral、Qwen、Gemma等参数量2B–7B的主流开源模型，训练数据规模从1T到36T token不等。HRM-Text 1B模型仅用400亿unique token和约1500美元预算从零训练，对比时明确说明了训练token数和估算算力/成本。 The model is evaluated on five standard reasoning and knowledge benchmarks: MMLU (general knowledge), ARC-Challenge (science reasoning), DROP (reading comprehension and arithmetic), GSM8K (grade-school math), and MATH (competition math). Baselines include a wide range of open-source models with 2B–7B parameters such as LLaMA, Mistral, Qwen, and Gemma, which were trained on 1T–36T tokens. The HRM-Text 1B model is trained from scratch on only 40 billion unique tokens with an estimated compute budget of $1,500, and comparisons explicitly account for training token counts and estimated FLOPs/dollar costs.
📊 实验
结果		 HRM-Text 1B在各基准上的成绩：**MMLU 60.7%、ARC-C 81.9%、DROP 82.2%、GSM8K 84.5%、MATH 56.2%**，仅用400亿unique token和约1500美元算力预算从零训练。与主流2B–7B基线相比（如在15T token上训练的LLaMA-3.1-8B），HRM-Text使用的训练token少**100–900倍**，估算算力少**96–432倍**，但在多数基准上表现相当甚至更优。在数学类任务（GSM8K 84.5%、MATH 56.2%）上接近或超越了多个用数量级更多数据训练的7B模型。 HRM-Text 1B achieves: **60.7% on MMLU**, **81.9% on ARC-C**, **82.2% on DROP**, **84.5% on GSM8K**, and **56.2% on MATH** — trained on only 40B unique tokens with ~$1,500 compute budget. Compared to standard 2-7B baselines (e.g., LLaMA-3.1-8B trained on 15T tokens), HRM-Text uses **100–900× fewer training tokens** and **96–432× less estimated compute**, while performing competitively or better on most benchmarks. On math-heavy tasks like GSM8K (84.5%) and MATH (56.2%), it approaches or exceeds several 7B models trained on orders of magnitude more data.
🏁 结论 HRM-Text证明了联合设计模型架构（分层循环）与训练目标（纯指令+PrefixLM）可以将算力-性能比降低2–3个数量级。这为"学术实验室规模也能从零做高质量预训练"提供了实证，从根本上降低了大语言模型基础研究的门槛。 HRM-Text demonstrates that co-designing the model architecture (hierarchical recurrence) and training objective (instruction-only + PrefixLM) can reduce the compute-to-performance ratio by 2–3 orders of magnitude compared to standard LLM pretraining. This serves as an empirical proof-of-concept that high-quality pretraining from scratch is achievable at academic-lab scale, fundamentally democratizing foundational LLM research.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解
