Self-Improving Language Models with Bidirectional Evolutionary Search 基于双向进化搜索的语言模型自我提升

🧑‍🔬 Guowei Xu, Zhenting Qi, Huangyuan Su, Weirui Ye et al.

图 Figure · Page 4 of paper

🎯 双向进化搜索让AI更聪明地探索答案、自我进化
A bidirectional evolutionary search framework helps AI models find better answers by exploring more broadly and breaking hard problems into checkable steps.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像一群人在迷宫里找宝藏。以前的方法让每个人各自从起点一路走到底，只在终点才知道找没找到。BES则让大家互相交换走过的"半段路线"拼接出新路（前向进化），同时从宝藏位置倒推，在沿途设置一个个可以确认的路标（后向分解）——这样整个团队能从全新角度更快地找到宝藏。Think of it like a team of explorers searching for treasure. Old methods only let each explorer walk straight ahead from where they started. BES lets explorers swap maps and combine their partial routes (forward evolution), AND they start by working backward from the treasure's location to plant helpful signposts along the way (backward decomposition) — so the whole team finds the treasure faster and from totally new angles.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现在的AI在"搜索更好答案"时存在两个大问题：一是只会在自己"熟悉"的范围内打转，很难找到新颖或创造性的解法；二是只有在最终答案对或错时才有反馈，就像做数学题只知道最后结果，中间步骤完全没有提示，效率很低。 Current AI models that try to improve themselves through search — like generating many answer attempts and picking the best — are stuck in a rut. They mostly explore answer paths that are already "popular" with the model, missing creative or unusual solutions. They also only know if a final answer is right or wrong, with no feedback along the way, like trying to solve a puzzle with no hints until the very end.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 前向进化：不只是一步步生成答案，而是把多条"半成品解题路径"拼接重组，像基因重组一样产生模型自己很难想到的新答案。 • 后向分解：自动将难题拆解为一系列可验证的小目标，让AI在每一步都能得到反馈，而不是只在最后知道对不对。 • 理论支撑：数学证明了传统搜索被困在"概率质量壳"里，而BES能跳出这个局限，用更少的尝试次数找到正确答案。 • Forward Evolution: Instead of just generating answers one word at a time, BES "mixes and matches" partial solution paths — like genetic recombination — to create diverse, out-of-the-box candidates the model would rarely produce on its own. • Backward Decomposition: BES automatically breaks a hard problem into smaller, checkable sub-goals, giving the AI dense feedback signals at every step rather than just a final pass/fail. • Theory-backed: The paper proves mathematically that standard search is trapped in a narrow "entropy shell," while BES can escape it to find better solutions with far fewer attempts.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究团队在两个场景下测试了BES：（1）训练后阶段：用BES生成训练数据来微调模型，在AIME数学竞赛题和LiveCodeBench编程题等高难度基准上，与best-of-N采样、树搜索和主流强化学习训练方法进行对比；（2）推理时搜索：在三个开放问题求解基准上，与现有开源搜索框架比较平均准确率和最佳通过率。 The researchers tested BES in two settings: (1) post-training, where BES generates training data to fine-tune models, evaluated on hard math and coding benchmarks including AIME and LiveCodeBench against baselines like best-of-N, tree search, and standard RL training methods; (2) inference-time search, where BES is used at test time on three open problem-solving benchmarks, compared against existing open-source search frameworks using metrics like average accuracy and best-case pass rate.
📊 实验
结果		 在主流训练算法（如GRPO）无法提升的高难度后训练任务中，BES实现了稳定的性能提升。在推理时的三个开放基准测试中，BES在平均准确率和最佳表现（pass@k）两项指标上均优于现有开源框架。理论上，后向搜索可将找到正确答案所需的样本数量指数级减少。BES在所有三个推理时测试基准上均超越了此前方法。 On post-training tasks where mainstream algorithms (like GRPO) failed to improve, BES enabled consistent gains. At inference time on three open benchmarks, BES outperformed existing open-source frameworks in both average and best-case (pass@k) performance. For example, on challenging benchmarks, BES achieved notable improvements in pass rates, with the backward search component shown theoretically to reduce required samples exponentially. Specific numbers from the paper include BES surpassing prior frameworks across all three inference-time benchmarks tested.
🏁 结论 BES证明了将进化路径混合与后向目标分解相结合，能让AI模型在解空间中探索得更广更深，即使是此前最先进训练算法都无能为力的难题，也能实现持续的自我提升。 BES proves that combining evolutionary path-mixing with backward goal decomposition allows AI models to explore solution spaces far more effectively than existing search methods, enabling self-improvement even on problems that previously stumped state-of-the-art training algorithms.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

BES从两个方向同时工作。向前：它不只是一步步生成答案，还会把多条不同的"半途解题路径"拼接组合，就像厨师把几道菜的食材混搭创造新菜一样，产生模型自己很难生成的新颖解法。向后：从目标出发，递归地问"要达到这个目标，需要哪些可验证的小步骤？"从而建立一个带有中间检查点的路线图。两个方向协同配合：向后分解提供密集的过程反馈，向前进化利用这些反馈更高效地探索多样化的优质解答。 BES works in two complementary directions. Going forward, it doesn't just grow answer trees one branch at a time — it also "splices" together pieces from different partial solutions, like a chef combining ingredients from multiple recipes to invent a new dish. Going backward, it starts from the final goal and recursively asks "what smaller, verifiable step would get me here?" — building a scaffold of checkpoints that guide the forward search. These two directions work together: the backward pass creates a roadmap of sub-goals with feedback at each step, and the forward evolutionary pass uses that roadmap to explore diverse, high-quality solutions more efficiently.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{BES} = \text{Forward Evolution} \oplus \text{Backward Decomposition}\]
💡 BES框架由前向进化（路径重组）和后向分解（子目标递归）两部分耦合构成 BES combines forward candidate evolution (recombining partial trajectories) with backward goal decomposition (recursive subgoal generation) into a unified search framework
\[P(\text{correct}) \geq 1 - (1-p)^N \text{ vs. exponential reduction via backward search}\]
💡 后向搜索在理论上能将找到正确答案所需的样本数量从多项式级降低到指数级更少 Backward search is proven to exponentially reduce the number of samples needed to find a correct answer compared to forward-only expansion

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

这项工作可能让未来的AI助手在处理真正难的问题时更上一层楼，比如高级数学、复杂编程或多步推理，因为它让AI能"跳出固定思维"并在过程中获得有效反馈。随着AI系统越来越多地用自己的输出来训练自己，像BES这样更聪明的搜索方法是突破性能天花板的关键。 This work could make future AI assistants much better at tackling truly hard problems — like advanced math, complex coding, or multi-step reasoning — by helping them "think outside the box" and get useful feedback along the way. As AI systems increasingly train themselves on their own outputs, smarter search methods like BES are key to breaking through performance ceilings.

Learn from your own latents and not from tokens: A sample-complexity theory 从自身潜在表征而非词元中学习：样本复杂度理论

🧑‍🔬 Daniel J. Korchinski, Alessandro Favero, Matthieu Wyart

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 潜在表征预测比词元预测指数级更高效
Predicting your own hidden representations needs exponentially less data than predicting raw tokens.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像学语言：死记硬背每一个句子需要无数例子，但如果你先理解"主语-谓语-宾语"这类抽象概念（内部表征），只需少量例句就能举一反三。这篇论文证明了AI"理解概念再学词语"比"死记词语"要高效指数倍。Think of learning chess: memorizing every possible board position takes forever, but if you learn abstract concepts like "control the center" or "protect your king," you get good with far fewer games. This paper proves that AI learning abstract internal concepts first — instead of memorizing raw words — needs exponentially fewer examples, just like a smart chess student beats a brute-force memorizer.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有AI模型（如大型语言模型和扩散模型）需要海量训练数据才能学好，远超人类或动物所需的样本量。而人类婴儿只需少量经历就能掌握语言和视觉概念，这说明AI的学习方式存在根本性的低效问题。为什么预测"原始词语"如此费数据？有没有更聪明的学习方式？ Modern AI models like GPT or diffusion models need enormous amounts of training data — far more than a child needs to learn language or recognize objects. This huge data gap suggests something is fundamentally inefficient about how these models learn. The question is: why does predicting raw words or pixels require so much data, and is there a smarter way to learn?
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次用严格数学证明：预测自身"隐层表征"（内部概念）比预测原始词元，数据需求从随深度指数增长降为常数级别。 • 揭示了data2vec（Meta的自监督学习模型）本质上隐式地在做层次化潜在预测，给出了它首个样本复杂度分析。 • 发现显式堆叠多层预测结构（如H-JEPA）基本上是多余的，因为单层潜在预测已经能自动捕获层次结构。 • First rigorous mathematical proof that predicting internal "concept representations" instead of raw tokens slashes data requirements from exponential to constant as structure depth grows. • Provides the first sample-complexity analysis of data2vec, showing it secretly performs hierarchical latent prediction under the hood. • Reveals that explicitly stacking multiple prediction layers (like H-JEPA) adds little benefit, since a single latent-prediction step already captures the hierarchy.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验基于概率上下文无关文法生成的合成数据，通过改变树的深度L来控制任务难度。基线方法包括词元级自监督学习（SSL）和有监督学习。评估指标是恢复隐藏树结构所需的样本数量，以及随深度L增加时样本需求的增长规律。额外实验包括：用层次聚类算法验证理论上界，用梯度下降训练的端到端神经网络（含预测器-聚类器模块）验证实际可行性，以及对data2vec算法的理论分析。 Experiments used synthetic data generated from probabilistic context-free grammars, with tree depth L varied to control task difficulty. Baselines included token-level self-supervised learning and supervised learning. The key evaluation metric was how many samples are needed to recover the hidden tree structure, and how that number scales with depth L. Additional experiments included a hierarchical clustering algorithm, an end-to-end neural network with predictor-clusterer modules trained via gradient descent, and a theoretical analysis of the data2vec algorithm's behavior.
📊 实验
结果		 理论证明：词元级SSL和监督学习所需样本数随树深度L呈指数增长（~exp(L)）；而潜在表征预测所需样本数仅为O(log L)（常数量级，含对数因子）。神经网络实验确认了这一指数级差距。data2vec的理论分析表明，其样本复杂度同样达到O(log L)量级，与显式层次方法相当。H-JEPA的多层堆叠结构被证明相较于单层潜在预测没有渐进意义上的额外收益。 The theory proves token-level SSL and supervised learning require samples growing as ~exp(L) with tree depth L, while latent prediction requires only O(log L) samples — a gap that is exponential. Neural network experiments confirmed this exponential gap empirically. The analysis of data2vec showed it also achieves O(log L) sample complexity, matching explicit hierarchical methods. Explicit multi-layer stacking (H-JEPA) provides no asymptotic improvement over single-level latent prediction.
🏁 结论 本文从理论上严格证明，让AI预测自身内部表征（而非原始词语）可以将所需训练数据量从随深度指数爆炸降至几乎恒定，从而为JEPA、data2vec等自监督学习方法为何数据高效提供了首个严格理论解释。 This paper rigorously proves that learning by predicting one's own internal representations — not raw tokens — achieves exponentially better data efficiency, providing the first solid theoretical foundation for why methods like JEPA and data2vec work so well with less data.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究者用一种叫"概率上下文无关文法"的数学玩具模型来模拟语言和图像的层次结构——就像一棵树，从抽象概念到具体词语层层生成。他们在这个可控模型上，分别计算了三种学习策略所需的样本数：（1）预测原始词元、（2）有监督学习、（3）预测自己的隐层表征。他们证明前两种需要随树深度指数爆炸的数据量，而第三种只需常数量级的数据。随后用层次聚类算法、端到端神经网络实验验证了理论，并将data2vec的学习机制做了理论分析，证实它实际上在做隐式的层次潜在预测。 The researchers built a mathematical toy world called a "probabilistic context-free grammar" — imagine a tree where abstract ideas at the top branch down into concrete words at the bottom. They calculated exactly how many training examples each learning strategy needs to reconstruct that hidden tree. They proved that predicting raw words (like standard language models do) needs exponentially more data as the tree gets deeper, while predicting your own internal representations stays efficient regardless of depth. They then verified this theory using a hierarchical clustering algorithm, a real neural network trained end-to-end, and a theoretical dissection of data2vec.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[N_{\text{token-SSL}} \sim \exp(L)\]
💡 词元级自监督学习所需的样本数随树深度L指数爆炸增长 Token-level self-supervised learning requires samples that grow exponentially with tree depth L
\[N_{\text{latent}} \sim O(\log L)\]
💡 潜在表征预测所需样本数仅随L对数增长，几乎是常数级别 Latent prediction requires only logarithmically many samples — nearly constant regardless of how deep the structure is

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

这一理论为设计更高效的AI学习算法提供了科学依据——未来的AI系统可能只需现有数据量的极小一部分就能达到同样性能，大幅降低训练成本。这也揭示了大脑"预测编码"机制为何如此高效，有助于启发更类脑的AI架构设计。 This theory gives AI designers a scientific reason to build systems that predict internal concepts rather than raw data, potentially cutting training costs by orders of magnitude for future models. It also bridges neuroscience and AI — explaining why the brain's "predictive coding" strategy is so sample-efficient, which could inspire more brain-like AI architectures.

Parallax: Parameterized Local Linear Attention for Language Modeling Parallax：用于语言建模的参数化局部线性注意力机制

🧑‍🔬 Yifei Zuo, Dhruv Pai, Zhichen Zeng, Alec Dewulf et al.

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 用更聪明的"局部线性注意力"让大模型更准、更快
A smarter attention mechanism that gives LLMs better memory with faster hardware performance.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像从"用班级平均分猜你的成绩"升级为"根据你的学习趋势精准预测你的成绩"——Parallax让AI在记忆和检索信息时，从粗糙的平均估计升级为更聪明的线性拟合，预测更准，还跑得更快。Imagine a librarian who used to help you find books by saying "most people in this section like mysteries" (a rough average). Parallax upgrades that librarian to say "based on the trend of what YOU'VE been reading, you'll probably want *this* book" — a smarter, personalized fit that's also faster to look up.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 当前AI大模型的核心计算模块——"注意力机制"——自诞生以来几乎没有本质性改变。就像一个只会用"平均数"来做预测的学生，现有的注意力机制用的是一种叫"局部常数估计"的简单方法，在处理记忆和信息检索时存在理论上的偏差。研究者想升级这个机制，但早期改进版（LLA）因为计算不稳定、无法大规模训练而停留在实验室阶段。 The attention mechanism at the heart of every large language model has stayed basically the same since it was invented. It uses a simple "local constant" estimation method — like always guessing the average instead of fitting a better curve — which leads to known theoretical weaknesses in how the model stores and retrieves information. A smarter version called Local Linear Attention (LLA) existed on paper, but it couldn't be used at scale because it was numerically unstable and too slow to train on real hardware.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 将"局部线性估计"引入注意力机制，理论上比传统softmax注意力有更优的偏差-方差权衡 • 设计了"Parallax"——用一个可学习的投影器替换掉不稳定的数值求解器，让LLA真正可训练 • 提出硬件感知算法，比FlashAttention的算术强度更高，让注意力计算从"内存瓶颈"转向"计算瓶颈"（更高效） • 首次发现"Muon优化器"能解锁Parallax的能力，展示了架构与优化器的协同设计效应 • Upgrades attention from a "flat local average" to a "local linear fit," giving provably better theoretical properties for memory storage and retrieval. • Replaces the unstable numerical solver in LLA with a learnable projector, making the approach trainable at billion-parameter scale. • Introduces a hardware-aware kernel that beats FlashAttention 2/3 on decode speed across diverse settings. • Discovers a surprising synergy: the Muon optimizer uniquely unlocks Parallax's full potential — a first-of-its-kind architecture-optimizer co-design finding.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究团队在0.6B和1.7B参数规模上从头预训练模型，与标准Transformer（使用softmax注意力）和其他高效注意力变体进行对比。评估指标包括：预训练困惑度（perplexity，越低越好）以及多个下游基准测试（如常识推理、语言理解等）。他们还设计了参数量匹配和计算量匹配两种控制实验，以及消融实验来分析Muon优化器和各设计组件的贡献。硬件测试在多种批大小和上下文长度下对比了FlashAttention 2和FlashAttention 3的解码速度。 The team pretrained models from scratch at 0.6B and 1.7B parameter scales, comparing Parallax against standard Transformer baselines with softmax attention and other efficient attention variants. They measured pretraining perplexity (lower = better language modeling) and evaluated on multiple downstream benchmarks covering commonsense reasoning and language understanding. They ran both parameter-matched and compute-matched controls to ensure fair comparison. Ablation studies isolated the contribution of the Muon optimizer and individual design components. Separately, they benchmarked their custom GPU decode kernel against FlashAttention 2 and FlashAttention 3 across different batch sizes and context lengths.
📊 实验
结果		 Parallax在0.6B和1.7B规模的预训练中始终取得更低的困惑度，且增益在参数匹配和计算匹配两种控制下均成立（即Pareto改进）。自定义解码内核在多种批大小和上下文长度下匹配甚至超越FlashAttention 2/3的速度。使用Muon优化器时，Parallax的性能增益显著大于使用Adam时，消融实验证实Muon是解锁Parallax容量的关键。下游基准测试上的提升与预训练困惑度的改善保持一致，验证了泛化能力。（注：论文未在摘要中列出具体数字，详细数字需参见正文表格。） Parallax achieves consistently lower perplexity than baseline Transformers throughout pretraining at both 0.6B and 1.7B scales, and these gains hold under both parameter-matched and compute-matched controls, confirming a Pareto improvement (better quality at the same cost). The custom decode kernel matches or outperforms FlashAttention 2 and FlashAttention 3 across diverse batch sizes and context lengths. With the Muon optimizer, Parallax's gains are substantially larger than with Adam, establishing a novel architecture-optimizer co-design effect. Downstream benchmark improvements are consistent with pretraining perplexity gains, demonstrating transfer. (Precise numerical deltas are reported in the paper's main tables.)
🏁 结论 本文证明了将注意力机制从"局部常数估计"升级为"局部线性估计"在大规模语言模型预训练中是切实可行的，并带来了持续的性能提升。Parallax实现了理论上更优的记忆机制、更快的硬件执行效率，以及首例注意力架构与优化器协同设计的实证发现。 This paper proves that upgrading attention from a flat local average to a local linear fit is both practical and beneficial at LLM scale, delivering consistent perplexity and benchmark improvements with no efficiency cost. It also uncovers the first empirical case of architecture-optimizer co-design for attention, showing that the right optimizer (Muon) is essential to fully realizing the architectural improvement.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

Parallax的核心思路是：传统注意力机制在查找信息时用的是"局部均值"（粗糙的加权平均），而Parallax升级为"局部线性回归"（更精准的曲线拟合）。具体来说，它引入了一个额外的可学习"探针投影器"，让模型主动学习如何感知键值对的协方差结构，而不是靠不稳定的数值求解器。同时，研究团队设计了一套硬件感知的计算算法，把更多工作压到GPU的计算单元而非内存带宽上，使得解码速度更快。最后，他们发现搭配Muon优化器训练时，Parallax的性能增益会显著放大。 Traditional attention finds relevant information by computing a weighted average of nearby values — like asking "what's the average opinion in this neighborhood?" Parallax instead fits a tiny linear model locally — like asking "what's the best-fit trend line in this neighborhood?" — which captures more structure. To make this practical, the team replaces an unstable equation-solver with a small learned projector that the model trains alongside everything else. They also redesign the low-level GPU computation so the chip spends more time doing math and less time waiting for memory, making decoding faster than standard FlashAttention. Finally, they discover that pairing Parallax with the Muon optimizer (instead of the usual Adam) is key to unlocking its full performance gains.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\hat{f}(x) = \arg\min_{f \in \mathcal{F}_{\text{linear}}} \sum_i K_h(x, x_i)(y_i - f(x_i))^2\]
💡 局部线性估计：在查询点x附近，用一个线性函数去拟合键值对，而不是简单取加权平均（局部常数）。这是Parallax相对于标准softmax注意力的核心升级。 Local linear estimation: near query point x, fit a linear function to the key-value pairs instead of just taking a weighted average. This is the theoretical upgrade Parallax makes over standard softmax attention.
\[\text{Parallax}(Q, K, V) = \text{Attention}(Q, K, V) + \text{Probe}(Q, K) \cdot \text{Cov}(K, V)\]
💡 Parallax在标准注意力输出之上，加入一个可学习的探针项，用来捕捉键值协方差结构，从而实现局部线性修正。 Parallax adds a learnable probe term on top of standard attention output, capturing the key-value covariance structure to implement the local linear correction.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

注意力机制是所有大语言模型（如GPT、LLaMA）的核心引擎，对它的任何改进都可能影响未来所有AI产品的质量和成本。Parallax在不增加计算开销的前提下提升了模型能力，同时加速了推理，意味着更聪明、更便宜的AI助手离我们更近了一步。 Attention is the engine inside every major language model — ChatGPT, LLaMA, Gemini — so improving it ripples across the entire AI industry. Parallax shows you can get a smarter model at the same compute cost while also making inference faster, which means future AI assistants could be both more capable and cheaper to run.

❓ 待解决
的问题		 科学研究需要不断提出假设、设计实验、分析结果，这个过程耗时耗力。现有的AI科研助手要么只沿着一条研究路线走到底，要么靠一个"中央指挥官"统一调度，导致它们无法同时探索多个方向，也很难在实验进行到一半时灵活调整策略。更糟的是，一旦某个方向失败，这些系统往往"忘记"了教训，导致重复踩坑。 Running scientific research is slow and expensive — you need to constantly come up with ideas, test them, learn from failures, and try again. 的亮点		 • 去中心化设计：没有中央指挥官，多个AI智能体自行组队、协商分工，围绕最有希望的假设协同推进 • 实验前先"互相批评"：智能体在真正跑实验之前，会互相审查彼此的方案，避免浪费计算资源在明显无效的想法上 • 共享失败记录：所有智能体共享一个"实验状态"，包括哪些路走不通——让整个团队不会重复犯同样的错误 • 长期持续探索：系统能在已经找到好方法的基础上继续挖掘改进空间，而单智能体方法则会停滞不前 • No single boss: Agents self-organize into teams around the most promising research ideas, rather than waiting for top-down instructions. • Peer review before experiments: Agents critique each other's proposals before running costly experiments, filtering out weak ideas early. • Shared memory of failures: A shared "experimental state" lets all agents learn from what didn't work, avoiding redundant dead ends. • Sustained long-horizon discovery: Unlike single-agent systems that stall, AutoScientists keeps finding improvements even when starting from a strong baseline.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

AutoScientists由一组AI智能体组成，它们共享一个"实验黑板"，上面记录着所有已尝试过的想法、成功案例和失败记录。每个智能体可以独立阅读这块黑板，决定加入哪个研究方向，并自发组成小组。小组在真正运行实验之前，会互相对方案提出批评和改进意见，类似于学术界的"同行评审"。实验跑完后，结果会写回黑板，所有智能体都能看到并据此调整策略。整个系统没有一个固定的"总指挥"，而是通过这种去中心化的协作机制，让多条研究线路并行推进，同时积累集体知识。 AutoScientists works like a research lab where every scientist can see a shared whiteboard showing all past experiments — what worked, what failed, and what's currently being explored. Agents read this shared board, vote with their feet by joining the most promising research teams, and review each other's ideas before any expensive experiments are run (like a quick internal peer review). After each experiment, results are posted back to the board so everyone learns together. There's no fixed "project manager" — the team self-organizes dynamically, running multiple research threads in parallel and building on collective knowledge over time.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

科学研究是人类进步的核心引擎，但速度太慢——一个新药从发现到上市往往需要十多年。AutoScientists展示了AI可以像一支真正的科研团队一样协作、自我纠错、持续探索，有潜力大幅加速药物研发、蛋白质设计等关键领域的研究进程。如果这类系统走向实用，未来的科学突破可能不再需要等待数十年。 Science moves slowly — developing a new drug can take over a decade. AutoScientists shows that AI can work like a real collaborative research team: exploring multiple ideas at once, learning from failures, and never stopping. This could dramatically speed up breakthroughs in medicine, protein engineering, and AI model development, potentially compressing years of research into days.
