Wan-Streamer v0.1: End-to-end Real-time Interactive Foundation Models Wan-Streamer v0.1：端到端实时交互基础模型

🧑‍🔬 Lianghua Huang, Zhi-Fan Wu, Wei Wang, Yupeng Shi et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 单一模型实现超低延迟全双工实时音视频对话
One unified AI model handles real-time audio-visual conversation with under 550ms total latency.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像以前打国际长途需要经过好几个转接员一个个传话，又慢又容易出错；而Wan-Streamer就像开通了直拨专线，你说完话对方几乎瞬间就能听到并回应，整个对话流畅得像在同一个房间聊天。Think of old-school international phone calls that had to bounce through five different operators before reaching the other person — slow, garbled, and prone to miscommunication. Wan-Streamer is like switching to a direct fiber line: you speak, and the AI responds almost instantly, as if it's sitting right across from you.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 现有的AI对话系统（比如视频通话AI助手）通常是把好几个独立模块"拼凑"在一起：一个负责听你说话，一个负责理解语义，一个负责生成回复，一个负责合成语音，还有一个负责驱动虚拟形象动起来。这种"流水线"方式导致系统反应慢、延迟高，还容易出错——每一步的小错误都会叠加放大。就像打电话时信号一卡，你说完话要等好几秒对方才能听到，非常影响体验。 Existing AI conversation systems that handle video, audio, and language together are built like assembly lines — one module listens, another understands, another generates speech, another animates a face. This patchwork approach is slow: you say something and the AI takes seconds to respond, breaking the natural flow of conversation. Each handoff between modules also introduces new errors, making the whole system less reliable.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 全球首个将语言、音频、视频的感知与生成统一在单一Transformer模型中的原生流式交互系统，无需任何外部模块。 • 创新设计了"块因果注意力"机制，让模型可以边接收输入边实时输出，流式单元短至160毫秒，速度达25帧/秒。 • 模型侧响应延迟仅约200毫秒，加上网络传输后总交互延迟约550毫秒，实现真正的亚秒级全双工音视频对话。 • First native-streaming model to unify language, audio, and video perception AND generation inside a single Transformer — no external modules needed at all. • Novel "block-causal attention" lets the model start outputting responses while still receiving input, with streaming chunks as short as 160ms at 25fps. • Achieves ~200ms model-side latency and ~550ms total latency (including network), enabling truly natural, sub-second back-and-forth conversation.
🧪 实验
内容		 论文主要通过系统延迟测量和实时交互演示来验证模型性能，包括模型侧响应延迟、总交互延迟（含网络传输）、流式帧率等指标的测量。与传统级联式多模块系统（分别依赖VAD、ASR、TTS、动画生成等独立组件）进行对比，评估端到端方案在延迟和系统复杂度上的优势。 The paper measures system latency in a real-world streaming setup, reporting model-side response delay and total round-trip interaction latency (including 350ms simulated bidirectional network delay). It compares against traditional cascaded pipeline systems that chain together separate modules for speech detection, speech recognition, language modeling, text-to-speech, and avatar animation, evaluating how much faster and simpler the end-to-end approach is.
📊 实验
结果		 模型侧响应延迟约200毫秒；加入350毫秒双向网络延迟后，总交互延迟约550毫秒，达到亚秒级全双工交互；流式输出单元短至160毫秒，视频帧率25fps。相比传统级联系统，消除了多模块流水线的累积误差和额外延迟，实现了端到端统一处理。 ~200ms model-side response latency; ~550ms total interaction latency (including 350ms network delay), achieving sub-second duplex audio-visual communication; streaming units as short as 160ms at 25fps video output. Compared to cascaded pipeline systems, the end-to-end approach eliminates accumulated errors and extra latency from chaining multiple independent modules.
🏁 结论 Wan-Streamer证明了可以用一个统一的端到端模型，同时完成实时音视频对话的感知、理解、生成和时序协调，而无需依赖任何外部模块。这为构建真正自然、低延迟的多模态AI交互系统提供了全新的技术路线，是迈向"像真人一样实时对话的AI"的重要一步。 Wan-Streamer proves that a single unified model can handle all aspects of real-time audio-visual conversation — listening, understanding, speaking, and animating — without stitching together separate tools. This establishes a new benchmark for end-to-end multimodal streaming AI, showing that sub-second human-like interactive AI is practically achievable today.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

Wan-Streamer把文字、声音和视频画面全部"翻译"成一串交错排列的数字符号，由一个统一的Transformer模型同时处理输入和输出。它设计了专门的"因果编码器"和"因果解码器"，使模型可以像人类一样边听边说，而不是等对方说完才开始思考。核心技巧是"块因果注意力"——模型把内容分成一小块一小块地处理，每块只能看到之前的内容，从而实现增量式流式输出。此外，低延迟多模态调度机制确保音频、视频、文字的时间同步，让整个交互自然流畅。 Wan-Streamer converts text, audio, and video frames all into a single stream of tokens (think of it like a universal language that covers all three), then feeds everything into one giant Transformer model that handles both understanding and generation simultaneously. The key trick is "block-causal attention" — instead of waiting for a full sentence to arrive before responding, the model processes tiny chunks (160ms each) and starts generating replies almost immediately. Special causal encoders and decoders ensure audio and video stay in sync, like a TV broadcast where the picture and sound never drift apart.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{Total Latency} \approx \underbrace{200\text{ ms}}_{\text{model-side}} + \underbrace{350\text{ ms}}_{\text{network (bidirectional)}} \approx 550\text{ ms}\]
💡 总交互延迟 = 模型响应延迟（约200毫秒）+ 双向网络传输延迟（约350毫秒）≈ 550毫秒，实现亚秒级全双工对话。 Total interaction delay equals the model's own processing time (~200ms) plus the round-trip network travel time (~350ms), adding up to ~550ms — fast enough to feel like a real conversation.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

这项工作直接推动了AI视频客服、虚拟主播、远程医疗助手等产品的落地，让AI对话体验更接近真实的人际交流。从更长远来看，它为多模态AI统一建模提供了一套完整的工程范式，有望成为下一代交互式AI的基础架构。 This technology could power the next generation of AI video assistants, virtual companions, and customer service agents that feel as natural as talking to a real person. By proving that one model can replace an entire pipeline of specialized tools, it also simplifies building and deploying multimodal AI — making such systems cheaper and more reliable for real products.

🎯 系统拆解并评估AI智能体记忆系统，揭示没有"万能"架构。
A rigorous benchmark framework that dissects and compares AI agent memory systems beyond simple task success scores.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy把AI智能体的记忆想象成医生的病历柜。现在我们只看病人有没有康复来评价医生，但从没人检查病历是否整理有序、旧记录有没有及时更新、找到正确病历要花多长时间。这篇论文就像首次对这个病历柜做"全面审计"，给每个抽屉和文件夹单独打分，让我们终于能建起一个真正井然有序的"诊所"。Think of an AI agent's memory like a doctor's filing cabinet. Right now, we only judge the doctor by whether patients get better — but nobody checks if the files are organized, if old records get updated, or how long it takes to find the right chart. This paper is like the first-ever audit of that filing cabinet, rating every drawer and folder separately so we can finally build a truly well-organized medical office.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 AI智能体（比如聊天机器人或私人助手）需要"记忆"来记住过去的对话、事实和用户偏好。但现有的评测方法只看最终答案对不对，完全忽视了记忆系统本身是否高效、便宜、稳定。这就像评价一个图书馆，只看学生考试有没有及格，从不检查书架是否整齐、借书是否方便、书的内容是否过时。 AI agents (like chatbots or personal assistants) need memory to remember past conversations, facts, and user preferences — just like humans do. But current ways of testing these memory systems only check whether the agent got the final answer right, completely ignoring whether the memory itself works efficiently, cheaply, or reliably over time. This is like judging a library only by whether a student passed the exam, without ever checking if the books are organized, accessible, or up to date.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 首次将AI智能体记忆系统拆分为四个核心模块（存储、提取、检索、维护），并逐一深入评估，打破"黑盒"评测模式。 • 在11个数据集上测试了12种主流记忆系统和2种基线，是迄今为止最全面的横向对比研究。 • 发现没有任何一种记忆架构能在所有场景下都表现最优，选择哪种架构取决于具体任务的瓶颈所在。 • 证明"局部维护"（只更新改变的部分）比"全局重组"（每次重建整个记忆）更省钱、更高效。 • First systematic study to break AI agent memory into four clear components — storage, extraction, retrieval, and maintenance — and evaluate each independently. • Tested 12 real memory systems plus 2 baselines across 11 datasets, giving the most comprehensive comparison to date. • Discovered that no single memory design wins in all situations — the best choice always depends on the specific task, like picking the right tool for the right job. • Showed that "local" memory updates (fixing only what changed) are far cheaper than rebuilding memory from scratch every time.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究在5个基准测试任务、11个数据集上评测了12种主流智能体记忆系统（如MemGPT、Zep和基于图的系统）以及2种参考基线。这些数据集涵盖了开放域问答、多轮对话、动态知识更新和长程推理等多种任务类型。评估不仅包括最终任务准确率（F1、BLEU），还包括模块级指标，如检索精度、更新正确性、长期稳定性以及运营成本（以API调用次数和消耗的token数量衡量）。 The study evaluated 12 representative agent memory systems (such as MemGPT, Zep, and graph-based systems) plus 2 reference baselines across 5 benchmark workloads covering 11 datasets. These datasets spanned diverse tasks including open-domain QA, multi-session dialogue, dynamic knowledge updates, and long-horizon reasoning. Evaluation measured not just final task accuracy (F1, BLEU) but also module-level metrics like retrieval precision, update correctness, long-horizon stability, and operational cost (measured in API calls and tokens consumed).
📊 实验
结果		 没有任何单一系统在所有基准测试中都占优，最佳方案因任务类型而异。基于图的记忆系统在关系推理任务上表现出色，但运营成本比简单检索增强系统高出多达3倍。在动态更新场景中，局部维护策略比全局重组策略节省超过40%的成本。在长程任务中，若不主动维护记忆，检索精度会明显下降（最高达15–20%）。细粒度消融研究揭示，检索与路由模块是大多数任务的最大瓶颈，主导了端到端性能的大部分方差。 No single system dominated across all benchmarks — the best performer varied by task type. Graph-based memory systems showed strong performance on relational reasoning tasks but incurred up to 3× higher operational costs compared to simpler retrieval-augmented systems. Localized (targeted) memory maintenance was found to be significantly more cost-efficient than global reorganization strategies, with cost savings exceeding 40% in dynamic update scenarios. Retrieval precision degraded noticeably (by up to 15–20%) in long-horizon tasks when memory was not actively maintained. Fine-grained ablations revealed that the retrieval and routing module was the single biggest bottleneck for most workloads, accounting for the majority of end-to-end performance variance.
🏁 结论 本文证明了AI智能体不存在"万能"记忆架构——最优方案始终取决于具体任务和工作负载。通过提供首个模块化、成本感知的基准框架，为研究者和工程师指明了构建更智能、更经济、更稳健的智能体记忆系统的明确方向。 This paper proves that no one-size-fits-all memory architecture exists for AI agents — the best system always depends on the specific task and workload. By providing the first modular, cost-aware benchmark framework, it gives researchers and engineers a clear roadmap for building smarter, cheaper, and more reliable agent memory systems.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究团队设计了一个分析框架，将智能体记忆拆分成四个"积木块"：信息如何被存储和表示（比如选择用便条纸还是电子数据库）、如何从原始文本中提取关键事实、在执行任务时如何搜索和调度相关记忆、以及如何更新或删除过时或矛盾的记忆。他们对每个模块分别进行受控实验——就像单独测试汽车发动机的每个零部件——这样就能精确定位记忆系统成败的根本原因，而不只是看最终的"行驶速度"。 The researchers designed an analytical framework that breaks agent memory into four building blocks: how information is stored and represented (like choosing between a notebook vs. a database), how key facts are extracted from raw text, how relevant memories are searched and routed during a task, and how outdated or conflicting memories are updated or removed. They then ran controlled experiments on each module separately — like testing each part of a car engine individually — so they could pinpoint exactly what makes a memory system succeed or fail, rather than only looking at the car's top speed.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着AI助手逐渐融入日常生活——帮助工作、医疗和教育——它们可靠地记忆和更新信息的能力变得至关重要。本文为开发者提供了首套真正意义上的"诊断工具箱"，让他们精确了解AI记忆在哪里出问题，从而让未来的AI助手既更聪明又更省钱。 As AI assistants become part of daily life — helping with work, health, and education — their ability to reliably remember and update information becomes critical. This paper gives developers the first proper "diagnostic toolkit" to understand exactly where their AI's memory breaks down, so future assistants can be both smarter and cheaper to run.

Five benchmarks can predict a model's scores on 133 evals to within 4 points.
🗣️ 一句话比喻 · Plain-English Analogy就像医生以前每次体检都要做133项化验，而这篇论文发现只需5项关键血液指标就能告诉你几乎一切——省时、省钱、少挨好多针。Think of it like a doctor who used to order 133 different lab tests for every patient checkup — this paper discovers that just 5 key blood tests can tell you almost everything the full panel would, saving time, money, and a lot of needle pokes.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 每次AI公司发布新模型时，都要跑40多个标准测试，而且在训练过程中还要反复跑——光是评测就要耗费大量时间和算力。这非常昂贵。问题是：我们真的需要跑完所有测试吗？还是说几个关键测试就能告诉我们全部结论？ Every time an AI lab releases a new model, they run it through 40+ standardized tests — and they repeat this dozens of times during training just to track progress. This is incredibly expensive in time and computing cost. The question is: do we really need to run ALL of these tests, or could a small handful of them tell us everything we need to know?
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 发现所有AI基准测试的分数，本质上只由两个潜在数字决定——整个评测体系远比看起来简单。 • 构建了"BenchPress"——一个智能预测系统，可以用少量已知分数推断缺失的测试结果，像给AI成绩单"填空"。 • 找到了仅需5个基准测试的"魔法组合"，就能预测模型在133项测试上的分数，误差在4分以内。 • 加入置信度机制，告诉你什么时候可以信任预测，什么时候需要真正去跑测试。 • Discovered that all AI benchmark scores are essentially driven by just TWO underlying numbers — meaning the whole evaluation landscape is far simpler than it looks. • Built "BenchPress," a smart prediction system that fills in missing benchmark scores from just a few known ones, like a crossword solver for AI report cards. • Identified a magic set of just 5 benchmarks that can predict a model's full scorecard (133 tests) to within ~4 points of accuracy. • Added a confidence layer so the system tells you when to trust its predictions and when to actually run the real test.
🧪 实验
内容		 研究者整理了84个前沿AI模型在133个基准上的得分矩阵（共2604个格子，23.3%已填）。通过"遮住"已知分数再预测的方式测试BenchPress的准确性，对比了不同秩的矩阵分解方法和简单基线（如列平均值）。还通过贪心搜索找出预测效果最好的最小基准子集。 The researchers compiled a public score matrix of 84 frontier AI models across 133 benchmarks. They tested how well missing scores could be recovered using matrix completion methods, comparing rank-1, rank-2, and rank-3 factorizations against simple baselines like column averages. They evaluated BenchPress by hiding known scores and measuring how closely the predictions matched reality, and they ran a greedy search to find the smallest subset of benchmarks that best predicts the rest.
📊 实验
结果		 • BenchPress预测缺失基准分数的平均绝对误差仅4.6分。 • 仅5个基准{GPQA-D, HLE, Codeforces, MMLU-Pro, ARC-AGI-1}就能将133项测试的完整成绩预测到误差3.93分以内。 • 更便宜的5项组合{GPQA-D, MMLU-Pro, Aider Polyglot, MATH-500, AIME 2026}预测误差4.55分。 • 两个因子解释了84个模型在133个基准上超过90%的差异。 • 评分矩阵覆盖84个前沿模型×133个基准=2604个数据格，23.3%已填充。 • BenchPress recovers held-out benchmark scores to within 4.6 points on average (mean absolute error). • A set of just 5 benchmarks {GPQA-D, HLE, Codeforces, MMLU-Pro, ARC-AGI-1} can recover a model's full 133-benchmark scorecard to within 3.93 points. • For a cheaper option, {GPQA-D, MMLU-Pro, Aider Polyglot, MATH-500, AIME 2026} predicts scores to within 4.55 points. • Two factors explain over 90% of variance across all 133 benchmarks among the 84 models tested. • The score matrix covers 84 frontier models × 133 benchmarks = 2,604 data cells, with 23.3% filled.
🏁 结论 本文证明了AI基准测试分数高度冗余——仅两个潜在因子就能解释几乎一切，只需跑5个精心挑选的测试，就能以4分误差预测模型在133项评测上的表现。BenchPress将AI评估从昂贵的全量测试变成了一个聪明高效的过程。 This paper proves that AI benchmark scores are highly redundant — just two hidden factors explain almost everything, and running just 5 carefully chosen tests can predict a model's performance on 133 benchmarks to within 4 points. BenchPress turns AI evaluation from an expensive, exhaustive process into a smart, efficient one.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究者首先建了一张大表：84个顶级AI模型（如GPT-4、Claude、Gemini等）在133个不同基准上的得分——不过只有23%的格子是填好的，因为没有哪个模型跑了所有测试。他们用一种叫"矩阵分解"的数学技术（类似Netflix预测你喜欢哪部电影）找到隐藏规律。发现仅需两个因子就能解释模型间90%以上的差异——本质上是"整体有多聪明"和"聪明的方式"两个维度。BenchPress系统在"logit空间"（一种处理百分比分数更精准的数学变换）中运用这个二因子结构，预测任意缺失分数，并用置信层评估每个预测的可信度。 The researchers first built a giant score table: 84 top AI models (like GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, etc.) scored on 133 different benchmarks — though only 23% of cells were actually filled in, since no single model runs every test. They then applied a math technique called matrix factorization (like how Netflix predicts your movie ratings) to find the hidden structure. They discovered that just TWO factors explain over 90% of the difference between models across all benchmarks — essentially a "how smart is it overall" score and a "what kind of smart" score. They then built BenchPress, which uses this two-factor structure in "logit space" (a mathematical transformation that handles percentage scores more accurately) to predict any missing score. A confidence layer estimates how reliable each prediction is based on how well-connected that benchmark is to the others.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\hat{M} = U V^\top, \quad \text{rank}(\hat{M}) = 2\]
💡 BenchPress用秩为2的矩阵（两个因子的乘积）来近似完整的模型-基准得分矩阵，意味着所有模型的所有分数可以用两个潜在维度重建。 BenchPress approximates the full model-benchmark score matrix as the product of two small matrices (rank-2), meaning every model's scores on every benchmark can be reconstructed from just two underlying numbers per model.
\[\text{MAE} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}|\hat{s}_i - s_i| \leq 4.6\]

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

团队构建了NatureGym自动化流水线：它读取一篇科学论文，然后自动为这篇论文的核心任务搭建一个干净、独立的软件运行环境。就像一个机器人实验室助理，先把实验所需的所有材料和工具摆好，再把任务交给AI来完成。十种主流AI智能体配置在这90个任务上接受了严格测试：禁止访问互联网、计算资源固定、评分标准统一。判断标准是AI能否超越论文本身报告的最优结果（SOTA）。 The team built NatureGym, an automated pipeline that reads a published scientific paper and automatically sets up a clean, self-contained software environment for that paper's core task. Think of it like a robot lab assistant that reads a recipe (the paper), then neatly lays out all the right ingredients and tools on the bench before handing it over to the AI. Ten different frontier AI agent configurations were then tested on these 90 tasks under strict conditions: no internet access, fixed compute budgets, and standardized scoring. Results were measured by whether the agent could beat the paper's own reported state-of-the-art score.
