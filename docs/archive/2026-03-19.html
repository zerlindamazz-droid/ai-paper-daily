Process Supervision for Chain-of-Thought Reasoning via Monte Carlo Net Information Gain 基于蒙特卡洛净信息增益的思维链推理过程监督

🧑‍🔬 Corentin Royer, Debarun Bhattacharjya, Gaetano Rossiello, Andrea Giovannini et al.

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 用信息论自动生成推理步骤质量标签，高效监督大模型思维链
Information-theoretic step-level labels enable scalable, efficient supervision of LLM chain-of-thought reasoning.

❓ Problem · 解决什么问题

大型语言模型在进行多步推理时，中间步骤的错误会像"滚雪球"一样不断传播，最终导致错误答案。现有的"过程奖励模型"（PRM）虽然可以逐步打分来纠错，但训练这类模型要么依赖昂贵的人工标注，要么需要大量计算资源，难以大规模推广。 Large language models performing multi-step reasoning are vulnerable to cascading errors, where a mistake in one intermediate step corrupts all subsequent steps. Existing process reward models (PRMs) that score each step individually require either expensive human annotations or computationally intensive automatic labeling methods, making scalable training difficult.

⚙️ Method · 方法

本文提出用信息论中的"净信息增益"来衡量每个推理步骤的质量：若一个步骤让模型更接近正确答案，则该步骤获得正向标签，反之则获得负向标签。具体通过蒙特卡洛采样估算每步对正确答案概率的影响，无需人工介入即可自动生成步骤级标签。关键创新在于将算法复杂度从此前方法的 O(N log N) 降低至 O(N)，大幅提升了计算效率，使其更适合大规模应用。 The method assigns a quality signal to each reasoning step by estimating how much that step increases or decreases the likelihood of producing the correct final answer, using an Information Theory concept called Net Information Gain. Monte Carlo sampling is used to approximate this signal automatically, without any human annotation. This reformulation reduces the computational complexity from O(N log N) to O(N), making the labeling process significantly more efficient and scalable for large datasets and diverse tasks.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{NIG}(s_i) = \log P(a^* \mid s_1, \ldots, s_i) - \log P(a^* \mid s_1, \ldots, s_{i-1})\]
💡 净信息增益（NIG）：衡量第 i 个推理步骤相对于前一步骤，使正确答案概率变化了多少（对数尺度）。若值为正，说明该步骤有益；若为负，说明该步骤有害。 Net Information Gain (NIG): measures how much step i changes the log-probability of the correct answer compared to the previous step. A positive value means the step helps; a negative value means it hurts.
\[\text{Complexity: } \mathcal{O}(N) \text{ vs. previous } \mathcal{O}(N \log N)\]
💡 本方法将自动标注的计算复杂度从 O(N log N) 降至 O(N)，意味着随着推理步骤数量增加，计算量的增长更为平缓，可扩展性更强。 The proposed method reduces the computational complexity of automatic step labeling from O(N log N) to O(N), meaning the cost grows linearly rather than super-linearly with the number of reasoning steps.

📊 Results · 结果

该方法在多个推理基准测试中验证了有效性，涵盖数学、Python编程、SQL查询和科学问答等多种任务类型。在"最优K选一"（best-of-K）评估设置下，基于本方法生成的标签训练的PRM能够有效筛选出质量更高的思维链，整体性能优于基线方法，同时计算开销显著降低（复杂度从 O(N log N) 降至 O(N)）。 The proposed method was validated across diverse reasoning benchmarks including mathematics, Python programming, SQL, and scientific question answering. In best-of-K evaluation settings, PRMs trained with the automatically generated Information Gain labels consistently outperformed baseline methods in selecting high-quality chain-of-thought reasoning paths. The approach also achieved a concrete reduction in computational complexity from O(N log N) to O(N), representing a significant efficiency improvement over prior automatic labeling methods.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

过程监督是提升大模型推理可靠性的关键技术，但高昂的标注成本一直是瓶颈。本文将信息论引入过程奖励模型的自动标注，在降低成本的同时兼顾了跨领域的泛化能力，为构建更可靠的推理型AI系统提供了实用路径。 Process supervision is a critical technique for building reliable LLM reasoning systems, but annotation costs have been a major bottleneck to scaling. This work demonstrates that information-theoretic signals can replace expensive labeling pipelines while generalizing across diverse task domains, paving the way for more trustworthy and efficient AI reasoning at scale.

Beyond Muon: MUD (MomentUm Decorrelation) for Faster Transformer Training 超越Muon：用于更快Transformer训练的MUD（动量去相关）优化器

🧑‍🔬 Ben S. Southworth, Stephen Thomas

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 MUD用三角白化替代极分解，大幅提升Transformer训练速度
MUD replaces Muon's costly polar decomposition with triangular whitening, achieving up to 3× faster transformer training.

❓ Problem · 解决什么问题

训练大型Transformer模型需要高效的优化器。Muon通过"正交化动量"提升训练效果，但其核心操作——极分解——需要多次大型矩阵乘法，计算开销大且因硬件而异，严重拖慢训练速度。 Training large Transformer models demands efficient optimizers. Muon improves training by orthogonalizing gradient momentum, but its core polar decomposition step requires multiple expensive large matrix multiplications. This overhead is substantial and varies across hardware, significantly slowing down wall-clock training time.

⚙️ Method · 方法

MUD（动量去相关）用一种受经典Gram–Schmidt正交化和Gauss-Seidel迭代启发的三角（类Cholesky）白化操作来替代Muon的极分解步骤。该方法对梯度动量的Gram矩阵做对称Gauss-Seidel预处理，计算量远低于极分解。论文证明了行正交矩阵是MUD映射的不动点，并在不动点附近证明了二次局部收敛性，为方法提供了坚实的理论保障。 MUD replaces Muon's polar decomposition with a triangular (Cholesky-like) whitening surrogate inspired by classical Gram–Schmidt orthogonalization and Gauss-Seidel iteration. It applies symmetric Gauss-Seidel preconditioning to the Gram matrix of the momentum, which is far cheaper to compute than a full polar factor. The authors prove that row-orthonormal matrices are fixed points of the MUD map and establish quadratic local convergence near those fixed points, providing a solid theoretical foundation.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[G = M M^\top, \quad M \leftarrow L^{-1} M\]
💡 MUD的核心步骤：计算动量矩阵M的Gram矩阵G=MM^T，然后用其Cholesky因子L对M进行左乘求逆，实现三角白化（去相关）。 The core MUD step: compute the Gram matrix G of momentum M, then left-multiply M by the inverse of G's Cholesky factor L, achieving triangular whitening (decorrelation).
\[\|G_{k+1} - I\| = O(\|G_k - I\|^2)\]
💡 MUD在不动点（行正交矩阵）附近具有二次局部收敛性：每次迭代后，Gram矩阵与单位矩阵的偏差以平方速度缩小。 MUD exhibits quadratic local convergence near its fixed point: the deviation of the Gram matrix from the identity shrinks quadratically with each iteration, guaranteeing fast local convergence.

📊 Results · 结果

与调优后的AdamW和Muon相比，MUD在"时间-困惑度"指标上实现了一致的10–50%挂钟时间提升。相对于Muon，MUD将峰值吞吐量（tokens/s）提升约1.3–2.6×（多数设置），在A100上训练GPT-2 Large时提升接近3×。在ESM-2 150M蛋白质语言模型训练中，MUD以显著更少的挂钟时间达到与Muon相当的验证困惑度。MUD每步收敛略慢于Muon，但优化器本身开销大幅降低。 MUD achieves consistent 10–50% wall-clock time improvements over tuned AdamW and Muon in time-to-perplexity benchmarks. Relative to Muon, MUD improves peak throughput (tokens/s) by approximately 1.3–2.6× across most settings, and up to nearly 3× on GPT-2 Large on an A100 GPU. In protein language model training (ESM-2 150M), MUD matches Muon-level validation perplexity in significantly less wall-clock time. While MUD converges slightly slower per step than Muon, its drastically reduced optimizer overhead more than compensates.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

MUD在不牺牲模型最终性能的前提下，大幅降低了正交化动量优化器的计算开销，使这类先进优化器在实际工程中更具可行性。该方法还配有严格的收敛性证明，为未来设计更高效的优化器提供了理论基础。 MUD makes orthogonalized-momentum optimizers practically viable by dramatically cutting their computational overhead without sacrificing final model quality. The theoretical convergence guarantees also provide a principled foundation for designing the next generation of efficient, whitening-based optimizers for large-scale AI training.

EVA: Aligning Video World Models with Executable Robot Actions via Inverse Dynamics Rewards EVA：通过逆动力学奖励将视频世界模型与可执行机器人动作对齐

🧑‍🔬 Ruixiang Wang, Qingming Liu, Yueci Deng, Guiliang Liu et al.

图 Figure · Page 2 of paper

🎯 用强化学习训练奖励模型，弥合视频生成与机器人物理执行之间的鸿沟
EVA uses RL post-training to align robot video world models with physically executable actions via inverse dynamics rewards.

❓ Problem · 解决什么问题

视频生成模型被用于机器人"世界模型"——先生成未来画面，再将画面转换为机器人动作指令。但生成的视频画面看起来合理，却往往违反刚体物理和运动学约束，导致机器人收到不稳定甚至不可执行的控制命令。这种"视觉生成"与"物理可执行性"之间的差距，被作者称为"可执行性鸿沟"。 Video world models for robotics generate visually plausible future frames, but these frames often violate rigid-body physics and kinematic constraints, causing unstable or infeasible robot control commands when decoded. This disconnect between visual coherence and physical executability — termed the "executability gap" — is a core challenge. Existing workarounds like rejection sampling are too slow due to the high cost of video generation.

⚙️ Method · 方法

EVA提出了一个强化学习后训练框架。首先，在真实机器人轨迹数据上训练一个逆动力学模型（IDM），该模型能将视频帧序列转换为动作序列。然后，将这个IDM复用为奖励模型：让它解码生成视频所产生的动作，并根据速度、加速度、加加速度（jerk）等指标奖励平滑运动，同时惩罚违反机器人本体约束（如关节限位）的动作。即便生成视频存在明显视觉瑕疵，奖励信号依然有效，因为视觉瑕疵往往直接对应不稳定或越界的动作输出。 EVA introduces a reinforcement learning post-training framework built around an inverse dynamics model (IDM) trained on real robot trajectory data. This IDM is repurposed as a reward model: it decodes generated video frames into action sequences and scores them based on motion smoothness — rewarding low velocity, acceleration, and jerk — while penalizing actions that violate embodiment constraints such as joint limits. This reward signal guides RL fine-tuning of the video world model, and crucially remains informative even when generated videos have severe visual artifacts, since those artifacts consistently map to physically invalid actions.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[r = r_{\text{smooth}} - \lambda \cdot r_{\text{constraint}}\]
💡 EVA的奖励函数由两部分组成：平滑奖励（鼓励速度、加速度、加加速度小）减去约束惩罚项（惩罚违反关节限位等本体约束的动作），λ为平衡权重。 EVA's reward combines a smoothness reward (encouraging low velocity, acceleration, and jerk in the decoded actions) minus a weighted penalty for actions that violate the robot's embodiment constraints such as joint limits.
\[\hat{a}_{t} = \text{IDM}(f_t, f_{t+1})\]
💡 逆动力学模型（IDM）以相邻两帧视频为输入，预测出该时间步的机器人动作，从而将生成视频帧序列转化为可评估的动作序列。 The inverse dynamics model takes two consecutive generated video frames as input and predicts the robot action at that timestep, converting the visual rollout into an evaluable action sequence.

📊 Results · 结果

在RoboTwin基准测试和真实双臂机器人实验中，EVA均有效减少了生成视频中针对特定机器人本体的视觉伪影。在下游任务执行成功率上取得了显著提升，实验验证了相比拒绝采样等推理时方法，EVA在训练效率和执行稳定性上的优势。真实双臂机器人实验进一步证明了该方法的实际可部署性。（注：论文未在摘要中披露具体数值，结果以基准对比和成功率提升呈现。） On the RoboTwin benchmark and a real bimanual robot platform, EVA measurably reduces embodiment-specific visual artifacts in generated rollouts and improves downstream task execution success rates. The framework outperforms inference-time baselines such as rejection sampling in both efficiency and control stability. Real-robot experiments on a bimanual system confirm the method's deployability beyond simulation. (Note: the abstract does not report specific numerical metrics; full quantitative results are detailed in the main paper.)

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

该工作为视频世界模型引入了物理可执行性这一关键约束，填补了视觉生成与机器人控制之间长期被忽视的鸿沟。EVA的框架通用性强——只需真实机器人轨迹数据即可构建奖励模型，无需额外物理仿真器，为未来具身智能的世界模型训练提供了新范式。 This work addresses a critical but underexplored gap in robot learning: video world models that look realistic but produce physically infeasible actions. By embedding physical executability directly into the training objective via RL, EVA offers a scalable and simulation-free path to more reliable robot world models, with broad implications for embodied AI and real-world robot deployment.
