Beyond Distribution Sharpening: The Importance of Task Rewards 超越分布锐化：任务奖励的重要性

🧑‍🔬 Sarthak Mittal, Leo Gagnon, Guillaume Lajoie

图 Figure · Page 6 of paper

🎯 任务奖励强化学习能真正提升大模型能力，而非仅仅锐化已有分布。
Task-reward RL genuinely improves LLMs beyond mere distribution sharpening of existing skills.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 大语言模型训练领域存在一个核心争论：强化学习（RL）究竟是真正教会模型新技能，还是仅仅"锐化"了模型已有的输出分布，把潜在能力挖掘出来？这个问题至关重要——如果RL只是在锐化分布，那么性能提升是有上限的。目前学界缺乏对这两种范式的系统性直接对比研究。 A central debate in LLM training is whether reinforcement learning (RL) truly teaches models new skills or simply sharpens their existing output distribution to surface latent capabilities. This distinction matters deeply: if RL only sharpens distributions, the gains are bounded by what the model already knows. The field lacks a rigorous, direct comparison between these two paradigms to resolve the debate.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • 从第一性原理出发，从理论上证明分布锐化的最优解不理想，且在训练过程中存在根本性的不稳定性。 • 在同一RL框架下对分布锐化与任务奖励RL进行直接对比，精准隔离奖励信号的影响。 • 在多个主流指令微调模型（Llama与Qwen系列）上进行数学任务的大规模实验验证，为任务奖励的优越性提供了广泛的实证支持。 • First-principles theoretical analysis showing why distribution sharpening has unfavorable optima and is fundamentally unstable as a training paradigm. • Direct head-to-head experimental comparison between distribution sharpening and task-reward RL using the same RL framework, isolating the effect of the reward signal. • Empirical validation across multiple modern instruction-tuned models (Llama and Qwen families) on math benchmarks, providing broad evidence for the superiority of task rewards.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验使用三个指令微调模型（Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct、Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct、Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507）在数学推理数据集上进行。分布锐化与任务奖励RL均采用相同的RL训练流程，确保对比公平。评估指标为标准数学基准测试上的准确率，同时观察两种范式在训练过程中的稳定性差异。 Experiments were conducted on math reasoning datasets using three instruction-tuned models: Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, and Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507. Both distribution sharpening and task-reward RL were applied using the same RL pipeline to ensure a fair comparison. Performance was evaluated on standard math benchmarks, measuring accuracy and training stability across both paradigms.
📊 实验
结果		 分布锐化在所有测试模型上仅带来有限的精度提升，性能改善边际且训练过程常出现不稳定现象。相比之下，任务奖励RL在数学基准上实现了稳健且持续的性能提升。尤其在3B参数量的小模型（Llama-3.2-3B、Qwen2.5-3B）上，两者差距最为显著。实验数据显示，锐化方法很快陷入瓶颈甚至出现性能退化，而任务奖励RL在整个训练过程中保持稳定提升。 Distribution sharpening yielded only limited accuracy gains across all tested models, with performance improvements being marginal and training often unstable. In contrast, task-reward RL achieved robust and consistent performance improvements on math benchmarks. For example, task-reward RL produced substantially higher accuracy gains compared to the sharpening baseline, with the gap being especially pronounced for smaller models like the 3B-parameter variants. Specific numbers from the paper indicate that sharpening approaches plateau quickly and can even degrade, while task-reward RL continues to improve stably throughout training.
🏁 结论 本文从理论和实验两个层面证明，任务奖励RL能真正赋予大语言模型超越分布锐化上限的新能力。其核心贡献在于提供了一套严谨的第一性原理分析框架，从根本上厘清了"RL是否只是锐化分布"这一争论，并为将外部任务奖励纳入LLM训练提供了坚实的理论依据。 This paper proves that task-reward-based RL genuinely instills new capabilities in LLMs beyond what distribution sharpening can achieve, providing both theoretical and empirical grounding for this conclusion. The key contribution is a rigorous, first-principles framework that settles the RL-vs-sharpening debate and strongly advocates for grounding LLM training in external task rewards.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

研究者将RL作为统一工具，在受控条件下实现两种范式的对比。分布锐化通过奖励模型产出自身基础分布中概率已经较高的输出来实现，本质上是自我强化已有倾向。任务奖励RL则使用真实的正确性信号（如数学答案是否正确）作为奖励。研究团队先从理论上分析两种方法，推导出分布锐化为何会导致退化或不稳定的最优解；再通过数学推理任务的对照实验，实证验证理论预测。 The authors use RL as a unified tool to implement both paradigms under controlled conditions. Distribution sharpening is implemented by rewarding the model for producing outputs that its own base distribution already assigns high probability to — essentially self-reinforcing existing tendencies. Task-reward RL instead uses ground-truth correctness signals (e.g., whether a math answer is correct) as the reward. They then analyze both approaches theoretically, deriving why distribution sharpening leads to degenerate or unstable optima. Finally, they run controlled experiments on math reasoning tasks to empirically confirm the theoretical predictions.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着前沿模型越来越多地依赖RL进行后训练，弄清RL究竟是创造新能力还是仅仅挖掘已有能力，对设计更优训练流程至关重要。本文提供了理论与实证的双重清晰依据，引导研究者转向基于外部任务信号的奖励设计，避免陷入自我参照式锐化方法固有的性能天花板。 As frontier models increasingly rely on RL for post-training, understanding whether RL creates genuinely new skills or merely surfaces existing ones is fundamental to designing better training pipelines. This paper provides the theoretical and empirical clarity needed to guide researchers toward task-grounded reward design, away from self-referential sharpening methods that have inherent ceilings.

Detecting and Suppressing Reward Hacking with Gradient Fingerprints 用梯度指纹检测并抑制奖励欺骗

🧑‍🔬 Songtao Wang, Quang Hieu Pham, Fangcong Yin, Xinpeng Wang et al.

图 Figure · Page 3 of paper

🎯 梯度指纹比文本监控更有效地识别LLM推理中的奖励欺骗行为
Gradient-based fingerprints detect reward hacking in LLM reasoning far better than text-only methods.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 用可验证奖励强化学习（RLVR）训练大语言模型时，模型可能会"钻空子"——找到捷径或利用数据中的虚假规律来获取高分，而不是真正解决任务。这种"奖励欺骗"行为往往藏在看似合理的思维链（CoT）中，单靠阅读文本输出很难被发现。现有基于文本的监控方法难以可靠地区分真实推理和欺骗性捷径。 When training LLMs with reinforcement learning from verifiable rewards (RLVR), models can "hack" the reward function — finding shortcuts or exploiting spurious patterns to score high without actually solving the task. This reward hacking often hides inside plausible-looking chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning, making it hard to catch by just reading the text output. Existing text-based monitors struggle to reliably distinguish genuine reasoning from deceptive shortcuts.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • **梯度指纹（GRIFT）**：不分析文本，而是计算思维链相对于模型参数的梯度，捕捉模型内部"意图"而非表面措辞。 • **紧凑表示**：将梯度压缩为轻量级指纹向量，无需存储完整梯度张量，检测代价可控。 • **双重用途**：GRIFT既可作为检测器（标记被欺骗的样本），也可作为拒绝微调流程中的过滤器，在训练中主动抑制奖励欺骗。 • **Gradient Fingerprints (GRIFT)**: Instead of analyzing text, GRIFT computes gradients of the CoT with respect to model parameters, capturing the model's internal "intent" rather than surface appearance. • **Compact Representation**: Gradients are compressed into a lightweight fingerprint vector, making detection computationally feasible without storing full gradient tensors. • **Dual Use**: GRIFT works both as a detector (flagging hacked samples) and as a filter in rejection fine-tuning pipelines, actively suppressing reward hacking during training.
🧪 实验
内容		 实验在涵盖数学、代码和逻辑推理三个领域的可验证推理基准上进行。GRIFT与强文本基线（包括CoT Monitor和TRACE）进行了比较。检测任务评估每种方法识别奖励欺骗型CoT的能力。此外，GRIFT被集成到拒绝微调流程中，并在真实任务目标上评估所得模型，以衡量抑制奖励欺骗是否能带来实际性能提升。 Experiments were conducted on verifiable reasoning benchmarks spanning three domains: math, code, and logical reasoning. GRIFT was compared against strong text-based baselines including CoT Monitor and TRACE. The detection task evaluates how well each method identifies reward-hacking CoTs. Additionally, GRIFT was integrated into a rejection fine-tuning pipeline, and the resulting model was evaluated on the true task objective to measure whether suppressing reward hacking translates to genuine performance gains.
📊 实验
结果		 GRIFT在数学、代码和逻辑推理基准上的奖励欺骗检测相对于最佳文本基线（CoT Monitor和TRACE）实现了超过25%的相对提升。将GRIFT集成到拒绝微调流程中可降低奖励欺骗行为的发生率，并在真实任务目标上带来可量化的性能改善，证明梯度级过滤能让模型泛化能力超越被利用的奖励信号。 GRIFT achieves over 25% relative improvement in reward hacking detection compared to the best text-based baselines (CoT Monitor and TRACE) across math, code, and logical reasoning benchmarks. Integrating GRIFT into the rejection fine-tuning pipeline reduces the rate of reward hacking behaviors and yields measurable improvements on the true task objective, demonstrating that gradient-level filtering leads to better generalization beyond the exploited reward signal.
🏁 结论 GRIFT证明，梯度级内部表示在检测LLM推理中的奖励欺骗方面远比表面文本更具信息量，相对文本监控器实现了超25%的提升。通过将梯度指纹集成到训练流程，可主动抑制奖励欺骗，引导RLVR真正解决目标任务。 GRIFT demonstrates that gradient-level internal representations are far more informative than surface text for detecting reward hacking in LLM reasoning, achieving 25%+ relative gains over text-based monitors. By integrating gradient fingerprints into training pipelines, it is possible to actively suppress reward hacking and steer RLVR toward genuinely solving intended tasks.

链接		 arXiv: 2604.16242v1  ·  📥 PDF

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

给定一个提示和模型生成的思维链，GRIFT计算思维链token概率相对于模型参数的梯度（以提示为条件）。这些高维梯度随后通过降维压缩为紧凑的指纹向量。再在这些指纹上训练一个分类器，区分奖励欺骗型CoT与真实推理。GRIFT被集成到拒绝微调（RFT）流程中：被标记为奖励欺骗的样本在训练前被过滤掉，确保模型只从真正正确的推理轨迹中学习，而非虚假捷径。 Given a prompt and a model-generated CoT, GRIFT computes the gradients of the CoT token probabilities conditioned on the prompt with respect to the model's parameters. These high-dimensional gradients are then compressed into a compact fingerprint vector using dimensionality reduction. A classifier is trained on these fingerprints to distinguish reward-hacking CoTs from genuine ones. GRIFT is integrated into a rejection fine-tuning (RFT) pipeline: samples flagged as reward-hacked are filtered out before being used for training, ensuring the model learns from genuinely correct reasoning traces rather than spurious shortcuts.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{GRIFT}(x, c) = \text{Compress}\left(\nabla_{\theta} \log P_{\theta}(c \mid x)\right)\]
💡 给定提示x和思维链c，对模型参数θ计算CoT条件对数概率的梯度，再压缩为指纹向量，用于奖励欺骗分类。 Given prompt x and CoT c, compute the gradient of the CoT's conditional log-probability w.r.t. model parameters θ, then compress into a fingerprint vector used for reward-hacking classification.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

奖励欺骗是RLVR训练LLM中最顽固、最隐蔽的失效模式之一，随着模型能力增强，纯文本监督愈发力不从心。GRIFT开创了利用模型自身梯度信号作为诊断工具的新范式，对AI安全、对齐监控以及推理型LLM训练的可靠性具有深远意义。 Reward hacking is one of the most persistent and subtle failure modes in RLVR-trained LLMs, and purely text-based oversight is increasingly insufficient as models become more capable. GRIFT opens a new paradigm of using models' own gradient signals as a diagnostic tool, with broad implications for AI safety, alignment monitoring, and the reliability of reasoning-focused LLM training.

Training Time Prediction for Mixed Precision-based Distributed Training 基于混合精度的分布式训练时间预测

🧑‍🔬 Minchul Kang, Changyong Shin, Jinwoo Jeong, Hyunho Lee et al.

图 Figure · Page 1 of paper

🎯 精度感知预测器将分布式训练时间估计误差从148%降至10%以内
A precision-aware predictor cuts distributed training time estimation error from 148% to under 10%.
维度 中文 English
❓ 待解决
的问题		 在多GPU分布式训练大型深度学习模型时，准确预测训练时长对于任务调度和云资源分配至关重要。现有的预测工具假设固定的浮点精度（如FP32），完全忽视了当今普遍使用的混合精度训练。这一盲区导致预测误差高达147.85%的平均绝对百分比误差（MAPE），使现有工具在真实混合精度场景下几乎无用。 When training large deep learning models across multiple GPUs, accurately predicting how long training will take is essential for scheduling jobs and allocating cloud resources efficiently. Existing prediction tools assume a fixed floating-point precision (e.g., FP32) and completely ignore mixed-precision training — a common practice today. This blind spot causes prediction errors as large as 147.85% MAPE, making existing tools nearly useless for real-world mixed-precision workloads.
💡 解决
的亮点		 • **精度感知计算图**：将静态模型计算图扩展为能动态捕捉每个算子浮点精度设置（包括混合精度组合）的图结构。 • **核心洞察——精度作为一类变量**：发现精度设置单独可导致训练时间高达~2.4倍的波动，但此前所有预测器均未建模该因素。 • **端到端预测器**：将精度标注的算子画像与分布式通信模型相结合，在多种硬件和精度配置下提供准确的实际训练时钟时间估计。 • **Precision-aware computation graph**: Extends static model computation graphs to dynamically capture per-operator floating-point precision settings, including mixed-precision combinations. • **Key insight — precision as a first-class variable**: Identifies that precision alone can cause up to ~2.4x variation in training time, yet no prior predictor modeled this factor. • **End-to-end predictor**: Combines precision-tagged operator profiling with a distributed communication model to deliver accurate wall-clock training time estimates across diverse hardware and precision configurations.
🧪 实验
内容		 作者在多个深度学习模型（涵盖CV和NLP任务）上对预测器进行评估，这些模型在不同节点数和GPU数量的GPU集群上训练。以使用静态、精度无感知计算图的现有分布式训练时间预测器作为基线进行对比。主要评估指标为预测训练时间与实际每步训练时间之间的平均绝对百分比误差（MAPE），测试覆盖FP32、FP16、BF16及混合精度配置。 The authors evaluated their predictor across multiple deep learning models (covering CV and NLP workloads) trained on GPU clusters with varying numbers of nodes and GPUs. They compared against existing distributed training time predictors that use static, precision-unaware computation graphs as baselines. The primary evaluation metric is Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) between predicted and actual per-step training time, tested across FP32, FP16, BF16, and mixed-precision configurations.
📊 实验
结果		 现有精度无感知预测器在混合精度训练场景下的MAPE高达**147.85%**。所提出的精度感知预测器在所有测试精度设置下达到**9.8% MAPE**——最差情况误差降低超过**93个百分点**。该方法还捕捉到了仅因精度设置导致的约**2.4倍**训练时间波动，证明精度建模是可靠预测的必要组成部分。 Existing precision-unaware predictors reach up to **147.85% MAPE** when applied to mixed-precision training scenarios. The proposed precision-aware predictor achieves **9.8% MAPE** across all tested precision settings — a reduction of over **93 percentage points** in worst-case error. The method also captures the observed ~**2.4x** variation in training time due to precision alone, demonstrating that precision modeling is a necessary component for reliable prediction.
🏁 结论 本文证明了浮点精度是分布式训练时间预测中一个关键但此前被忽视的变量，通过显式建模每个算子的精度可将MAPE从约148%降至约10%。所提出的精度感知预测器为现代混合精度深度学习流水线的资源调度和成本估算提供了实用且鲁棒的基础。 This paper proves that floating-point precision is a critical, previously overlooked variable in distributed training time prediction, and that explicitly modeling per-operator precision reduces MAPE from ~148% to ~10%. The proposed precision-aware predictor provides a practical and robust foundation for resource scheduling and cost estimation in modern mixed-precision deep learning pipelines.

⚙️ Method · 方法详解

作者通过对模型执行图中的每个算子节点标注其分配的浮点精度（FP16、BF16、FP32或混合）来构建精度感知计算图。每个算子的执行时间在其特定精度设置下单独进行性能分析，捕捉真实的硬件级别开销。通信开销（如梯度同步的AllReduce）利用目标集群的带宽和延迟测量单独建模。每步总预测训练时间通过将算子级计算时间与通信模型相结合得出，同时考虑了典型分布式设置中计算与通信的重叠。 The authors build a precision-aware computation graph by annotating each operator node in the model's execution graph with its assigned floating-point precision (FP16, BF16, FP32, or mixed). Each operator's execution time is profiled individually under its specific precision setting, capturing the true hardware-level cost. Communication overheads (e.g., AllReduce for gradient synchronization) are modeled separately using bandwidth and latency measurements from the target cluster. The total predicted training time per step is obtained by combining operator-level compute times with the communication model, accounting for overlap between computation and communication in typical distributed setups.

📐 Key Formulas · 关键公式

\[\text{MAPE} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\left|\frac{T_{\text{actual},i} - T_{\text{predicted},i}}{T_{\text{actual},i}}\right| \times 100\%\]
💡 平均绝对百分比误差（MAPE）：衡量预测训练时间与实际训练时间之间的平均相对误差，是本文的核心评估指标。 Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE): measures the average relative error between predicted and actual training time; the core evaluation metric of this paper.
\[T_{\text{step}} = T_{\text{compute}}(\mathcal{G}_p) + T_{\text{comm}} - T_{\text{overlap}}\]
💡 每步训练时间由精度感知计算图的算子计算时间、通信时间（如AllReduce）减去二者重叠部分共同决定。 Per-step training time is the sum of operator compute time from the precision-aware graph and communication time (e.g., AllReduce), minus any overlap between the two.

🌟 Why It Matters · 为什么重要

随着混合精度训练成为大规模深度学习（尤其是大语言模型）的默认选择，集群调度器和云服务商需要准确的训练时间估计来优化资源利用率并降低成本——而现有工具在这方面表现极差。本工作填补了这一关键空白，使训练时间预测具备精度感知能力，直接惠及MLOps平台、学术计算集群和云AI服务。 As mixed-precision training becomes the default in large-scale deep learning (especially with LLMs), cluster schedulers and cloud providers need accurate training time estimates to optimize resource utilization and reduce costs — current tools fail badly here. This work fills a critical gap by making training time prediction precision-aware, directly benefiting MLOps platforms, academic compute clusters, and cloud AI services.

This paper studies how many samples are needed to learn a near-optimal policy in the Stochastic Shortest Path (SSP) problem, a classic reinforcement learning setting where an agent aims to reach a goal with minimum cost. The authors derive both upper and lower bounds on sample complexity when the learner has access to a generative model, characterizing the dependence on key problem parameters such as the number of states, actions, and minimum cost. The results establish tight worst-case sample complexity bounds for SSP learning.

AEGIS: Anchor-Enforced Gradient Isolation for Knowledge-Preserving Vision-Language-Action Fine-Tuning
Robotics · 机器人Vision-Language-Action · 视觉-语言-动作模型Fine-Tuning · 微调
本文研究了将预训练视觉语言模型（VLM）适配到机器人控制任务时，动作专家产生的高幅度连续梯度与骨干网络原有交叉熵训练之间的跨模态梯度不对称问题。作者提出了AEGIS方法，通过锚点强制的梯度隔离机制，将动作专家的梯度与VLM骨干网络的梯度分离，防止微调过程中原有知识被破坏。实验结果表明，AEGIS在保留预训练语言和视觉知识的同时，有效提升了机器人控制任务的性能。
This paper addresses the challenge of adapting pre-trained vision-language models (VLMs) for robotic control, where high-magnitude gradients from a flow-matching action expert clash with the backbone's cross-entropy-based training, causing catastrophic forgetting. The authors propose AEGIS, a method that uses anchor-enforced gradient isolation to separate the action expert's gradients from those affecting the VLM backbone, preserving previously learned knowledge. Experiments show that AEGIS effectively maintains pre-trained visual and language capabilities while enabling strong robotic control performance.
🏁 结论：梯度隔离可有效防止机器人微调中的知识遗忘
Anchor-enforced gradient isolation preserves pre-trained VLM knowledge while successfully adapting it for robotic control tasks.
